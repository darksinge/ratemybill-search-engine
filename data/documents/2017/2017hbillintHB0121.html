7     LONG TITLE
8     General Description:
9          This bill creates the Local Food Advisory Council.
10     Highlighted Provisions:
11          This bill:
12          ▸     creates the Local Food Advisory Council;
13          ▸     designates the duties of the council; and
14          ▸     requires the council to periodically report to the Natural Resources, Agriculture, and
15     Environment Interim Committee and the Department of Agriculture and Food.

28          Section 1. Local Food Advisory Council Created.
29          (1) There is created the Local Food Advisory Council consisting of the following 15
30     members:
31          (a) one member of the Senate appointed by the president of the Senate;
32          (b) two members of the House of Representatives appointed by the speaker of the
33     House of Representatives, each from a different political party;
34          (c) the commissioner of the Department of Agriculture and Food, or the
35     commissioner's designee;
36          (d) the executive director of the Department of Health, or the executive director's
37     designee;
38          (e) three direct-to-consumer food producers;
39          (f) one member of a local agriculture organization;
40          (g) one food retailer;
41          (h) one licensed dietician;
42          (i) two county health department representatives;
43          (j) one community garden representative; and
44          (k) one representative of a business engaged in the processing, packaging, or
45     distribution of food, to be appointed by the governor.
46          (2) (a) The president of the Senate shall designate a member of the Senate appointed
47     under Subsection (1)(a) as a cochair of the commission.
48          (b) The speaker of the House of Representatives shall designate a member of the House
49     of Representatives appointed under Subsection (1)(b) as a cochair of the commission.
50          (3) In appointing members to the council under Subsections (1)(f) through (h), the
51     governor shall strive to take into account the geographical makeup of the council.
52          (4) A vacancy on the council resulting from the council shall be filled in the same
53     manner in which the original appointment was made.
54          (5) Compensation for a member of the council who is a legislator shall be paid in
55     accordance with Section 36-2-2 and Legislative Joint Rules, Title 5, Chapter 3, Legislator
56     Compensation.
57          (6) Council members who are employees of the state shall receive no additional
58     compensation.
59          (7) The Department of Agriculture and Food shall provide staff support for the council.
60          Section 2. Duties.
61          (1) The council shall study and make recommendations on:
62          (a) how to best promote vibrant, locally owned farms;
63          (b) how to best promote resilient ecosystems;
64          (c) how to best promote strong communities and healthy eating;
65          (d) how to best enhance thriving local food economies;
66          (e) how best to assess impacts of population growth and urbanization and the decline in
67     productive ranch and farmland;
68          (f) assessment of laws and regulations that deter or hinder the direct sales of locally
69     grown and produced food; and
70          (g) necessary steps to develop and implement a robust, integrated local food system.
71          (2) It is the purpose of the Local Food Advisory Council to contribute to:
72          (a) building a local food economy;
73          (b) benefitting the state by creating jobs;
74          (c) stimulating statewide economic development;
75          (d) circulating money from local food sales within local communities;
76          (e) preserving open space;
77          (f) fostering the viability of family-owned farms;
78          (g) preserving and protecting the natural environment;
79          (h) increasing consumer access to fresh and nutritious food; and
80          (i) providing greater food security for state residents.
81          Section 3. Duties -- Interim report.
82          (1) The council shall:
83          (a) convene at least four times each year; and
84          (b) review and make recommendations regarding the policy issues listed in Section 2.
85          (2) The council shall prepare an annual report and present the report before November
86     30, 2017, and every November thereafter to:
87          (a) the Natural Resources, Agriculture, and Environment Interim Committee;
88          (b) the Department of Agriculture and Food; and
89          (c) the Food Advisory Board.
