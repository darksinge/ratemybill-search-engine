7     LONG TITLE
8     General Description:
9          This bill modifies provisions related to state parks and the burial of ancient Native
10     American remains.
11     Highlighted Provisions:
12          This bill:
13          ▸     directs the Native American Remains Review Committee to advise the Board of
14     Parks and Recreation regarding reserving land in state parks for the burial of ancient
15     Native American remains;
16          ▸     requires the Board of Parks and Recreation to reserve land in certain state parks for
17     the burial of ancient Native American Remains;
18          ▸     requires the Board of Parks and Recreation to report to the Native American
19     Legislative Liaison Committee; and
20          ▸     makes technical changes.
21     Money Appropriated in this Bill:
22          None

24          None

31     Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah:
32          Section 1. Section 9-9-405 is amended to read:
33          9-9-405. Review committee.
34          (1) There is created a Native American Remains Review Committee.
35          (2) (a) The review committee [shall be] is composed of seven members as follows:
36          (i) four Tribal members shall be appointed by the director from nominations submitted
37     by the elected officials of Indian Tribal Nations described in Subsection 9-9-104.5(2)(b); and
38          (ii) three shall be appointed by the director from nominations submitted by
39     representatives of Utah's repositories.
40          (b) A member appointed under Subsection (2)(a)(i) shall have familiarity and
41     experience with this part.
42          (c) (i) A member appointed under Subsection (2)(a)(i) serves at the will of the director,
43     and if the member represents an Indian Tribal Nation, at the will of that Indian Tribal Nation.
44     Removal of a member who represents an Indian Tribal Nation requires the joint decision of the
45     director and the Indian Tribal Nation.
46          (ii) A member appointed under Subsection (2)(a)(ii) serves at the will of the director,
47     and if the member represents a repository, at the will of the Division of State History. Removal
48     of a member who represents a repository requires the joint decision of the director and the
49     Division of State History.
50          (d) When a vacancy occurs in the membership for any reason, the director shall appoint
51     a replacement in the same manner as the original appointment under Subsection (2)(a).
52          (e) A member may not receive compensation or benefits for the member's service, but
53     may receive per diem and travel expenses in accordance with:
54          (i) Section 63A-3-106;
55          (ii) Section 63A-3-107; and
56          (iii) rules made by the Division of Finance pursuant to Sections 63A-3-106 and
57     63A-3-107.
58          (f) The review committee shall designate one of its members as chair.
59          (3) The review committee shall:
60          (a) monitor the identification process conducted under Section 9-9-403 to ensure a fair
61     and objective consideration and assessment of all available relevant information and evidence;
62          (b) review a finding relating to the following, subject to the rules made by the division
63     under Subsection 9-9-403(6):
64          (i) the identity or cultural affiliation of Native American remains; or
65          (ii) the return of Native American remains;
66          (c) facilitate the resolution of a dispute among Indian Tribal Nations or lineal
67     descendants and state agencies relating to the return of Native American remains, including
68     convening the parties to the dispute if considered desirable;
69          (d) consult with Indian Tribal Nations on matters within the scope of the work of the
70     review committee affecting these Indian Tribal Nations;
71          (e) consult with the division in the development of rules to carry out this part;
72          (f) perform other related functions as the division may assign to the review committee;
73     [and]
74          (g) make recommendations, if appropriate, regarding care of Native American remains
75     that are to be repatriated[.]; and
76          (h) in accordance with Section 79-4-306, advise the Board of Parks and Recreation,
77     created in Section 79-4-301, regarding the Board of Parks and Recreation reserving land in
78     state parks for the burial of ancient Native American remains.
79          (4) A record or finding made by the review committee relating to the identity of or
80     cultural affiliation of Native American remains and the return of Native American remains may
81     be admissible in any action brought under this part.
82          (5) The appropriate state agency having primary authority over the lands as provided in
83     Chapter 8, Part 3, Antiquities, shall ensure that the review committee has reasonable access to:
84          (a) Native American remains under review; and
85          (b) associated scientific and historical documents.
86          (6) The division shall provide reasonable administrative and staff support necessary for
87     the deliberations of the review committee.
88          (7) The department shall include in the annual written report described in Section
89     9-1-208, a description of the progress made, and any barriers encountered, by the review
90     committee in implementing this section during the previous year.
91          Section 2. Section 79-4-306 is enacted to read:
92          79-4-306. Burial of ancient Native American remains in state parks.
93          (1) As used in this section, "ancient Native American remains" means ancient human
94     remains, as defined in Section 9-8-302, that are Native American remains, as defined in
95     Section 9-9-402.
96          (2) (a) By no later than July 1, 2018, the board shall reserve land for the burial of
97     ancient Native American remains in each state park for which the board finds:
98          (i) burial of ancient Native American remains is permissible under the purposes of the
99     state park, after working with federal agencies when necessary; and
100          (ii) the state park has land sufficient and appropriate to reserve a portion of the land for
101     the burial of ancient Native American remains.
102          (b) The Native American Remains Review Committee, created under Section 9-9-405,
103     shall advise the board regarding the appropriateness of land on state parks for the burial of
104     ancient Native American remains and the cultural requirements for the burial of ancient Native
105     American remains.
106          (3) The board shall report in writing, in accordance with Section 68-3-14, to the Native
107     American Legislative Liaison Committee by November 1, 2017, regarding the implementation
108     of this section.
