7     LONG TITLE
8     General Description:
9          This bill adds constables and deputy constables to the list of persons designated as a
10     law enforcement officer.
11     Highlighted Provisions:
12          This bill:
13          ▸     adds constables and deputy constables to the list of persons designated as law
14     enforcement officers.
15     Money Appropriated in this Bill:
16          None
17     Other Special Clauses:
18          None
19     Utah Code Sections Affected:
20     AMENDS:
21          53-13-103, as last amended by Laws of Utah 2015, Chapter 436
22     

23     Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah:
24          Section 1. Section 53-13-103 is amended to read:
25          53-13-103. Law enforcement officer.
26          (1) (a) "Law enforcement officer" means a sworn and certified peace officer who is an
27     employee of a law enforcement agency that is part of or administered by the state or any of its
28     political subdivisions, and whose primary and principal duties consist of the prevention and
29     detection of crime and the enforcement of criminal statutes or ordinances of this state or any of
30     its political subdivisions.
31          (b) "Law enforcement officer" includes the following:
32          (i) any sheriff or deputy sheriff, chief of police, police officer, constable or deputy
33     constable, or marshal of any county, city, or town;
34          (ii) the commissioner of public safety and any member of the Department of Public
35     Safety certified as a peace officer;
36          (iii) all persons specified in Sections 23-20-1.5 and 79-4-501;
37          (iv) any police officer employed by any college or university;
38          (v) investigators for the Motor Vehicle Enforcement Division;
39          (vi) investigators for the Department of Insurance, Fraud Division;
40          (vii) special agents or investigators employed by the attorney general, district attorneys,
41     and county attorneys;
42          (viii) employees of the Department of Natural Resources designated as peace officers
43     by law;
44          (ix) school district police officers as designated by the board of education for the
45     school district;
46          (x) the executive director of the Department of Corrections and any correctional
47     enforcement or investigative officer designated by the executive director and approved by the
48     commissioner of public safety and certified by the division;
49          (xi) correctional enforcement, investigative, or adult probation and parole officers
50     employed by the Department of Corrections serving on or before July 1, 1993;
51          (xii) members of a law enforcement agency established by a private college or
52     university provided that the college or university has been certified by the commissioner of
53     public safety according to rules of the Department of Public Safety;
54          (xiii) airport police officers of any airport owned or operated by the state or any of its
55     political subdivisions; and
56          (xiv) transit police officers designated under Section 17B-2a-822.
57          (2) Law enforcement officers may serve criminal process and arrest violators of any
58     law of this state and have the right to require aid in executing their lawful duties.
59          (3) (a) A law enforcement officer has statewide full-spectrum peace officer authority,
60     but the authority extends to other counties, cities, or towns only when the officer is acting
61     under Title 77, Chapter 9, Uniform Act on Fresh Pursuit, unless the law enforcement officer is
62     employed by the state.
63          (b) (i) A local law enforcement agency may limit the jurisdiction in which its law
64     enforcement officers may exercise their peace officer authority to a certain geographic area.
65          (ii) Notwithstanding Subsection (3)(b)(i), a law enforcement officer may exercise
66     authority outside of the limited geographic area, pursuant to Title 77, Chapter 9, Uniform Act
67     on Fresh Pursuit, if the officer is pursuing an offender for an offense that occurred within the
68     limited geographic area.
69          (c) The authority of law enforcement officers employed by the Department of
70     Corrections is regulated by Title 64, Chapter 13, Department of Corrections - State Prison.
71          (4) A law enforcement officer shall, prior to exercising peace officer authority:
72          (a) (i) have satisfactorily completed the requirements of Section 53-6-205; or
73          (ii) have met the waiver requirements in Section 53-6-206; and
74          (b) have satisfactorily completed annual certified training of at least 40 hours per year
75     as directed by the director of the division, with the advice and consent of the council.
