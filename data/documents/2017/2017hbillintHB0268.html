7     LONG TITLE
8     General Description:
9          This bill modifies the State Money Management Act by amending provisions relating to
10     money management.
11     Highlighted Provisions:
12          This bill:
13          ▸     amends definitions;
14          ▸     specifies the term to final maturity for certain deposits or investments that are
15     invested by a public agency insurance mutual;
16          ▸     authorizes the state treasurer, county, city, and town treasurers, the clerk or treasurer
17     of each school district, and other public treasurers to procure crime or theft
18     insurance;
19          ▸     modifies political party requirements for members of the State Money Management
20     Council;
21          ▸     provides that the State Money Management Council may authorize an exception to
22     certain maturity dates in certain circumstances; and
23          ▸     makes technical and conforming changes.
24     Money Appropriated in this Bill:
25          None
26     Other Special Clauses:
27          None

70     nationally recognized statistical rating organizations in the highest short-term rating category.

72     corpus is intended to be held in perpetuity by formal institutional designation according to the

81          (13) "Higher education institution" means the institutions specified in Section

97     amount of:
98          (a) deposits in excess of the federal deposit insurance limit; and
99          (b) nonqualifying repurchase agreements.
100          (20) "Money market mutual fund" means an open-end managed investment fund:

107     has been designated as a nationally recognized statistical rating organization by the Securities
108     and Exchange Commission's Division of Market Regulation.
109          (22) "Nonqualifying repurchase agreement" means a repurchase agreement evidencing
110     indebtedness of a qualified depository arising from the transfer of obligations of the United
111     States Treasury or other authorized investments to public treasurers that is:
112          (a) evidenced by a safekeeping receipt issued by the qualified depository;
113          (b) included in the depository's maximum amount of public funds; and
114          (c) valued and maintained at market value plus an appropriate margin collateral
115     requirement based upon the term of the agreement and the type of securities acquired.
116          (23) "Operating funds" means current balances and other funds that are to be disbursed
117     for operation of the state government or any of its boards, commissions, institutions,
118     departments, divisions, agencies, or other similar instrumentalities, or any county, city, school
119     district, political subdivision, or other public body.
120          (24) "Permanent funds" means funds whose principal may not be expended, the
121     earnings from which are to be used for purposes designated by law.
122          (25) "Permitted depository" means any out-of-state financial institution that meets
123     quality criteria established by rule of the council.
124          (26) "Public funds" means money, funds, and accounts, regardless of the source from
125     which the money, funds, and accounts are derived, that are owned, held, or administered by the
126     state or any of its boards, commissions, institutions, departments, divisions, agencies, bureaus,
127     laboratories, or other similar instrumentalities, or any county, city, school district, political
128     subdivision, or other public body.
129          (27) (a) "Public money" means "public funds."
130          (b) "Public money," as used in Article VII, Sec. 15, Utah Constitution, means the same
131     as "state funds."
132          (28) "Public treasurer" includes the state treasurer and the official of any state board,
133     commission, institution, department, division, agency, or other similar instrumentality, or of
134     any county, city, school district, charter school, political subdivision, or other public body who
135     has the responsibility for the safekeeping and investment of any public funds.
136          (29) "Qualified depository" means a Utah depository institution or an out-of-state
137     depository institution, as those terms are defined in Section 7-1-103, that is authorized to

139     Failing Depository Institutions or Holding Companies, whose deposits are insured by an

142     council to be eligible to receive deposits of public funds.
143          (30) "Qualifying repurchase agreement" means a repurchase agreement evidencing
144     indebtedness of a financial institution or government securities dealer acting as principal
145     arising from the transfer of obligations of the United States Treasury or other authorized
146     investments to public treasurers only if purchased securities are:
147          (a) delivered to the public treasurer's safekeeping agent or custodian as contemplated
148     by Section 7 of the Master Repurchase Agreement; and
149          (b) valued and maintained at market value plus an appropriate margin collateral
150     requirement based upon the term of the agreement and the type of securities acquired.
151          (31) "Reciprocal deposits" means deposits that are initially deposited into a qualified
152     depository and are then redeposited through a deposit account registry service:
153          (a) in one or more FDIC-insured depository institutions in amounts up to the relevant
154     FDIC-insured deposit limit for a depositor in each depository institution; and
155          (b) in exchange for reciprocal FDIC-insured deposits made through the deposit account
156     registry service to the qualified depository.
157          (32) "Securities division" means Utah's Division of Securities created within the
158     Department of Commerce by Section 13-1-2.
159          (33) "State funds" means:
160          (a) public money raised by operation of law for the support and operation of the state
161     government; and
162          (b) all other money, funds, and accounts, regardless of the source from which the
163     money, funds, or accounts are derived, that are owned, held, or administered by the state or any
164     of its boards, commissions, institutions, departments, divisions, agencies, bureaus, laboratories,
165     or other similar instrumentalities.

293          Section 3. Section 51-7-15 is amended to read:
294          51-7-15. Bonds of state treasurer and other public treasurers -- Reports to
295     council.
296          (1) (a) The state treasurer, county, city, and town treasurers, the clerk or treasurer of
297     each school district, and other public treasurers that the council designates by rule shall be
298     bonded or may procure crime or theft insurance as allowed in Section 17-16-11 in an amount
299     of not less than that established by the council.
300          (b) The council shall base the minimum bond amount or crime or theft insurance as
301     allowed in Section 17-16-11 on the amount of public funds normally in the treasurer's
302     possession or control.
303          (2) (a) When a public treasurer deposits or invests public funds as authorized by this
304     chapter, the public treasurer and the public treasurer's bondsmen or insurers are not liable for
305     any loss of public funds invested or deposited unless the loss is caused by the malfeasance of
306     the public treasurer or a member of the public treasurer's staff.
307          (b) A public treasurer and the public treasurer's bondsmen or insurers are liable for a
308     loss for any reason from deposits or investments not made in conformity with this chapter and
309     the rules of the council.
310          (3) (a) A public treasurer shall file a written report with the council on or before January
311     31 and July 31 of each year.
312          (b) The report shall contain:
313          (i) the information about the deposits and investments of that public treasurer during
314     the preceding six months ending December 31 and June 30, respectively, that the council
315     requires by rule; and
316          (ii) information detailing the nature and extent of interest rate contracts permitted by
317     Subsection 51-7-17(3).
318          (c) A public treasurer shall make copies of the report available to the public at the
319     public treasurer's office during normal business hours.
320          Section 4. Section 51-7-16 is amended to read:
321          51-7-16. State Money Management Council -- Members -- Terms -- Vacancies --
322     Chair and vice chair-- Executive secretary -- Meetings -- Quorum -- Members' disclosure
323     of interests -- Per diem and expenses.
324          (1) (a) There is created a State Money Management Council composed of five
325     members appointed by the governor after consultation with the state treasurer and with the
326     consent of the Senate.
327          (b) The members of the council shall be qualified by training and experience in the
328     field of investment or finance as follows:
329          (i) at least one member, but not more than two members, shall be experienced in the
330     banking business;
331          (ii) at least one member, but not more than two members, shall be an elected treasurer;
332          (iii) at least one member, but not more than two members, shall be an appointed public
333     treasurer; and
334          (iv) two members, but not more than two members, shall be experienced in the field of
335     investment.
336          [(c) No more than three members of the council may be from the same political party.]
337          (c) The members of the council shall be appointed without regard to the members'
338     political party affiliation.
339          (2) (a) Except as required by Subsection (2)(b), the council members shall be appointed
340     for terms of four years.
341          (b) Notwithstanding the requirements of Subsection (2)(a), the governor shall, at the
342     time of appointment or reappointment, adjust the length of terms to ensure that the terms of
343     council members are staggered so that approximately half of the council is appointed every two
344     years.
345          (c) When a vacancy occurs in the membership for any reason, the replacement shall be
346     appointed for the unexpired term.
347          (d) All members shall serve until their successors are appointed and qualified.
348          (3) (a) The council members shall elect a chair and vice chair.
349          (b) The state treasurer shall serve as executive secretary of the council without vote.
350          (4) (a) The council shall meet at least once per quarter at a regular date to be fixed by
351     the council and at other times at the call of the chair, the state treasurer, or any two members of
352     the council.
353          (b) Three members are a quorum for the transaction of business.
354          (c) Actions of the council require a vote of a majority of those present.
355          (d) All meetings of the council and records of its proceedings are open for inspection
356     by the public at the state treasurer's office during regular business hours except for:
357          (i) reports of the commissioner of financial institutions concerning the identity,
358     liquidity, or financial condition of qualified depositories and the amount of public funds each is
359     eligible to hold; and
360          (ii) reports of the director concerning the identity, liquidity, or financial condition of
361     certified dealers.
362          (5) (a) Each member of the council shall file a sworn or written statement with the
363     lieutenant governor that discloses any position or employment or ownership interest that he has
364     in any financial institution or investment organization.
365          (b) Each member shall file the statement required by this Subsection (5) when he
366     becomes a member of the council and when substantial changes in his position, employment,
367     or ownership interests occur.
368          (6) A member may not receive compensation or benefits for the member's service, but
369     may receive per diem and travel expenses in accordance with:
370          (a) Section 63A-3-106;
371          (b) Section 63A-3-107; and
372          (c) rules made by the Division of Finance pursuant to Sections 63A-3-106 and
373     63A-3-107.
374          Section 5. Section 51-7-23 is amended to read:
375          51-7-23. Transition of investments previously authorized.
376          (1) Any investment held by a public treasurer that as of June 30, 2015, is not in
377     compliance with the provisions of this chapter is subject to review by the council.
378          (2) (a) No later than July 31, 2015, a public treasurer who holds an investment
379     described in Subsection (1) shall provide the council a written report that outlines a reasonable
380     plan to bring the investment into compliance.
381          (b) A plan described in Subsection (2)(a) is subject to annual review by the council.
382          (c) The council may authorize, with substantial justification, an exception to the
383     five-year maturity requirements of Section 51-7-11.
