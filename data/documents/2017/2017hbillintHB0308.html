7     LONG TITLE
8     General Description:
9          This bill requires the Department of Health to create an online education module
10     regarding certain preventable diseases.
11     Highlighted Provisions:
12          This bill:
13          ▸     requires the Department of Health to create an online education module for parents
14     of children who are not immune to vaccine-preventable diseases.
15     Money Appropriated in this Bill:
16          None
17     Other Special Clauses:
18          None
19     Utah Code Sections Affected:
20     ENACTS:
21          26-7-9, Utah Code Annotated 1953
22     

23     Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah:
24          Section 1. Section 26-7-9 is enacted to read:
25          26-7-9. Online public health education module.
26          (1) As used in this section:
27          (a) "Health care provider" means the same as that term is defined in Section
28     78B-3-403.
29          (b) "Nonimmune" means that a child or an individual:
30          (i) has not received each vaccine required in Section 53A-11-303 and has not
31     developed a natural immunity through previous illness to a vaccine-preventable disease, as
32     documented by a health care provider;
33          (ii) cannot receive each vaccine required in Section 53A-11-303; or
34          (iii) is otherwise known to not be immune to a vaccine-preventable disease.
35          (c) "Vaccine-preventable disease" means an infectious disease that can be prevented by
36     a vaccination required in Section 53A-11-303.
37          (2) The department shall develop an online education module regarding
38     vaccine-preventable diseases:
39          (a) to assist a parent of a nonimmune child to:
40          (i) recognize the symptoms of vaccine-preventable diseases;
41          (ii) respond in the case of an outbreak of a vaccine-preventable disease;
42          (iii) protect children who contract a vaccine-preventable disease; and
43          (iv) prevent the spread of vaccine-preventable diseases;
44          (b) that contains only the following:
45          (i) information about vaccine-preventable diseases necessary to achieve the goals
46     stated in Subsection (2)(a), including the best practices to prevent the spread of
47     vaccine-preventable diseases;
48          (ii) recommendations to reduce the likelihood of a nonimmune individual contracting
49     or transmitting a vaccine-preventable disease; and
50          (iii) information about additional available resources related to vaccine-preventable
51     diseases and the availability of low-cost vaccines;
52          (c) that includes interactive questions or activities; and
53          (d) that is expected to take an average user 20 minutes or less to complete, based on
54     user testing.
55          (3) In developing the online education module described in Subsection (2), the
56     department shall consult with individuals interested in vaccination or vaccine-preventable
57     diseases, including:
58          (a) representatives from organizations of health care professionals; and
59          (b) parents of nonimmune children.
60          (4) The department shall make the online education module described in Subsection
61     (2) publicly available to parents through:
62          (a) a link on the department's website;
63          (b) county health departments, as that term is defined in Section 26A-1-102;
64          (c) local health departments, as that term is defined in Section 26A-1-102;
65          (d) local education agencies, as that term is defined in Section 53A-1-401; and
66          (e) other public health programs or organizations.
67          (5) The department shall report to the Health and Human Services Interim Committee
68     before November 30, 2017, regarding compliance with this section.
