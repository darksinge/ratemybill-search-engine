7     LONG TITLE
8     General Description:
9          This bill amends provisions related to teacher salary supplements.
10     Highlighted Provisions:
11          This bill:
12          ▸     defines terms;
13          ▸     amends provisions related to an individual's eligibility for a teacher salary
14     supplement; and
15          ▸     makes technical and conforming changes.
16     Money Appropriated in this Bill:
17          None
18     Other Special Clauses:
19          None
20     Utah Code Sections Affected:
21     AMENDS:
22          53A-17a-156, as last amended by Laws of Utah 2016, Chapter 217
23     

24     Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah:
25          Section 1. Section 53A-17a-156 is amended to read:
26          53A-17a-156. Teacher Salary Supplement Program -- Appeal process.
27          (1) As used in this section:
28          (a) "Board" means the State Board of Education.
29          (b) "Certificate teacher" means a teacher who holds a National Board certification.
30          (c) "Eligible teacher" means a teacher who:
31          (i) has an assignment to teach:
32          (A) a secondary school level mathematics course;
33          (B) integrated science in grade [seven or eight] 8;
34          (C) chemistry;
35          (D) physics; or
36          (E) computer science;
37          (ii) holds the appropriate endorsement for the assigned course;
38          (iii) has qualifying educational background; and
39          (iv) (A) is a new employee; or
40          (B) received a satisfactory rating or above on the teacher's most recent evaluation.
41          (d) "Endorsement" means a stipulation, authorized by the board and appended to a
42     license, that specifies an area of practice to which the license applies.
43          (e) "Field of computer science" means:
44          (i) computer science; or
45          (ii) computer information technology.
46          (f) "Field of science" means:
47          (i) integrated science;
48          (ii) chemistry;
49          (iii) physics;
50          (iv) physical science; or
51          (v) general science.
52          (g) "License" means the same as that terms is defined in Section 53A-6-103.
53          [(d)] (h) "National Board certification" means the same as that term is defined in
54     Section 53A-6-103.
55          [(e)] (i) "Qualifying educational background" means:
56          (i) for a teacher who is assigned a secondary school level mathematics course:
57          (A) a bachelor's degree major, master's degree, or doctoral degree in mathematics; [or]
58          (B) a bachelor's degree major, master's degree, or doctoral degree that has course
59     requirements that are substantially equivalent to the course requirements for a bachelor's degree
60     major, master's degree, or doctoral degree in mathematics; or
61          (C) a license with a mathematics level four endorsement, as established by the board;
62          (ii) for a teacher who is assigned a grade [seven or eight] 8 integrated science course,
63     chemistry course, or physics course[,]:
64          (A) a bachelor's degree major, master's degree, or doctoral degree in[:] a field science;
65          [(A) integrated science;]
66          [(B) chemistry;]
67          [(C) physics;]
68          [(D) physical science;]
69          [(E) general science; or]
70          [(F)] (B) a bachelor's degree major, master's degree, or doctoral degree that has course
71     requirements that are substantially equivalent to the course requirements of those required for a
72     degree [listed in Subsections (1)(e)(ii)(A) through (E);] described in Subsection (1)(i)(ii)(A); or
73          (C) a license with a chemistry or physics endorsement, as established by the board; or
74          (iii) for a teacher who is assigned a computer science course[,]:
75          (A) a bachelor's degree major, master's degree, or doctoral degree in[:] a field of
76     computer science;
77          [(A) computer science;]
78          [(B) computer information technology; or]
79          [(C)] (B) a bachelor's degree major, master's degree, or doctoral degree that has course
80     requirements that are substantially equivalent to the course requirements of those required for a
81     degree [listed in Subsections (1)(e)(iii)(A) and (B).] described in Subsection (1)(i)(iii)(A); or
82          (C) a license with a computer science level two endorsement, as established by the
83     board.
84          [(f)] (j) "Title I school" means a school that receives funds under the Elementary and
85     Secondary Education Act of 1965, Title I, 20 U.S.C. Sec. 6301 et seq.
86          [(g)] (k) "Title I school certificate teacher" means a certificate teacher who is assigned
87     to teach at a Title I school.
88          (2) (a) Subject to future budget constraints, the Legislature shall annually appropriate
89     money to the Teacher Salary Supplement Restricted Account established in Section
90     53A-17a-157 to fund the Teacher Salary Supplement Program.
91          (b) Money appropriated for the Teacher Salary Supplement Program shall include
92     money for the following employer-paid benefits:
93          (i) retirement;
94          (ii) workers' compensation;
95          (iii) social security; and
96          (iv) Medicare.
97          (3) (a) (i) The annual salary supplement for an eligible teacher who is assigned full
98     time to teach one or more courses listed in Subsections (1)(c)(i)(A) through (E) is $4,100.
99          (ii) An eligible teacher who has a part-time assignment to teach one or more courses
100     listed in Subsections (1)(c)(i)(A) through (E) shall receive a partial salary supplement based on
101     the number of hours worked in [a] the course assignment [that meets the requirements of
102     Subsections (1)(c)(ii) and (iii)].
103          (b) The annual salary supplement for a certificate teacher is $750.
104          (c) (i) The annual salary supplement for a Title I school certificate teacher is $1,500.
105          (ii) A certificate teacher who qualifies for a salary supplement under Subsections (3)(b)
106     and (c) may only receive the salary supplement that is greater in value.
107          (4) The board shall:
108          (a) create an online application system for a teacher to apply to receive a salary
109     supplement through the Teacher Salary Supplement Program;
110          (b) determine if a teacher:
111          (i) (A) is an eligible teacher; and
112          (B) has a course assignment as listed in Subsections (1)(c)(i)(A) through (E);
113          (ii) is a certificate teacher; or
114          (iii) is a Title I school certificate teacher;
115          (c) verify, as needed, the determinations made under Subsection (4)(b) with school
116     district and school administrators; and
117          (d) certify a list of eligible teachers, certificate teachers, and Title I school certificate
118     teachers.
119          (5) (a) An eligible teacher, a certificate teacher, or a Title I school certificate teacher
120     shall apply with the board before the conclusion of a school year to receive the salary
121     supplement authorized in this section.
122          (b) An eligible teacher, a certificate teacher, or a Title I school certificate teacher may
123     apply with the board, after verification that the requirements under this section have been
124     satisfied, to receive a salary supplement after the completion of:
125          (i) the school year as an annual award; or
126          (ii) a semester or trimester as a partial award based on the portion of the school year
127     that has been completed.
128          (6) (a) The board shall establish and administer an appeal process for a teacher to
129     follow if the teacher applies for [the] a salary supplement and [is not certified under Subsection
130     (4)] does not receive a salary supplement under Subsection (8).
131          (b) (i) The appeal process established in Subsection (6)(a) shall allow a teacher to
132     appeal eligibility as an eligible teacher on the basis that the teacher has a degree or degree
133     major with course requirements that are substantially equivalent to the course requirements for
134     a degree [listed] described in:
135          (A) Subsection (1)[(e)](i)(i)(A);
136          (B) [Subsections (1)(e)(ii)(A) through (E)] Subsection (1)(i)(ii)(A); or
137          (C) [Subsections (1)(e)(iii)(A) and (B)] Subsection (1)(i)(iii)(A).
138          (ii) A teacher shall provide transcripts and other documentation to the board in order
139     for the board to determine if the teacher has a degree or degree major with course requirements
140     that are substantially equivalent to the course requirements for a degree [listed] described in:
141          (A) Subsection (1)[(e)](i)(i)(A);
142          (B) [Subsections (1)(e)(ii)(A) through (E)] Subsection (1)(i)(ii)(A); or
143          (C) [Subsections (1)(e)(iii)(A) and (B)] Subsection (1)(i)(iii)(A).
144          (c) (i) The appeal process established under Subsection (6)(a) shall allow a teacher to
145     appeal eligibility as a certificate teacher on the basis that the teacher holds a current certificate.
146          (ii) A teacher shall provide to the board a certificate or other related documentation in
147     order for the board to determine if the teacher holds a current certificate.
148          (d) (i) The appeal process established under Subsection (6)(a) shall allow a teacher to
149     appeal eligibility as a Title I school certificate teacher on the basis that the teacher:
150          (A) holds a current certificate; and
151          (B) is assigned to teach at a Title I school.
152          (ii) A teacher shall provide to the board:
153          (A) information described in Subsection (6)(c)(ii); and
154          (B) verification that the teacher is assigned to teach at a Title I school.
155          (7) (a) The board shall distribute money from the Teacher Salary Supplement
156     Restricted Account to school districts and charter schools for the Teacher Salary Supplement
157     Program in accordance with the provisions of this section.
158          (b) The board shall include the employer-paid benefits described under Subsection
159     (2)(b) in the amount of each salary supplement.
160          (c) The employer-paid benefits described under Subsection (2)(b) are an addition to the
161     salary supplement limits described under Subsection (3).
162          (8) (a) Money received from the Teacher Salary Supplement Restricted Account shall
163     be used by a school district or charter school to provide a salary supplement equal to the
164     amount specified in Subsection (3) for each eligible teacher, certificate teacher, or Title I
165     school certificate teacher.
166          (b) The salary supplement is part of the teacher's base pay, subject to the teacher's
167     qualification as an eligible teacher, a certificate teacher, or a Title I school certificate teacher
168     every year, semester, or trimester.
169          (9) Notwithstanding the provisions of this section, if the appropriation for the program
170     is insufficient to cover the costs associated with salary supplements, the board [may limit or
171     reduce the salary supplements] shall distribute the funds in the Teacher Salary Supplement
172     Restricted Account on a pro rata basis.
