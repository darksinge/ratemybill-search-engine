7     LONG TITLE
8     General Description:
9          This bill addresses sibling visitation for children in the custody of the Division of Child
10     and Family Services.
11     Highlighted Provisions:
12          This bill:
13          ▸     defines the term "sibling";
14          ▸     requires the division to make reasonable efforts for sibling visitation when siblings
15     are separated due to foster care or adoptive placement;
16          ▸     allows the court to order sibling visitation when the visitation is in the best interest
17     of the child; and
18          ▸     makes technical changes.
19     Money Appropriated in this Bill:
20          None
21     Other Special Clauses:
22          None

178          Section 3. Section 62A-4a-205 is amended to read:
179          62A-4a-205. Child and family plan -- Parent-time and relative visitation.
180          (1) No more than 45 days after a child enters the temporary custody of the division, the
181     child's child and family plan shall be finalized.
182          (2) (a) The division may use an interdisciplinary team approach in developing each
183     child and family plan.
184          (b) The interdisciplinary team described in Subsection (2)(a) may include
185     representatives from the following fields:
186          (i) mental health;
187          (ii) education; and
188          (iii) if appropriate, law enforcement.
189          (3) (a) The division shall involve all of the following in the development of a child's
190     child and family plan:
191          (i) both of the child's natural parents, unless the whereabouts of a parent are unknown;
192          (ii) the child;
193          (iii) the child's foster parents;
194          (iv) if appropriate, the child's stepparent; and
195          (v) the child's guardian ad litem, if one has been appointed by the court.
196          (b) In relation to all information considered by the division in developing a child and
197     family plan, additional weight and attention shall be given to the input of the child's natural and
198     foster parents upon their involvement pursuant to Subsections (3)(a)(i) and (iii).
199          (c) (i) The division shall make a substantial effort to develop a child and family plan
200     with which the child's parents agree.
201          (ii) If a parent does not agree with a child and family plan:
202          (A) the division shall strive to resolve the disagreement between the division and the
203     parent; and
204          (B) if the disagreement is not resolved, the division shall inform the court of the
205     disagreement.
206          (4) A copy of the child and family plan shall, immediately upon completion, or as soon
207     as reasonably possible thereafter, be provided to the:
208          (a) guardian ad litem;
209          (b) child's natural parents; and
210          (c) child's foster parents.
211          (5) Each child and family plan shall:
212          (a) specifically provide for the safety of the child, in accordance with federal law; and
213          (b) clearly define what actions or precautions will, or may be, necessary to provide for
214     the health, safety, protection, and welfare of the child.
215          (6) The child and family plan shall set forth, with specificity, at least the following:
216          (a) the reason the child entered into the custody of the division;
217          (b) documentation of the:
218          (i) reasonable efforts made to prevent placement of the child in the custody of the
219     division; or
220          (ii) emergency situation that existed and that prevented the reasonable efforts described
221     in Subsection (6)(b)(i), from being made;
222          (c) the primary permanency plan for the child and the reason for selection of that plan;
223          (d) the concurrent permanency plan for the child and the reason for the selection of that
224     plan;
225          (e) if the plan is for the child to return to the child's family:
226          (i) specifically what the parents must do in order to enable the child to be returned
227     home;
228          (ii) specifically how the requirements described in Subsection (6)(e)(i) may be
229     accomplished; and
230          (iii) how the requirements described in Subsection (6)(e)(i) will be measured;
231          (f) the specific services needed to reduce the problems that necessitated placing the
232     child in the division's custody;
233          (g) the name of the person who will provide for and be responsible for case
234     management;
235          (h) subject to Subsection (10), a parent-time schedule between the natural parent and
236     the child;
237          (i) subject to Subsection (7), the health and mental health care to be provided to
238     address any known or diagnosed mental health needs of the child;
239          (j) if residential treatment rather than a foster home is the proposed placement, a
240     requirement for a specialized assessment of the child's health needs including an assessment of
241     mental illness and behavior and conduct disorders; [and]
242          (k) social summaries that include case history information pertinent to case planning[.];
243     and
244          (l) subject to Subsection (12), a sibling visitation schedule.
245          (7) (a) Subject to Subsection (7)(b), in addition to the information required under
246     Subsection (6)(i), the plan shall include a specialized assessment of the medical and mental
247     health needs of a child, if the child:
248          (i) is placed in residential treatment; and
249          (ii) has medical or mental health issues that need to be addressed.
250          (b) Notwithstanding Subsection (7)(a), a parent shall retain the right to seek a separate
251     medical or mental health diagnosis of the parent's child from a licensed practitioner of the
252     parent's choice.
253          (8) (a) Each child and family plan shall be specific to each child and the child's family,
254     rather than general.
255          (b) The division shall train its workers to develop child and family plans that comply
256     with:
257          (i) federal mandates; and
258          (ii) the specific needs of the particular child and the child's family.
259          (c) All child and family plans and expectations shall be individualized and contain
260     specific time frames.
261          (d) Subject to Subsection (8)(h), child and family plans shall address problems that:
262          (i) keep a child in placement; and
263          (ii) keep a child from achieving permanence in the child's life.
264          (e) Each child and family plan shall be designed to minimize disruption to the normal
265     activities of the child's family, including employment and school.
266          (f) In particular, the time, place, and amount of services, hearings, and other
267     requirements ordered by the court in the child and family plan shall be designed, as much as
268     practicable, to help the child's parents maintain or obtain employment.
269          (g) The child's natural parents, foster parents, and where appropriate, stepparents, shall
270     be kept informed of and supported to participate in important meetings and procedures related
271     to the child's placement.
272          (h) For purposes of Subsection (8)(d), a child and family plan may only include
273     requirements that:
274          (i) address findings made by the court; or
275          (ii) (A) are requested or consented to by a parent or guardian of the child; and
276          (B) are agreed to by the division and the guardian ad litem.
277          (9) (a) Except as provided in Subsection (9)(b), with regard to a child who is three
278     years of age or younger, if the plan is not to return the child home, the primary permanency
279     plan for that child shall be adoption.
280          (b) Notwithstanding Subsection (9)(a), if the division documents to the court that there
281     is a compelling reason that adoption, reunification, guardianship, and a placement described in
282     Subsection 78A-6-306(6)(e) are not in the child's best interest, the court may order another
283     planned permanent living arrangement in accordance with federal law.
284          (10) (a) Except as provided in Subsection (10)(b), parent-time may only be denied by a
285     court order issued pursuant to Subsections 78A-6-312(3), (6), and (7).
286          (b) Notwithstanding Subsection (10)(a), the person designated by the division or a
287     court to supervise a parent-time session may deny parent-time for that session if the supervising
288     person determines that, based on the parent's condition, it is necessary to deny parent-time in
289     order to:
290          (i) protect the physical safety of the child;
291          (ii) protect the life of the child; or
292          (iii) consistent with Subsection (10)(c), prevent the child from being traumatized by
293     contact with the parent.
294          (c) In determining whether the condition of the parent described in Subsection (10)(b)
295     will traumatize a child, the person supervising the parent-time session shall consider the impact
296     that the parent's condition will have on the child in light of:
297          (i) the child's fear of the parent; and
298          (ii) the nature of the alleged abuse or neglect.
299          (11) The division shall consider visitation with their grandparents for children in state
300     custody if the division determines visitation to be in the best interest of the child and:
301          (a) there are no safety concerns regarding the behavior or criminal background of the
302     grandparents;
303          (b) allowing visitation would not compete with or undermine the reunification plan;
304          (c) there is a substantial relationship between the grandparents and children; and
305          (d) the visitation will not unduly burden the foster parents.
306          (12) The child and family plan shall incorporate all reasonable efforts for sibling
307     visitation when:
308          (a) siblings are separated due to foster care or adoptive placement; and
309          (b) visitation is in the best interest of the child for whom the plan is developed.

347     abuse, or severe neglect are involved, neither the division nor the court has any duty to make
348     "reasonable efforts" or to, in any other way, attempt to provide reunification services, or to

351     concern in determining whether reasonable efforts to reunify should be made.
352          (6) For purposes of Subsection (3), parent-time is in the best interests of a minor unless
353     the court makes a finding that it is necessary to deny parent-time in order to:
354          (a) protect the physical safety of the minor;
355          (b) protect the life of the minor; or

366          (ii) an explanation of the effect of abandoning or modifying the primary permanency

368          (b) In determining the primary permanency plan and concurrent permanency plan, the

370          (i) the preference for kinship placement over nonkinship placement;
