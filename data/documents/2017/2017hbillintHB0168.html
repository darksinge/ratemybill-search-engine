7     LONG TITLE
8     General Description:
9          This bill establishes the kindergarten supplemental enrichment program.
10     Highlighted Provisions:
11          This bill:
12          ▸     defines terms;
13          ▸     establishes the kindergarten supplemental enrichment program;
14          ▸     requires the State Board of Education to:
15               •     develop kindergarten entry and exist assessments for use in a kindergarten
16     supplemental enrichment program; and
17               •     administer a grant program to support certain kindergarten supplemental
18     enrichment programs; and
19          ▸     gives rulemaking authority.
20     Money Appropriated in this Bill:
21          None

23          None

30     Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah:
31          Section 1. Section 53A-15-2001 is enacted to read:
32     
Part 20. Kindergarten Supplemental Enrichment Program

33          53A-15-2001. Title.
34          This part is known as "Kindergarten Supplemental Enrichment Program."
35          Section 2. Section 53A-15-2002 is enacted to read:
36          53A-15-2002. Definitions.
37          As used in this part:
38          (1) "Board" means the State Board of Education.
39          (2) "Kindergarten supplemental enrichment program" means a program to improve the
40     academic competency of kindergarten students that:
41          (a) meets the criteria described in Section 53A-15-2003;
42          (b) receives funding from a grant program described in Section 53A-15-2003; and
43          (c) is administered by a public school.
44          Section 3. Section 53A-15-2003 is enacted to read:
45          53A-15-2003. Grant funding for kindergarten supplemental enrichment
46     programs.
47          (1) (a) In accordance with this section, the board shall distribute funds appropriated
48     under this section to support kindergarten supplemental enrichment programs.
49          (b) The board may use up to 1% of the funds described in Subsection (1)(a) for the
50     administrative cost of running the grant programs described in Subsection (2).
51          (c) The board shall develop kindergarten entry and exit assessments for use by a
52     kindergarten supplemental enrichment program.
53          (2) The board shall administer a qualifying grant program as described in this
54     Subsection (2) to distribute funds described in Subsection (1)(a) to a public school:
55          (a) that applies for a grant;
56          (b) that offers a kindergarten supplemental enrichment program that meets the
57     requirements described in Subsection (3);
58          (c) where 50% of students were eligible to receive free or reduced lunch in the prior
59     school year;
60          (d) that has an overall need for a kindergarten supplemental enrichment program, based
61     on the results of the public school's kindergarten entry and exit assessments described in
62     Subsection (3)(b)(ii);
63          (e) if the school has previously established a kindergarten supplemental enrichment
64     program under this section, that shows success of the public school's kindergarten
65     supplemental enrichment program, based on the results of the public school's kindergarten
66     entry and exit assessments described in Subsection (3)(b)(ii); and
67          (f) that proposes a kindergarten supplemental enrichment program that addresses the
68     particular needs of students at risk of experiencing intergenerational poverty, as defined in
69     Section 35A-9-102, using eligibility for free or reduced lunch as a primary indicator.
70          (3) A public school that receives a grant as described in Subsection (2) shall:
71          (a) use the grant money to offer a kindergarten supplemental enrichment program that:
72          (i) target kindergarten students at risk for not meeting grade 3 core standards for Utah
73     public schools, established by the board under Section 53A-1-402.6, by the end of each
74     student's grade 3 year;
75          (ii) use an evidence-based early intervention model;
76          (iii) focus on academically improving age-appropriate literacy and numeracy skills;
77          (iv) emphasize the use of live instruction;
78          (v) administer the kindergarten entry and exit assessments described in Subsection
79     (1)(c); and
80          (vi) deliver the kindergarten supplemental enrichment program through additional
81     hours or other means; and
82          (b) report to the board annually regarding:
83          (i) how the public school used grant money received under Subsection (2);
84          (ii) the results of the public school's kindergarten entry and exit assessments for the
85     prior year;
86          (iii) with assistance from board employees, the number of students served, including
87     the number of students who are eligible for free or reduced lunch; and
88          (iv) with assistance from board employees, student performance outcomes achieved by
89     the public school's kindergarten supplemental enrichment program, disaggregated by economic
90     and ethnic subgroups.
91          (4) A public school that receives a grant as described in Subsection (2) may not receive
92     funds appropriated under Section 53A-17a-167.
93          (5) A parent or legal guardian may decline participation of the parent or legal
94     guardian's kindergarten student in a public school's kindergarten supplemental enrichment
95     program.
96          (6) In accordance with Title 63G, Chapter 3, Utah Administrative Rulemaking Act, the
97     board shall make rules to establish reporting procedures and administer this section.
