8     LONG TITLE
9     General Description:
10          This bill creates a restricted account within the General Fund.
11     Highlighted Provisions:
12          This bill:
13          ▸     creates a restricted account known as the Public Utility Regulatory Restricted
14     Account in the Department of Commerce;
15          ▸     provides that the Department of Commerce shall deposit special regulation fees into
16     the Public Utility Regulatory Restricted Account;
17          ▸     provides that funds in the Public Utility Regulatory Restricted Account may be used
18     to fund certain entities within the Department of Commerce;
19          ▸     designates funds in the Public Utility Regulatory Restricted Account as nonlapsing;
20     and
21          ▸     transfers public utility regulatory fees designated as nonlapsing into the Public
22     Utility Regulatory Restricted Account.
23     Money Appropriated in this Bill:
24          None
25     Other Special Clauses:
26          This bill provides a special effective date.

34     Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah:
35          Section 1. Section 54-5-1.5 is amended to read:
36          54-5-1.5. Special regulation fee -- Supplemental Levy Committee -- Supplemental
37     fee -- Fee for electrical cooperatives.
38          (1) (a) A special fee to defray the cost of regulation is imposed upon all public utilities
39     subject to the jurisdiction of the Public Service Commission.
40          (b) The special fee is in addition to any charge now assessed, levied, or required by
41     law.
42          (2) (a) The executive director of the Department of Commerce shall determine the
43     special fee for the Department of Commerce.
44          (b) The chair of the Public Service Commission shall determine the special fee for the
45     Public Service Commission.
46          (c) The fee shall be assessed as a uniform percentage of the gross operating revenue for
47     the preceding calendar year derived from each public utility's business and operations during
48     that period within this state, excluding income derived from interstate business. Gross
49     operating revenue shall not include income to a wholesale electric cooperative derived from the
50     sale of power to a rural electric cooperative which resells that power within the state.
51          (3) (a) The executive director of the Department of Commerce shall notify each public
52     utility subject to the provisions of this chapter of the amount of the fee.
53          (b) The fee is due and payable on or before July 1 of each year.
54          (4) (a) There is created a restricted account within the General Fund known as the
55     Public Utility Regulatory Restricted Account.
56          (b) Notwithstanding Subsection 13-1-2(3)(c), the Department of Commerce shall
57     deposit a fee assessed under this section into the Public Utility Regulatory Restricted Account.
58          (c) Within appropriations by the Legislature:
59          (i) the Department of Commerce may use the funds in the Public Utility Regulatory
60     Restricted Account to administer:
61          (A) the Division of Public Utilities; and
62          (B) the Office of Consumer Services; and
63          (ii) the Public Service Commission may use the funds in the Public Utility Regulatory
64     Restricted Account to administer the Public Service Commission.
65          [(4)] (5) (a) [It is the intent of the] The Legislature intends that the public utilities
66     provide all of the funds for the administration, support, and maintenance of:
67          (i) the Public Service Commission;
68          (ii) state agencies within the Department of Commerce involved in the regulation of
69     public utilities; and
70          (iii) expenditures by the attorney general for utility regulation.
71          (b) Notwithstanding Subsection [(4)] (5)(a), the fee imposed by Subsection (1) shall
72     not exceed the greater of:
73          (i) (A) for a public utility other than an electrical cooperative, .3% of the public utility's
74     gross operating revenues for the preceding calendar year; or
75          (B) for an electrical cooperative, .15% of the electrical cooperative's gross operating
76     revenues for the preceding calendar year; or
77          (ii) $50.
78          [(5)] (6) (a) There is created a Supplemental Levy Committee to levy additional
79     assessments on public utilities when unanticipated costs of regulation occur in any fiscal year.
80          (b) The Supplemental Levy Committee shall consist of:
81          (i) one member selected by the executive director of the Department of Commerce;
82          (ii) one member selected by the chairman of the Public Service Commission;
83          (iii) two members selected by the three public utilities that paid the largest percent of
84     the current regulatory fee; and
85          (iv) one member selected by the four appointed members.
86          (c) (i) The members of the Supplemental Levy Committee shall be selected within 10
87     working days after the executive director of the Department of Commerce gives written notice
88     to the Public Service Commission and the public utilities that a supplemental levy committee is
89     needed.
90          (ii) If the members of the Supplemental Levy Committee have not been appointed
91     within the time prescribed, the governor shall appoint the members of the Supplemental Levy
92     Committee.
93          (d) (i) During any state fiscal year, the Supplemental Levy Committee, by a majority
94     vote and subject to audit by the state auditor, may impose a supplemental fee on the regulated
95     utilities for the purpose of defraying any increased cost of regulation.
96          (ii) The supplemental fee imposed upon the utilities shall equal a percentage of their
97     gross operating revenue for the preceding calendar year.
98          (iii) The aggregate of all fees, including any supplemental fees assessed, shall not
99     exceed .3% of the gross operating revenue of the utilities assessed for the preceding calendar
100     year.
101          (iv) Payment of the supplemental fee is due within 30 days after receipt of the
102     assessment.
103          (v) The utility may, within 10 days after receipt of assessment, request a hearing before
104     the Public Service Commission if it questions the need for, or the reasonableness of, the
105     supplemental fee.
106          (e) (i) Any supplemental fee collected to defray the cost of regulation shall be
107     transferred to the state treasurer as a departmental collection according to the provisions of
108     Section 63J-1-104.
109          (ii) Supplemental fees are excess collections, credited according to the procedures of
110     Section 63J-1-104.
111          (iii) Charges billed to the Department of Commerce by any other state department,
112     institution, or agency for services rendered in connection with regulation of a utility shall be
113     credited by the state treasurer from the special or supplemental fees collected to the
114     appropriations account of the entity providing that service according to the procedures provided
115     in Title 63J, Chapter 1, Budgetary Procedures Act.
116          [(6)] (7) (a) For purposes of this section, "electrical cooperative" means:
117          (i) a distribution electrical cooperative; or
118          (ii) a wholesale electrical cooperative.
119          (b) Subject to Subsection [(6)] (7)(c), if the regulation of one or more electrical
120     cooperatives causes unanticipated costs of regulation in a fiscal year, the commission may
121     impose a supplemental fee on the one or more electrical cooperatives in this state responsible
122     for the increased cost of regulation.
123          (c) The aggregate of all fees imposed under this section on an electrical cooperative in
124     a calendar year shall not exceed the greater of:
125          (i) .3% of the electrical cooperative's gross operating revenues for the preceding
126     calendar year; or
127          (ii) $50.
