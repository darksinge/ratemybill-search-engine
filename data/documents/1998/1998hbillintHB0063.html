H.B. 63

1    

REGULATION OF MOTORIZED SCOOTERS

2    
1998 GENERAL SESSION

3    
STATE OF UTAH

4    
Sponsor: Lowell A. Nelson

5    AN ACT RELATING TO MOTOR VEHICLES; PROVIDING CERTAIN EXCEPTIONS FROM
6    REGISTRATION; PROVIDING DEFINITIONS; PROVIDING RESTRICTIONS;
7    PROVIDING AN EXCEPTION FROM INSURANCE REQUIREMENTS.
8    This act affects sections of Utah Code Annotated 1953 as follows:
9    AMENDS:
10         41-1a-202, as last amended by Chapter 360, Laws of Utah 1997
11         41-6-1, as last amended by Chapter 208, Laws of Utah 1996
12         41-6-107.8, as last amended by Chapter 208, Laws of Utah 1996
13         41-12a-301, as last amended by Chapters 128 and 208, Laws of Utah 1996
14    ENACTS:
15         41-6-90.5, Utah Code Annotated 1953
16    Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah:
17        Section 1. Section 41-1a-202 is amended to read:
18         41-1a-202. Definitions -- Vehicles exempt from registration -- Registration of vehicles
19     after establishing residency.
20        (1) In this section:
21        (a) "Domicile" means the place:
22        (i) where an individual has a fixed permanent home and principal establishment;
23        (ii) to which the individual if absent, intends to return; and
24        (iii) in which the individual and his family voluntarily reside, not for a special or
25    temporary purpose, but with the intention of making a permanent home.
26        (b) (i) "Resident" means any of the following:
27        (A) an individual who:

1        (I) has established a domicile in this state;
2        (II) regardless of domicile, remains in this state for an aggregate period of six months or
3    more during any calendar year;
4        (III) engages in a trade, profession, or occupation in this state or who accepts employment
5    in other than seasonal work in this state and who does not commute into the state;
6        (IV) declares himself to be a resident of this state for the purpose of obtaining a driver
7    license or motor vehicle registration; or
8        (V) declares himself a resident of Utah to obtain privileges not ordinarily extended to
9    nonresidents, including going to school, or placing children in school without paying nonresident
10    tuition or fees;
11        (B) any individual, partnership, limited liability company, firm, corporation, association,
12    or other entity that:
13        (I) maintains a main office, branch office, or warehouse facility in this state and that bases
14    and operates a motor vehicle in this state; or
15        (II) operates a motor vehicle in intrastate transportation for other than seasonal work.
16        (ii) "Resident" does not include any of the following:
17        (A) a member of the military temporarily stationed in Utah; and
18        (B) an out-of-state student, as classified by the institution of higher education, enrolled
19    with the equivalent of seven or more quarter hours, regardless of whether the student engages in
20    a trade, profession, or occupation in this state or accepts employment in this state.
21        (2) Registration under this chapter is not required for any:
22        (a) vehicle registered in another state and owned by a nonresident of the state or operating
23    under a temporary registration permit issued by the division or a dealer authorized by this chapter,
24    driven or moved upon a highway in conformance with the provisions of this chapter relating to
25    manufacturers, transporters, dealers, lien holders, or interstate vehicles;
26        (b) vehicle driven or moved upon a highway only for the purpose of crossing the highway
27    from one property to another;
28        (c) implement of husbandry, whether of a type otherwise subject to registration or not, that
29    is only incidentally operated or moved upon a highway;
30        (d) special mobile equipment;
31        (e) vehicle owned or leased by the federal government;

- 2 -

1        (f) motor vehicle not designed, used, or maintained for the transportation of passengers
2    for hire or for the transportation of property if the motor vehicle is registered in another state and
3    is owned and operated by a nonresident of this state;
4        (g) vehicle or combination of vehicles designed, used, or maintained for the transportation
5    of persons for hire or for the transportation of property if the vehicle or combination of vehicles
6    is registered in another state and is owned and operated by a nonresident of this state and if the
7    vehicle or combination of vehicles has a gross laden weight of 26,000 pounds or less;
8        (h) trailer of 750 pounds or less unladen weight and not designed, used, and maintained
9    for hire for the transportation of property or person;
10        (i) manufactured home or mobile home;
11        (j) off-highway vehicle currently registered under Section 41-22-3 if the off-highway
12    vehicle is:
13        (i) being towed;
14        (ii) operated on a street or highway designated as open to off-highway vehicle use; or
15        (iii) operated in the manner prescribed in Section 41-22-10.3;
16        (k) off-highway implement of husbandry operated in the manner prescribed in Subsections
17    41-22-5.5(3) through (5); [or]
18        (l) modular and prebuilt homes conforming to the uniform building code and presently
19    regulated by the United States Department of Housing and Urban Development that are not
20    constructed on a permanent chassis;
21        (m) electric assisted bicycles defined under Section 41-6-1; or
22        (n) motor assisted scooters defined under Section 41-6-1.
23        (3) Unless otherwise exempted under Subsection (2), registration under this chapter is
24    required for any motor vehicle, combination of vehicles, trailer, semitrailer, or vintage vehicle
25    within 60 days of the owner establishing residency in this state.
26        Section 2. Section 41-6-1 is amended to read:
27         41-6-1. Definitions.
28        As used in this chapter:
29        (1) "Alley" means a street or highway intended to provide access to the rear or side of lots
30    or buildings in urban districts and not intended for through vehicular traffic.
31        (2) "All-terrain type I vehicle" is used as defined in Section 41-22-2.

- 3 -

1        (3) "Authorized emergency vehicle" means fire department vehicles, police vehicles,
2    ambulances, and other publicly or privately owned vehicles as designated by the commissioner of
3    the Department of Public Safety.
4        (4) "Bicycle" means every device propelled by human power upon which any person may
5    ride, having two tandem wheels, except scooters and similar devices.
6        (5) "Bus" means every motor vehicle designed for carrying more than 15 passengers and
7    used for the transportation of persons; and every motor vehicle, other than a taxicab, designed and
8    used for the transportation of persons for compensation.
9        (6) "Controlled-access highway" means every highway, street, or roadway to or from
10    which owners or occupants of abutting lands and other persons have no legal right of access,
11    except at points as determined by the public authority having jurisdiction over the highway, street,
12    or roadway.
13        (7) "Crosswalk" means:
14        (a) that part of a roadway at an intersection included within the connections of the lateral
15    lines of the sidewalks on opposite sides of the highway measured from the curbs or, in the absence
16    of curbs, from the edges of the traversable roadway; and in the absence of a sidewalk on one side
17    of the roadway, that part of a roadway included within the extension of the lateral lines of the
18    existing sidewalk at right angles to the centerline; or
19        (b) any portion of a roadway at an intersection or elsewhere distinctly indicated for
20    pedestrian crossing by lines or other markings on the surface.
21        (8) "Department" means the Department of Public Safety.
22        (9) "Divided highway" means a highway divided into two or more roadways by unpaved
23    intervening space or by a physical barrier or by a clearly indicated dividing section constructed to
24    impede vehicular traffic.
25        (10) "Electric assisted bicycle" means a moped with an electric motor with a power output
26    of not more than 1,000 watts, which is not capable of propelling the device at a speed of more than
27    20 miles per hour on level ground, and which is not capable of increasing the speed of the device
28    when human power is used to propel the device at more than 20 miles per hour.
29        (11) "Explosives" means any chemical compound or mechanical mixture commonly used
30    or intended for the purpose of producing an explosion and which contains any oxidizing and
31    combustive units or other ingredients in proportions, quantities, or packing so that an ignition by

1    fire, friction, concussion, percussion, or detonator of any part of the compound or mixture may
2    cause a sudden generation of highly heated gases, and the resultant gaseous pressures are capable
3    of producing destructive effects on contiguous objects or of causing death or serious bodily injury.
4        (12) "Farm tractor" means every motor vehicle designed and used primarily as a farm
5    implement, for drawing plows, mowing machines, and other implements of husbandry.
6        (13) "Flammable liquid" means any liquid which has a flashpoint of 100 degrees F. or
7    less, as determined by a tagliabue or equivalent closed-cup test device.
8        (14) "Gross weight" means the weight of a vehicle without load plus the weight of any
9    load on the vehicle.
10        (15) "Highway" means the entire width between property lines of every way or place of
11    any nature when any part of it is open to the use of the public as a matter of right for vehicular
12    travel.
13        (16) "Intersection" means the area embraced within the prolongation or connection of the
14    lateral curblines, or, if none, then the lateral boundary lines of the roadways of two or more
15    highways which join one another.
16        (a) Where a highway includes two roadways 30 feet or more apart, every crossing of each
17    roadway of the divided highway by an intersecting highway is a separate intersection; if the
18    intersecting highway also includes two roadways 30 feet or more apart, then every crossing of two
19    roadways of the highways is a separate intersection.
20        (b) The junction of an alley with a street or highway is not an intersection.
21        (17) "Local authorities" means every county, municipal, and other local board or body
22    having authority to enact laws relating to traffic under the constitution and laws of the state.
23        (18) "Metal tire" means a tire, the surface of which in contact with the highway is wholly
24    or partly of metal or other hard nonresilient material.
25        (19) "Mobile home" means:
26        (a) a trailer or semitrailer which is designed, constructed, and equipped as a dwelling
27    place, living abode, or sleeping place either permanently or temporarily, and is equipped for use
28    as a conveyance on streets and highways; or
29        (b) a trailer or a semitrailer whose chassis and exterior shell is designed and constructed
30    for use as a mobile home, as defined in Subsection (19)(a), but which is instead used permanently
31    or temporarily for the advertising, sales, display, or promotion of merchandise or services, or for

1    any other commercial purpose except the transportation of property for hire or the transportation
2    of property for distribution by a private carrier.
3        (20) "Moped" means a motor-driven cycle having both pedals to permit propulsion by
4    human power, and a motor which produces not more than two brake horsepower and which is not
5    capable of propelling the cycle at a speed in excess of 30 miles per hour on level ground. If an
6    internal combustion engine is used, the displacement may not exceed 50 cubic centimeters and the
7    moped shall have a power drive system that functions directly or automatically without clutching
8    or shifting by the operator after the drive system is engaged. A moped includes an electric assisted
9    bicycle and a motor assisted scooter.
10        (21) "Motor assisted scooter" means a self-propelled device with at least two wheels in
11    contact with the ground, a braking system capable of stopping the unit under typical operating
12    conditions, a gas or electric motor not exceeding 40 cubic centimeters, a deck design for a person
13    to stand while operating the device, and the ability to be propelled by human power alone.

17        [(22)] (23) "Motorcycle" means every motor vehicle, other than a tractor, having a seat or
18    saddle for the use of the rider and designed to travel with not more than three wheels in contact
19    with the ground.
20        [(23)] (24) "Motor-driven cycle" means every motorcycle and motor scooter, moped,
21    electric assisted bicycle, motor assisted scooter, and every motorized bicycle having an engine
22    with less than 150 cubic centimeters displacement or having a motor which produces not more
23    than five horsepower.
24        [(24)] (25) "Official traffic-control devices" means all signs, signals, markings, and
25    devices not inconsistent with this chapter placed or erected by authority of a public body or official

15        Section 3. Section 41-6-90.5 is enacted to read:
16         41-6-90.5. Motor assisted scooters - Conflicting provisions -- Restrictions -- Penalties.
17        (1) (a) Except as otherwise provided in this section, a motor assisted scooter is subject to
18    the provisions under this chapter for a bicycle, moped, or a motor-driven cycle.
19        (b) A person operating a motor assisted scooter is not subject to the provisions related to:
20        (i) seating positions under Section 41-6-107; and
21        (ii) required lights, horns, and mirrors under Section 41-6-154.50.
22        (2) A person under 12 years of age may not operate a motor assisted scooter using the
23    motor unless the person is under the direct supervision of the person's parent or guardian.
24        (3) A person may not operate a motor assisted scooter:
25        (a) in a public parking structure;
26        (b) on public property posted as an area prohibiting skateboards;
27        (c) on a highway consisting of a total of four or more lanes designated for regular
28    vehicular traffic;
29        (d) on a highway with a posted speed limit of 30 miles per hour or greater; or
30        (e) that has been structurally altered from the original manufacturer's design.
31        (4) Except where posted or prohibited by rule or local ordinance, a motor assisted scooter

- 9 -

1    is considered a nonmotorized vehicle if it is being used with the motor turned off.
2        (5) A person who violates this section is guilty of a class C misdemeanor.
3        Section 4. Section 41-6-107.8 is amended to read:
4         41-6-107.8. Motorcycle or motor-driven cycle -- Protective headgear -- Closed cab
5     excepted -- Electric assisted bicycles.
6         (1) A person under the age of 18 may not operate or ride on a motorcycle or motor-driven
7    cycle on a highway unless the person is wearing protective headgear which complies with rules

9        (2) This section does not apply to persons riding within an enclosed cab.
10        (3) (a) In accordance with Title 63, Chapter 46a, Utah Administrative Rulemaking Act,
11    the commissioner of the Department of Public Safety shall make rules establishing specifications
12    and standards for the use of protective headgear required under this section.
13        (b) The rules shall require that protective headgear for [operators] an operator of an electric
14    assisted [bicycles meets] bicycle and a motor assisted scooter meet the standards of the American
15    National Standards Institute or the Snell Memorial Foundation's Standards for Protective Headgear
16    for use in bicycling.
17        Section 5. Section 41-12a-301 is amended to read:
18         41-12a-301. Definition -- Requirement of owner's or operator's security --
19     Exceptions.
20        (1) As used in this section, "highway" has the same meaning as provided in Section
21    41-1a-102.
22        (2) Except as provided in Subsection (5):
23        (a) every resident owner of a motor vehicle shall maintain owner's or operator's security
24    in effect at any time that the motor vehicle is operated on a highway within the state; and
25        (b) every nonresident owner of a motor vehicle that has been physically present in this
26    state for:
27        (i) 90 or fewer days during the preceding 365 days shall maintain the type and amount of
28    owner's or operator's security required in his place of residence, in effect continuously throughout
29    the period the motor vehicle remains within Utah; or
30        (ii) more than 90 days during the preceding 365 days shall thereafter maintain owner's or
31    operator's security in effect continuously throughout the period the motor vehicle remains within

- 10 -

1    Utah.
2        (3) (a) Except as provided in Subsection (5), the state and all of its political subdivisions
3    and their respective departments, institutions, or agencies shall maintain owner's or operator's
4    security in effect continuously for their motor vehicles.
5        (b) Any other state is considered a nonresident owner of its motor vehicles and is subject
6    to Subsection (2)(b).
7        (4) The United States, any political subdivision of it, or any of its agencies may maintain

9        (5) Owner's or operator's security is not required for any of the following:

15        (c) electric assisted bicycles as defined under Section 41-6-1; or
16        (d) motor assisted scooters as defined under Section 41-6-1.
