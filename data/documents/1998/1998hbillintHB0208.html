5    AN ACT RELATING TO WORKFORCE SERVICES; MAKING THE CHAIR OF THE

18    or more panels to hear and decide appeals from the decision of an administrative law judge.
19        (2) (a) A panel shall consist of three impartial members appointed by the governor as
20    follows:
21        (i) the board chair, appointed in accordance with Subsection (5);
22        (ii) one member appointed to represent employers, in making this appointment, the
23    governor shall consider nominations from employer organizations; and
24        (iii) one member appointed to represent employees, in making this appointment, the
25    governor shall consider nominations from employee organizations.
26        (b) No more than two members of a panel may belong to the same political party.
27        (3) (a) The term of a member shall be six years beginning on March 1 of the year the
28    member is appointed, except that the governor shall, at the time of appointment or reappointment,
29    adjust the length of terms to ensure that the terms of members are staggered so that approximately
30    one third of the members are appointed every two years.
31        (b) When a vacancy occurs in the membership for any reason, the replacement shall be

22        (i) in the absence of a regular member or the chair; or

25        (7) The department shall provide the Workforce Appeals Board necessary staff support,
26    except, the board may employ, retain, or appoint legal counsel.

4    appealed] department's prior decision.

6    an administrative law judge, the decision of the administrative law judge is considered a decision
7    of the Workforce Appeals Board for purposes of judicial review and is subject to judicial review

16    the department unless within 30 days after the date the decision of the Workforce Appeals Board
17    is issued, further appeal is initiated under this title.
