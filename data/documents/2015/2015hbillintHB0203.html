7     LONG TITLE
8     General Description:
9          This bill amends provisions related to the Teacher Salary Supplement Program.
10     Highlighted Provisions:
11          This bill:
12          ▸     amends the definition of an eligible teacher;
13          ▸     changes the amount of the salary supplement for eligible teachers;
14          ▸     changes the entity that distributes money for the Teacher Salary Supplement
15     Program; and
16          ▸     makes technical and conforming and changes.
17     Money Appropriated in this Bill:
18          None
19     Other Special Clauses:
20          This bill provides a special effective date.
21     Utah Code Sections Affected:
22     AMENDS:
23          53A-17a-156, as last amended by Laws of Utah 2014, Chapter 351
24          53A-17a-157, as enacted by Laws of Utah 2008, Chapter 397

26     Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah:
27          Section 1. Section 53A-17a-156 is amended to read:
28          53A-17a-156. Teacher Salary Supplement Program -- Appeal process.
29          (1) As used in this section:
30          (a) "Board" means the State Board of Education.
31          (b) "Eligible teacher" means a teacher who:
32          (i) has an assignment to teach:
33          (A) a secondary school level mathematics course;
34          (B) integrated science in grade seven or eight;
35          (C) chemistry; [or]
36          (D) physics;
37          (E) computer science; or
38          (F) special education.
39          (ii) holds the appropriate endorsement for the assigned course;
40          (iii) has qualifying educational background; and
41          (iv) (A) is a new employee; or
42          (B) received a satisfactory rating or above on the teacher's most recent evaluation.
43          (c) "Qualifying educational background" means:
44          (i) for a teacher who is assigned a secondary school level mathematics course:
45          (A) a bachelor's degree major, master's degree, or doctoral degree in mathematics; or
46          (B) a bachelor's degree major, master's degree, or doctoral degree that has course
47     requirements that are substantially equivalent to the course requirements for a bachelor's degree
48     major, master's degree, or doctoral degree in mathematics; and
49          (ii) for a teacher who is assigned a grade seven or eight integrated science course,
50     chemistry course, [or] physics course, computer science course, or special education course, a
51     bachelor's degree major, master's degree, or doctoral degree in:
52          (A) integrated science;
53          (B) chemistry;
54          (C) physics;
55          (D) physical science;
56          (E) computer science;
57          (F) special education;
58          [(E)] (G) general science; or
59          [(F)] (H) a bachelor's degree major, master's degree, or doctoral degree that has course
60     requirements that are substantially equivalent to the course requirements of those required for a
61     degree listed in Subsections (1)(c)(ii)(A) through [(E)] (G).
62          (2) (a) Subject to future budget constraints, the Legislature shall annually appropriate
63     money to the Teacher Salary Supplement Restricted Account established in Section
64     53A-17a-157 to fund the Teacher Salary Supplement Program.
65          (b) Money appropriated for the Teacher Salary Supplement Program shall include
66     money for the following employer-paid benefits:
67          (i) retirement;
68          (ii) workers' compensation;
69          (iii) Social Security; and
70          (iv) Medicare.
71          [(3) (a) Beginning in fiscal year 2008-09, the annual salary supplement is $4,100 for an
72     eligible teacher who:]
73          [(i) is assigned full time to teach one or more courses listed in Subsections (1)(b)(i)(A)
74     through (D); and]
75          [(ii) meets the requirements of Subsections (1)(b)(ii) and (iii) for each course
76     assignment.]
77          (3) (a) The annual salary supplement for an eligible teacher who is assigned full time to
78     teach one or more courses listed in Subsections (1)(b)(i)(A) through (F) is:
79          (i) $5,100 for fiscal year 2016;
80          (ii) $6,100 for fiscal year 2017;
81          (iii) $7,100 for fiscal year 2018;
82          (iv) $8,100 for fiscal year 2019;
83          (v) $9,100 for fiscal year 2020; and
84          (vi) $10,000 for fiscal year 2021 and each fiscal year thereafter.
85          (b) An eligible teacher who has a part-time assignment to teach one or more courses
86     listed in Subsections (1)(b)(i)(A) through [(D)] (F) shall receive a partial salary supplement
87     based on the number of hours worked in a course assignment that meets the requirements of
88     Subsections (1)(b)(ii) and (iii).
89          (4) The board shall:
90          (a) create an online application system for a teacher to apply to receive a salary
91     supplement through the Teacher Salary Supplement Program;
92          (b) determine if a teacher:
93          (i) is an eligible teacher; and
94          (ii) has a course assignment as listed in Subsections (1)(b)(i)(A) through [(D)] (F); and
95          (c) verify, as needed, the determinations made under Subsection (4)(b) with school
96     district and school administrators[; and].
97          [(d) certify a list of eligible teachers and the amount of their salary supplement, sorted
98     by school district and charter school, to the Division of Finance.]
99          (5) (a) An eligible teacher shall apply with the board before the conclusion of a school
100     year to receive the salary supplement authorized in this section.
101          (b) An eligible teacher may apply with the board, after verification that the
102     requirements under this section have been satisfied, to receive a salary supplement after the
103     completion of:
104          (i) the school year as an annual award; or
105          (ii) a semester or trimester as a partial award based on the portion of the school year
106     that has been completed.
107          (6) (a) The board shall establish and administer an appeal process for a teacher to
108     follow if the teacher applies for the salary supplement and is not certified under Subsection
109     (4)(d).
110          (b) (i) The appeal process established in Subsection (6)(a) shall allow a teacher to
111     appeal on the basis that the teacher has a degree or degree major with course requirements that
112     are substantially equivalent to the course requirements for a degree listed in Subsection
113     (1)(c)(i)(A) or Subsections (1)(c)(ii)(A) through [(E)] (G).
114          (ii) A teacher shall provide transcripts and other documentation to the board in order
115     for the board to determine if the teacher has a degree or degree major with course requirements
116     that are substantially equivalent to the course requirements for a degree listed in Subsection
117     (1)(c)(i)(A) or Subsections (1)(c)(ii)(A) through [(E)] (G).
118          (7) (a) The [Division of Finance] board shall distribute money from the Teacher Salary
119     Supplement Restricted Account to school districts and charter schools for the Teacher Salary
120     Supplement Program in accordance with the provisions of this section.
121          (b) The board shall include the employer-paid benefits described under Subsection
122     (2)(b) in the amount of each salary supplement [certified to the Division of Finance].
123          (c) The employer-paid benefits described under Subsection (2)(b) are an addition to the
124     salary supplement limits described under Subsection (3).
125          (8) (a) Money received from the Teacher Salary Supplement Restricted Account shall
126     be used by a school district or charter school to provide a salary supplement equal to the
127     amount specified in Subsection (3) for each eligible teacher.
128          (b) The salary supplement is part of the teacher's base pay, subject to the teacher's
129     qualification as an eligible teacher every year, semester, or trimester.
130          (9) Notwithstanding the provisions of this section, if the appropriation for the program
131     is insufficient to cover the costs associated with salary supplements, the board may limit or
132     reduce the salary supplements.
133          Section 2. Section 53A-17a-157 is amended to read:
134          53A-17a-157. Teacher Salary Supplement Restricted Account.
135          (1) There is created within the Uniform School Fund a restricted account known as the
136     "Teacher Salary Supplement Restricted Account."
137          (2) The account shall be funded from appropriations made to the account by the
138     Legislature.
139          (3) The account shall be used to fund teacher salary supplements for school districts
140     and charter schools as provided in Section 53A-17a-156.
141          (4) The [Division of Finance] State Board of Education shall distribute account money
142     to school districts and charter schools for the Teacher Salary Supplement Program as provided
143     in Section 53A-17a-156.
144          Section 3. Effective date.
145          This bill takes effect on July 1, 2015.
