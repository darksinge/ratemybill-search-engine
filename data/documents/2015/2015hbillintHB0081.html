7     LONG TITLE
8     General Description:
9          This bill establishes meeting location requirements for local school boards.
10     Highlighted Provisions:
11          This bill:
12          ▸     requires a local school board to hold the local school board's public meetings within
13     the geographic boundary of the local school board's school district;
14          ▸     provides an exception for meetings held during a declared disaster or local
15     emergency; and
16          ▸     makes technical changes.
17     Money Appropriated in this Bill:
18          None
19     Other Special Clauses:
20          None

25     Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah:
26          Section 1. Section 53A-3-106 is amended to read:
27          53A-3-106. Local school board meetings -- Rules of order and procedure --
28     Location requirements.
29          [(1) As used in this section, "rules of order and procedure" means a set of rules that
30     govern and prescribe in a public meeting:]
31          [(a) parliamentary order and procedure;]
32          [(b) ethical behavior; and]
33          [(c) civil discourse.]
34          (1) As used in this section:
35          (a) "Disaster" means an event that:
36          (i) causes, or threatens to cause, loss of life, human suffering, public or private property
37     damage, or economic or social disruption resulting from attack, internal disturbance, natural
38     phenomenon, or technological hazard; and
39          (ii) requires resources that are beyond the scope of local agencies in routine responses
40     to emergencies and accidents and may be of a magnitude or involve unusual circumstances that
41     require a response by a governmental, not-for-profit, or private entity.
42          (b) "Local emergency" means a condition in any municipality or county of the state,
43     which requires that emergency assistance be provided by the affected municipality or county or
44     another political subdivision to save lives and protect property within its jurisdiction in
45     response to a disaster, or to avoid or reduce the threat of a disaster.
46          (c) "Rules of order and procedure" means a set of rules that governs and prescribes in a
47     public meeting:
48          (i) parliamentary order and procedure;
49          (ii) ethical behavior; and
50          (iii) civil discourse.
51          (2) Subject to Subsection (3), a local school board shall:
52          (a) adopt rules of order and procedure to govern a public meeting of the local school
53     board;
54          (b) conduct a public meeting in accordance with the rules of order and procedure
55     described in Subsection (2)(a); and
56          (c) make the rules of order and procedure described in Subsection (2)(a) available to
57     the public:
58          (i) at each public meeting of the local school board; and
59          (ii) on the local school board's public website, if available.
60          (3) (a) Except as provided in Subsection (3)(b), a local school board may not hold a
61     public meeting outside of the geographic boundary of the local school board's school district.
62          (b) A local school board may hold a public meeting outside of the geographic boundary
63     of the local school board's school district if it is necessary for the local school board to hold a
64     meeting during a disaster or local emergency.
65          [(3) Subjection (2)(a) does] (4) The requirements of this section do not affect a local
66     school board's duty to comply with Title 52, Chapter 4, Open and Public Meetings Act.
