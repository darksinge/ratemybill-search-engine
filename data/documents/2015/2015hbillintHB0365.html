7     LONG TITLE
8     General Description:
9          This bill prohibits a county from electing the county's elected officers through a
10     nonpartisan election.
11     Highlighted Provisions:
12          This bill:
13          ▸     prohibits a county that provided for the election of the county's elected officers
14     through a partisan election in the 2014 general election from changing to a process
15     that provides for the election of the county's elected officers through a nonpartisan
16     election.
17     Money Appropriated in this Bill:
18          None
19     Other Special Clauses:
20          None
21     Utah Code Sections Affected:
22     AMENDS:
23          17-16-6, as last amended by Laws of Utah 2014, Chapter 16
24     

25     Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah:
26          Section 1. Section 17-16-6 is amended to read:
27          17-16-6. County officers -- Time of holding elections -- County commissioners --
28     Terms of office.
29          (1) Except as otherwise provided in an optional plan adopted under Chapter 52,
30     Changing Forms of County Government:
31          (a) each elected county officer shall be elected at the regular general election every four
32     years in accordance with Section 20A-1-201, except as otherwise provided in this title;
33          (b) county commissioners shall be elected at the times, in the manner, and for the terms
34     provided in Section 17-52-501; and
35          (c) an elected officer shall hold office for the term for which the officer is elected,
36     beginning at noon on the first Monday in January following the officer's election and until a
37     successor is elected or appointed and qualified, except as provided in Section 17-16-1.
38          (2) (a) The terms of county officers shall be staggered in accordance with this
39     Subsection (2).
40          (b) Except as provided in Subsection (2)(c), in the 2014 general election:
41          (i) the following county officers shall be elected to one six-year term and thereafter
42     elected to a four-year term:
43          (A) county treasurer;
44          (B) county recorder;
45          (C) county surveyor; and
46          (D) county assessor; and
47          (ii) all other county officers shall be elected to a four-year term.
48          (c) If a county legislative body consolidates two or more county offices in accordance
49     with Section 17-16-3, and the consolidated offices are on conflicting election schedules, the
50     county legislative body shall pass an ordinance that sets the election schedule for the
51     consolidated offices in a reasonable manner that staggers the terms of county officers as
52     provided in this Subsection (2).
53          (3) A county that provided for the election of the county's elected officers through a
54     partisan election in the 2014 general election may not change to a process that provides for the
55     election of the county's elected officers through a nonpartisan election.
