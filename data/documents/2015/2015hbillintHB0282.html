7     LONG TITLE
8     General Description:
9          This bill expands the entities that may offer secondary school level and concurrent
10     enrollment courses through the Statewide Online Education Program and authorizes
11     institutions within the state system of higher education to offer secondary school level
12     and concurrent enrollment courses through the Statewide Online Education Program.
13     Highlighted Provisions:
14          This bill:
15          ▸     allows a program of a higher education institution that offers secondary school level
16     courses or concurrent enrollment courses exclusively online to offer the online
17     courses through the Statewide Online Education Program; and
18          ▸     authorizes an institution within the state system of higher education, including a
19     college campus of the Utah College of Applied Technology, to offer secondary
20     school level courses or concurrent enrollment courses through the Statewide Online
21     Education Program.

33     Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah:
34          Section 1. Section 53A-15-1205 is amended to read:
35          53A-15-1205. Authorized online course providers.
36          The following entities may offer online courses to eligible students through the
37     Statewide Online Education Program:
38          (1) [beginning with the 2011-12 school year,] a charter school or district school created
39     exclusively for the purpose of serving students online; [and]
40          (2) [beginning with the 2011-12 school year,] an LEA program, approved by the LEA's
41     governing board, that is created exclusively for the purpose of serving students online[.]; and
42          (3) a program of an institution of higher education that:
43          (a) offers secondary school level courses or concurrent enrollment courses; and
44          (b) is created exclusively for the purpose of serving students online.
45          Section 2. Section 53B-2a-106 is amended to read:
46          53B-2a-106. College campuses -- Duties.
47          (1) Each Utah College of Applied Technology college campus shall, within the
48     geographic area served by the college campus:
49          (a) offer a non-credit post-secondary and secondary career and technical education
50     curriculum;
51          (b) offer that curriculum at:
52          (i) low cost to adult students, as approved by the board of trustees; and
53          (ii) no tuition to secondary students;
54          (c) provide career and technical education that will result in:
55          (i) appropriate licensing, certification, or other evidence of completion of training; and
56          (ii) qualification for specific employment, with an emphasis on high demand, high
57     wage, and high skill jobs in business and industry;
58          (d) develop cooperative agreements with school districts, charter schools, other higher
59     education institutions, businesses, industries, and community and private agencies to maximize
60     the availability of instructional facilities within the geographic area served by the college
61     campus; and
62          (e) after consulting with school districts and charter schools within the geographic area
63     served by the college campus:
64          (i) ensure that secondary students in the public education system have access to career
65     and technical education at each college campus; and
66          (ii) prepare and submit an annual report to the Utah College of Applied Technology
67     detailing:
68          (A) how the career and technical education needs of secondary students within the
69     region are being met;
70          (B) what access secondary students within the region have to programs offered at
71     college campuses;
72          (C) how the emphasis on high demand, high wage, high skill jobs in business and
73     industry described in Subsection (1)(c)(ii) is being provided; and
74          (D) student tuition and fees.
75          (2) A college campus may offer:
76          (a) a competency-based high school diploma approved by the State Board of Education
77     in accordance with Section 53A-1-402;
78          (b) non-credit, basic instruction in areas such as reading, language arts, and
79     mathematics that are necessary for student success in a chosen career and technical education
80     or job-related program; [and]
81          (c) non-credit courses of interest when similar offerings to the community are limited
82     and courses are financially self-supporting[.]; and
83          (d) secondary school level courses or concurrent enrollment courses through the
84     Statewide Online Education Program in accordance with Section 53A-15-1205.
85          (3) [A] Except as provided in Subsection (2)(d), a college campus may not:
86          (a) offer courses other than non-credit career and technical education or the non-credit,
87     basic instruction described in Subsections (2)(b) and (c);
88          (b) offer a degree;
89          (c) offer career and technical education or basic instruction outside the geographic area
90     served by the college campus without a cooperative agreement between an affected institution,
91     except as provided in Subsection (6);
92          (d) provide tenure or academic rank for its instructors; and
93          (e) participate in intercollegiate athletics.
94          (4) The mission of a college campus is limited to non-credit career and technical
95     education and may not expand to include credit-based academic programs typically offered by
96     community colleges or other institutions of higher education.
97          (5) A campus shall be recognized as a college campus of the Utah College of Applied
98     Technology, and regional affiliation shall be retained and recognized through local
99     designations such as "Bridgerland Applied Technology College: A Utah College of Applied
100     Technology Campus."
101          (6) (a) A college campus may offer career and technical education or basic instruction
102     outside the geographic area served by the college campus without a cooperative agreement, as
103     required in Subsection (3)(c), if:
104          (i) the career and technical education or basic instruction is specifically requested by:
105          (A) an employer; or
106          (B) a craft, trade, or apprenticeship program;
107          (ii) the college campus notifies the affected institution about the request; and
108          (iii) the affected institution is given an opportunity to make a proposal, prior to any
109     contract being finalized or training being initiated by the college campus, to the employer,
110     craft, trade, or apprenticeship program about offering the requested career and technical
111     education or basic instruction, provided that the proposal shall be presented no later than one
112     business week from the delivery of the notice described under Subsection (6)(b).
113          (b) The requirements under Subsection (6)(a)(iii) do not apply if there is a prior
114     training relationship.
115          Section 3. Section 53B-16-108 is enacted to read:
116          53B-16-108. Courses offered through the Statewide Online Education Program.
117          An institution of higher education listed in Section 53B-2-101 may offer secondary
118     school level courses or concurrent enrollment courses through the Statewide Online Education
119     Program in accordance with Section 53A-15-1205.
