H.B. 155

             1     

EDUCATION TESTING AMENDMENTS

             2     
2006 GENERAL SESSION

             3     
STATE OF UTAH

             4     
Chief Sponsor: Neil A. Hansen

             5     
Senate Sponsor: ____________

             6     
             7      LONG TITLE
             8      General Description:
             9          This bill modifies certain education testing requirements.
             10      Highlighted Provisions:
             11          This bill:
             12          .    allows special education and adult education students to pass an alternative
             13      assessment in place of the basic skills competency test required for a basic high
             14      school diploma;
             15          .    provides certain permanent and temporary exemptions for the basic skills
             16      competency test or alternative assessment required for a basic high school diploma;
             17      and
             18          .    makes technical changes.
             19      Monies Appropriated in this Bill:
             20          None
             21      Other Special Clauses:
             22          None
             23      Utah Code Sections Affected:
             24      AMENDS:
             25          53A-1-602, as last amended by Chapter 7, Laws of Utah 2002, Sixth Special Session
             26          53A-1-611, as last amended by Chapter 7, Laws of Utah 2002, Fifth Special Session
             27     

             28      Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah:
             29          Section 1. Section 53A-1-602 is amended to read:
             30           53A-1-602. Definitions.
             31          As used in this part:
             32          (1) "Achievement test" means a standardized test which measures or attempts to
             33      measure the level of performance which a student has attained in one or more courses of study.
             34      Achievement tests shall include norm-referenced and criterion-referenced tests.
             35          (2) "Alternative competency assessment" means a competency assessment for a special
             36      education student or an adult education student, which may include an achievement test or
             37      other types of appropriate competency assessments.
             38          [(2)] (3) "Basic skills course" means a subject which requires mastery of specific
             39      functions, as defined under rules made by the State Board of Education, to include reading,
             40      language arts, mathematics through geometry, science, in grades 4 through 12, and
             41      effectiveness of written expression.
             42          [(3)] (4) "Utah Performance Assessment System for Students" or "U-PASS" means:
             43          (a) systematic norm-referenced achievement testing of all students in grades 3, 5, 8,
             44      and 11 required by this part in all schools within each school district by means of tests
             45      designated by the State Board of Education;
             46          (b) criterion-referenced achievement testing of students in all grade levels in basic
             47      skills courses;
             48          (c) beginning with the 2001-02 school year, a direct writing assessment in grades 6 and
             49      9;
             50          (d) beginning with the 2003-04 school year, a tenth grade basic skills competency test
             51      as detailed in Section 53A-1-611 ; [and]
             52          (e) beginning no later than the 2007-08 school year, an alternative competency
             53      assessment as detailed in Section 53A-1-611 ; and
             54          [(e)] (f) beginning with the 2002-03 school year, the use of student behavior indicators
             55      in assessing student performance.
             56          Section 2. Section 53A-1-611 is amended to read:
             57           53A-1-611. Standards and assessment processes to measure student performance
             58      -- Basic skills competency test.

             59          (1) The Legislature recognizes the need for the State Board of Education to develop
             60      and implement standards and assessment processes to ensure that student progress is measured
             61      and that school boards and school personnel are accountable.
             62          (2) (a) In addition to its responsibilities under Sections 53A-1-603 through 53A-1-605 ,
             63      the State Board of Education, through the state superintendent of public instruction, shall
             64      design a basic skills competency test and develop procedures and requirements for an
             65      alternative competency assessment to be administered in the tenth grade.
             66          [(b) A student must] (b) An alternative competency assessment provided for in this
             67      section may be taken in lieu of the basic skills competency test only by:
             68          (i) a special education student, as determined pursuant to Title 53A, Chapter 15, Part 3,
             69      Education of Children with Disabilities; and
             70          (ii) an adult education student, under Title 53A, Chapter 14, Part 4, Adult Education,
             71      whose class graduated after 2007.
             72          (c) Except as provided under Subsection (2)(f), a student shall pass the basic skills
             73      competency test or alternative competency assessment, in addition to the established
             74      requirements of the state and local board of education of the district in which the student
             75      attends school, in order to receive a basic high school diploma of graduation.
             76          [(c)] (d) The state board shall include in the basic skills competency test or alternative
             77      competency assessment, at a minimum, components on English language arts [and], reading,
             78      and mathematics.
             79          [(d) A] (e) Except as provided under Subsection (2)(f), a student who fails to pass all
             80      components of the basic skills competency test or alternative competency assessment may not
             81      receive a basic high school diploma but may receive a certificate of completion or alternative
             82      completion diploma under rules made by the State Board of Education in accordance with Title
             83      63, Chapter 46a, Utah Administrative Rulemaking Act.
             84          [(e) The] (f) (i) Through the 2006-07 school year, the following students may receive a
             85      basic high school diploma of graduation without passing the basic skills competency test or
             86      alternative competency assessment required under this section:
             87          (A) a special education student, as determined pursuant to Title 53A, Chapter 15, Part
             88      3, Education of Children with Disabilities; and
             89          (B) an adult education student under Title 53A, Chapter 14, Part 4, Adult Education,

             90      whose class graduated before 2007.
             91          (ii) The following students may receive a basic high school diploma of graduation
             92      without passing the basic skills competency test or alternative competency assessment required
             93      under this section:
             94          (A) a student who has passed a comparable test in another state that is approved by
             95      board rule;
             96          (B) a student who moves into this state from another state if the student has less than
             97      two opportunities to pass the basic skills competency test or alternative competency
             98      assessment; and
             99          (C) a student who has passed the general educational development test (GED).
             100          (g) In accordance with Title 63, Chapter 46a, Utah Administrative Rulemaking Act, the
             101      state board shall make rules that:
             102          (i) [to] allow students who initially fail the basic skills competency test or alternative
             103      competency assessment to retake all or part of the test or assessment; [and]
             104          (ii) establish standards and procedures for the alternative competency assessment; and
             105          [(ii) that] (iii) take into account and are consistent with federal law relating to students
             106      with disabilities in the administration of the test.
             107          (3) The state board shall implement:
             108          (a) the tenth grade basic skills competency test, no later than the beginning of the
             109      2003-04 school year[.]; and
             110          (b) the alternative competency assessment no later than the beginning of the 2007-08
             111      school year.
             112          (4) The requirements of this section are to be complementary to the other achievement
             113      testing provisions of this part.
