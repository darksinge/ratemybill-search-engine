H.B. 185

             1     

EDUCATION ACCOUNTABILITY

             2     
2006 GENERAL SESSION

             3     
STATE OF UTAH

             4     
Chief Sponsor: Duane E. Bourdeaux

             5     
Senate Sponsor: ____________

             6     
             7      LONG TITLE
             8      General Description:
             9          This bill defines achievement gap and requires certain actions to address the
             10      achievement gap.
             11      Highlighted Provisions:
             12          This bill:
             13          .    defines accountability system, achievement gap, and student group;
             14          .    requires the State Board of Education to set certain education performance
             15      standards, report specified information, and establish procedures to close any
             16      identified achievement gap;
             17          .    requires schools and school districts to annually report certain information on
             18      achievement of student groups;
             19          .    requires schools and school districts to make plans and take certain actions to close
             20      any identified achievement gap; and
             21          .    makes technical changes.
             22      Monies Appropriated in this Bill:
             23          None
             24      Other Special Clauses:
             25          None

             43          Section 1. Section 53A-1-402 is amended to read:
             44           53A-1-402. Board to establish minimum standards for public schools.
             45          (1) The State Board of Education shall establish rules and minimum standards for the
             46      public schools that are consistent with this title, including rules and minimum standards
             47      governing the following:
             48          (a) (i) the qualification and certification of educators and ancillary personnel who
             49      provide direct student services;
             50          (ii) required school administrative and supervisory services; and
             51          (iii) the evaluation of instructional personnel;
             52          (b) (i) access to programs;
             53          (ii) attendance;
             54          (iii) competency levels;
             55          (iv) graduation requirements; [and]
             56          (v) exemption rates for achievement tests administered under this chapter; and
             57          [(v)] (vi) discipline and control;
             58          (c) (i) school accreditation;

             59          (ii) the academic year;
             60          (iii) alternative and pilot programs;
             61          (iv) curriculum and instruction requirements;
             62          (v) school libraries; and
             63          (vi) services to:
             64          (A) persons with a disability as defined by and covered under:

             68          (B) other special groups;

             75      Education;
             76          (e) closing achievement gaps in:
             77          (i) performance on achievement tests administered under this chapter for individual
             78      students and student groups, including the performance of students who did not perform at a
             79      proficient level in the preceding school year on any assessment administered under this chapter;
             80      and
             81          (ii) other indicators under Section 53A-3-602.5 , as determined by the State Board of
             82      Education;
             83          [(d)] (f) (i) state reimbursed bus routes;

             85          (iii) other transportation needs; and
             86          [(e)] (g) (i) school productivity and cost effectiveness measures;

             88          (iii) the closure of identified achievement gaps;
             89          [(iii)] (iv) school budget formats; and

             90          [(iv)] (v) financial, statistical, and student accounting requirements.
             91          (2) The board shall determine if:
             92          (a) the minimum standards have been met; and
             93          (b) required reports are properly submitted.
             94          (3) The board may apply for, receive, administer, and distribute to eligible applicants
             95      funds made available through programs of the federal government.
             96          (4) (a) The Utah College of Applied Technology shall provide competency-based
             97      career and technical education courses that fulfill high school graduation requirements, as
             98      requested and authorized by the State Board of Education.
             99          (b) A school district may grant a high school diploma to a student participating in
             100      courses described under Subsection (4)(a) that are provided by the Utah College of Applied

             102          Section 2. Section 53A-1-602 is amended to read:
             103           53A-1-602. Definitions.
             104          As used in this part:
             105          (1) "Accountability system" means a comprehensive system for ensuring the academic
             106      achievement of each student, comprised of:
             107          (a) curriculum standards;
             108          (b) data, information, reporting, and assessment systems;
             109          (c) academic performance and academic growth indicators; and
             110          (d) reward and sanction systems that hold both institutions and students accountable
             111      for academic performance and expected academic growth.
             112          (2) "Achievement gap" means:
             113          (a) a difference between the performance of any individual student or a student group
             114      and the state's highest standard and expectation on a performance indicator;
             115          (b) a discrepancy in outcomes for individual students or student groups on a
             116      performance indicator, including:
             117          (i) results of standardized tests, including:
             118          (A) any achievement test administered under this chapter;
             119          (B) the Scholastic Assessment Test (SAT) and Advanced Placement (AP) test
             120      administered by the College Board;

             121          (C) the ACT test administered by ACT, Inc.; and
             122          (D) the National Assessment of Educational Progress;
             123          (ii) high school grade completion and graduation rates; and
             124          (iii) college participation and graduation rates; and
             125          (c) a discrepancy in access to education programs for individual students or student
             126      groups, including access to:
             127          (i) adequate school funding;
             128          (ii) a highly qualified teacher;
             129          (iii) a rigorous curriculum;
             130          (iv) advanced curricula;
             131          (v) classroom materials;
             132          (vi) program offerings; and
             133          (vii) educational technology.
             134          [(1)] (3) "Achievement test" means a standardized test which measures or attempts to
             135      measure the level of performance which a student has attained in one or more courses of study.
             136      Achievement tests shall include norm-referenced and criterion-referenced tests.
             137          [(2)] (4) "Basic skills course" means a subject which requires mastery of specific
             138      functions, as defined under rules made by the State Board of Education, to include reading,
             139      language arts, mathematics through geometry, science, in grades 4 through 12, and
             140      effectiveness of written expression.
             141          (5) "Student group" means students who share similar characteristics, including:
             142          (a) race;
             143          (b) ethnicity;
             144          (c) English language proficiency level;
             145          (d) status as recipient or nonrecipient of free or reduced price school lunch;
             146          (e) gender; and
             147          (f) special education status.
             148          [(3)] (6) "Utah Performance Assessment System for Students" or "U-PASS" means:
             149          (a) systematic norm-referenced achievement testing of all students in grades 3, 5, 8,
             150      and 11 required by this part in all schools within each school district by means of tests
             151      designated by the State Board of Education;

             152          (b) criterion-referenced achievement testing of students in all grade levels in basic
             153      skills courses;
             154          (c) beginning with the 2001-02 school year, a direct writing assessment in grades 6 and
             155      9;
             156          (d) beginning with the 2003-04 school year, a tenth grade basic skills competency test
             157      as detailed in Section 53A-1-611 ; and
             158          (e) beginning with the 2002-03 school year, the use of student behavior indicators in
             159      assessing student performance.
             160          Section 3. Section 53A-1-603 is amended to read:
             161           53A-1-603. Duties of State Board of Education.
             162          (1) The State Board of Education shall:
             163          (a) require each school district to implement the Utah Performance Assessment System
             164      for Students, hereafter referred to as U-PASS;
             165          (b) require the state superintendent of public instruction to submit and recommend
             166      criterion-referenced and norm-referenced achievement tests, a tenth grade basic skills
             167      competency test, and a direct writing assessment for grades 6 and 9 to the board for approval
             168      and adoption and distribution to each school district by the state superintendent;
             169          (c) develop an assessment method to uniformly measure statewide performance,
             170      school district performance, and school performance of students in grades 1 through 12 in
             171      mastering basic skills courses; and
             172          (d) provide for the state to participate in the National Assessment of Educational
             173      Progress state-by-state comparison testing program.
             174          (2) Under U-PASS, the state office shall annually require that each district administer:
             175          (a) a statewide norm-referenced test to all students in grades 3, 5, 8, and 11;
             176          (b) statewide criterion-referenced tests in all grade levels and courses in basic skill
             177      areas of the core curriculum;
             178          (c) a direct writing assessment to all students in grades 6 and 9, with the first
             179      assessment to be administered during the 2001-02 school year; and
             180          (d) a tenth grade basic skills competency test as detailed in Section 53A-1-611 , with
             181      the first test to be administered during the 2003-04 school year.
             182          (3) The board shall adopt rules for the conduct and administration of U-PASS to

             183      include the following:
             184          (a) the computation of student performance based on information that is disaggregated
             185      with respect to race, ethnicity, gender, limited English proficiency, special education status, and
             186      those students who qualify for free or reduced price school lunch;
             187          (b) security features to maintain the integrity of the system, which could include
             188      statewide uniform testing dates, multiple test forms, and test administration protocols;
             189          (c) the exemption of student test scores, by exemption category, such as limited
             190      English proficiency, mobility, and students with disabilities, with the percent or number of
             191      student test scores exempted being publically reported at a district level;
             192          (d) compiling of criterion-referenced and direct writing test scores and test score
             193      averages at the classroom level to allow for:
             194          (i) an annual review of those scores by parents of students and professional and other
             195      appropriate staff at the classroom [level], school, and school district levels at the earliest point
             196      in time and consistent with the timeline of the phase-in referred to in Section 53A-1-602 and
             197      this section, but no later than by the end of the school year beginning with the 2003-04 school
             198      year;
             199          (ii) the assessment of year-to-year student progress in specific classes, courses, and
             200      subjects;
             201          (iii) a teacher to review, prior to the beginning of a new school year for the 2003-04
             202      school year and for each school year thereafter, test scores from the previous school year of
             203      students who have been assigned to the teacher's class for the new school year; and
             204          (iv) allowing a school district to have its tests administered and scored electronically to
             205      accelerate the review of test scores and their usefulness to parents and educators under
             206      Subsections (3)(d)(i), (ii), and (iii), without violating the integrity of U-PASS; and
             207          (e) providing that:
             208          (i) scores on the tests and assessments required under Subsection (2)(b) shall be
             209      considered in determining a student's academic grade for the appropriate course and whether a
             210      student shall advance to the next grade level; and
             211          (ii) the student's score on the tenth grade basic skills competency test shall be recorded
             212      on the student's transcript of credits.
             213          Section 4. Section 53A-1-606.5 is amended to read:

             214           53A-1-606.5. Reading achievement in grades one through three -- Monitoring --
             215      Reporting -- Additional instruction.
             216          (1) (a) The Legislature recognizes that:
             217          (i) reading is the most fundamental skill, the gateway to knowledge and lifelong
             218      learning;
             219          (ii) there is an ever increasing demand for literacy in the highly technological society
             220      we live in;
             221          (iii) students who do not learn to read will be economically and socially disadvantaged;
             222          (iv) reading problems exist in almost every classroom;
             223          (v) almost all reading failure is preventable if reading difficulties are diagnosed and
             224      treated by no later than the end of the third grade; and
             225          (vi) early identification and treatment of reading difficulties can result in students
             226      learning to read by the end of the third grade.
             227          (b) It is therefore:
             228          (i) the long-term goal of the state to have every student in the state's public education
             229      system reading on or above grade level by the end of the third grade; and
             230          (ii) the short-term goal of the state to have 90% or more of all third graders reading on
             231      or above grade level by the end of the third grade in 2006.
             232          (c) (i) The State Board of Education, through the superintendent of public instruction,
             233      shall annually report on progress towards achieving the goals established in Subsection (1)(b).
             234          (ii) The information shall be reported in the school performance report required under
             235      Section 53A-3-602.5 , beginning with the 2002-03 school year, and include the following for
             236      each school district and elementary school:
             237          (A) the number and percent of all students reading on or above grade level at the end of
             238      the first, second, and third grades;
             239          (B) the annual reading achievement growth from the prior year and cumulative reading
             240      achievement growth from the base year of 2001-02 in the percent of students reading on or
             241      above grade level for each year;
             242          (C) those schools that reach the 90% reading achievement goal or achieve a sufficient
             243      magnitude of gain each year as determined by the State Board of Education from the 2001-02
             244      base year or do both; and

             245          (D) those elementary schools that fail to achieve a sufficient magnitude of gain each
             246      year as determined by the State Board of Education from the 2001-02 base year.
             247          (d) The State Board of Education, through the superintendent of public instruction,
             248      shall determine as part of the U-PASS testing program:
             249          (i) the appropriate statewide tests to assess reading levels at the end of each year in the
             250      first, second, and third grades;
             251          (ii) an appropriate standard or cut score on each assessment for determining grade level
             252      reading mastery; and
             253          (iii) the actual percent of students reading on or above grade level in the first, second,
             254      and third grades at each public elementary school as required under Subsection (1)(c)(i) by
             255      dividing the headcount enrollment of students reading on or above grade level at that grade by
             256      the headcount enrollment of students in the building at that grade on the date that the reading
             257      assessment is administered.
             258          (2) Local school boards shall annually review the U-PASS data regarding reading and
             259      achievement gaps identified under Section 53A-1a-108.5 at school and district levels and shall
             260      work with districts and schools to review and revise plans as needed to meet the goal set in
             261      Subsection (1)(b).
             262          (3) (a) Each school district shall require the elementary schools within its district
             263      boundaries to develop, in conjunction with all other school planning processes and
             264      requirements, a reading achievement plan at each school for its kindergarten, first, second, and
             265      third graders to reach the reading goals set in Subsection (1)(b).
             266          (b) The reading achievement plan shall be:
             267          (i) subject to Subsection (3)(e), developed under the direction of the school community
             268      council or a subcommittee or task force created by the school community council; and
             269          (ii) implemented by the school's principal, teachers, and other appropriate school staff.
             270          (c) The school principal shall take primary responsibility to provide leadership and
             271      allocate resources and support for teachers and students, most particularly for those who are
             272      reading below grade level, to achieve the reading goals.
             273          (d) Each reading achievement plan shall include:
             274          (i) an assessment component that:
             275          (A) allows only the principal, teachers, and other appropriate school staff to identify

             276      those students who are reading below grade level;
             277          (B) uses U-PASS and local assessment information throughout the year to determine
             278      students' instructional needs; and
             279          (C) is consistent with the exemption provisions of Subsection 53A-1-603 (3)(c)
             280      regarding such students as students with disabilities or limited English proficiency;
             281          (ii) an intervention component:
             282          (A) that provides adequate and appropriate interventions focused on bringing each
             283      student up to reading at or above grade level and which would permit retention in the grade
             284      level of a student reading below grade level based on a joint determination made by the
             285      principal or the principal's designee, the student's teacher, and the student's parent;
             286          (B) based on best practices identified through proven researched-based methods;
             287          (C) that includes parental participation; and
             288          (D) that, as resources allow, involves a reading specialist; and
             289          (iii) a reporting component consistent with the data to be included in the school
             290      performance report required under Section 53A-3-602.5 .
             291          (e) In developing or reviewing a reading achievement plan, a school community
             292      council or subcommittee or task force of a school community council may not have access to
             293      data that reveal the identity of students.
             294          (4) The school district shall approve each school's plan prior to its implementation and
             295      review each plan annually.
             296          Section 5. Section 53A-1a-103 is amended to read:
             297           53A-1a-103. Public education's mission.
             298          The Legislature recognizes that public education's mission is to assure Utah the best
             299      educated citizenry in the world and each individual the knowledge, skills, and training to
             300      succeed in a global society, by providing all students with learning and occupational skills,
             301      character development, literacy, and basic knowledge through a responsive educational system
             302      that guarantees local school communities autonomy, flexibility, and client choice, while
             303      holding them accountable for the results for all children in each student group.
             304          Section 6. Section 53A-1a-104 is amended to read:
             305           53A-1a-104. Characteristics of public education system.
             306          The Legislature shall assist in maintaining a public education system that has the

             307      following characteristics:
             308          (1) assumes that all students have the ability to learn and that each student departing
             309      the system will be prepared to achieve success in productive employment, further education, or
             310      both;
             311          (2) provides a personalized education plan or personalized education occupation plan
             312      for each student, which involves the student, the student's parent or guardian, and school
             313      personnel in establishing the plan;
             314          (3) provides students with the knowledge and skills to take responsibility for their
             315      decisions and to make appropriate choices;
             316          (4) provides opportunities for students to exhibit the capacity to learn, think, reason,
             317      and work effectively, individually and in groups;
             318          (5) offers a world-class core curriculum that enables all students to successfully
             319      compete in a global society, and to succeed as citizens of a constitutional republic;
             320          (6) incorporates an information retrieval system that provides students, parents, and
             321      educators with reliable, useful, and timely data on the progress of each student, school, and
             322      school district;
             323          (7) attracts, prepares, inducts, and retains excellent teachers for every classroom in
             324      large part through collaborative efforts among the State Board of Education, the State Board of
             325      Regents, and school districts, provides effective ongoing professional development
             326      opportunities for teachers to improve their teaching skills, and provides recognition, rewards,
             327      and compensation for their excellence;
             328          (8) empowers each school district and public school to create its own vision and plan
             329      to achieve results consistent with the objectives outlined in this chapter;
             330          (9) uses technology to improve teaching and learning processes and for the delivery of
             331      educational services;
             332          (10) promotes ongoing research and development projects at the district and the school
             333      level that are directed at improving or enhancing public education and closing achievement
             334      gaps;
             335          (11) offers a public school choice program, which gives students and their parents
             336      options to best meet the student's personalized education needs;
             337          (12) emphasizes the involvement of educators, parents, business partnerships, and the

             338      community at large in the educational process by allowing them to be involved in establishing
             339      and implementing educational goals and participating in decision-making at the school site;
             340      and
             341          (13) emphasizes competency-based standards and progress-based assessments,
             342      including [tracking and] information, accountability, and measurement systems.
