H.B. 219

             1     

CHILD PROTECTION - CLANDESTINE

             2     
LABORATORY OPERATION

             3     
2006 GENERAL SESSION

             4     
STATE OF UTAH

             5     
Chief Sponsor: Eric K. Hutchings

             6     
Senate Sponsor: ____________

             7     
             8      LONG TITLE
             9      General Description:
             10          This bill amends the Juvenile Court Act of 1996 to provide for the protection of
             11      children whose parents permit them to be present at a clandestine laboratory operation.
             12      Highlighted Provisions:
             13          This bill:
             14          .    defines terms;
             15          .    provides that the definition of a neglected child includes a minor whose parent
             16      permits the minor to be present at the location of a clandestine laboratory operation;
             17          .    provides that there is a presumption that reunification services should not be
             18      provided to a parent if the court finds, by clear and convincing evidence, that the
             19      parent knowingly permitted the parent's child to be present at a clandestine
             20      laboratory operation; and
             21          .    makes technical changes.
             22      Monies Appropriated in this Bill:
             23          None
             24      Other Special Clauses:
             25          None

             182           78-3a-301. Court-ordered protective custody of a minor following petition filing

             183      -- Grounds.
             184          (1) After a petition has been filed under Subsection 78-3a-305 (1), if the minor who is
             185      the subject of the petition is not in the protective custody of the division, a court may order that
             186      the minor be removed from the minor's home or otherwise taken into protective custody if the
             187      court finds, by a preponderance of the evidence, that any one or more of the following
             188      circumstances exist:
             189          (a) (i) there is an imminent danger to the physical health or safety of the minor; and
             190          (ii) the minor's physical health or safety may not be protected without removing the
             191      minor from the custody of the minor's parent or guardian;
             192          (b) (i) a parent or guardian engages in or threatens the minor with unreasonable
             193      conduct that causes the minor to suffer emotional damage; and
             194          (ii) there are no reasonable means available by which the minor's emotional health may
             195      be protected without removing the minor from the custody of the minor's parent or guardian;
             196          (c) the minor or another minor residing in the same household has been physically or
             197      sexually abused, or is considered to be at substantial risk of being physically or sexually
             198      abused, by a parent or guardian, a member of the parent's or guardian's household, or other
             199      person known to the parent or guardian;
             200          (d) the parent or guardian is unwilling to have physical custody of the minor;
             201          (e) the minor has been abandoned or left without any provision for the minor's support;
             202          (f) a parent or guardian who has been incarcerated or institutionalized has not arranged
             203      or cannot arrange for safe and appropriate care for the minor;
             204          (g) (i) a relative or other adult custodian with whom the minor has been left by the
             205      parent or guardian is unwilling or unable to provide care or support for the minor[,];
             206          (ii) the whereabouts of the parent or guardian are unknown[,]; and
             207          (iii) reasonable efforts to locate the parent or guardian have been unsuccessful;
             208          (h) the minor is in immediate need of medical care;
             209          (i) (i) a parent's or guardian's actions, omissions, or habitual action create an
             210      environment that poses a threat to the minor's health or safety; or
             211          (ii) a parent's or guardian's action in leaving a minor unattended would reasonably pose
             212      a threat to the minor's health or safety;
             213          (j) the minor or another minor residing in the same household has been neglected;

             214          (k) an infant has been abandoned, as defined in Section 78-3a-313.5 ;
             215          (l) the parent or guardian, or an adult residing in the same household as the parent or
             216      guardian, has been charged or arrested pursuant to Title 58, Chapter 37d, Clandestine Drug Lab
             217      Act, and any clandestine laboratory operation[, as defined in Section 58-37d-3 ,] was located in
             218      the residence or on the property where the minor resided; or
             219          (m) the minor's welfare is otherwise endangered.
             220          (2) (a) For purposes of Subsection (1)(a), if a minor has previously been adjudicated as
             221      abused, neglected, or dependent, and a subsequent incident of abuse, neglect, or dependency
             222      has occurred involving the same substantiated abuser or under similar circumstance as the
             223      previous abuse, that fact constitutes prima facie evidence that the minor cannot safely remain in
             224      the custody of the minor's parent.
             225          (b) For purposes of Subsection (1)(c):
             226          (i) another minor residing in the same household may not be removed from the home
             227      unless that minor is considered to be at substantial risk of being physically or sexually abused
             228      as described in Subsection (1)(c) or Subsection (2)(b)(ii); and
             229          (ii) if a parent or guardian has received actual notice that physical or sexual abuse by a
             230      person known to the parent has occurred, and there is evidence that the parent or guardian
             231      failed to protect the minor, after having received the notice, by allowing the minor to be in the
             232      physical presence of the alleged abuser, that fact constitutes prima facie evidence that the
             233      minor is at substantial risk of being physically or sexually abused.
             234          (3) In the absence of one of the factors described in Subsection (1), a court may not
             235      remove a minor from the parent's or guardian's custody on the basis of:
             236          (a) educational neglect;
             237          (b) mental illness or poverty of the parent or guardian; or
             238          (c) disability of the parent or guardian, as defined in [Subsection 57-21-3 (9)] Section
             239      57-21-2 .
             240          (4) A minor removed from the custody of the minor's parent or guardian under this
             241      section may not be placed or kept in a secure detention facility pending further court
             242      proceedings unless the minor is detainable based on guidelines promulgated by the Division of
             243      Juvenile Justice Services.
             244          (5) This section does not preclude removal of a minor from the minor's home without a

             245      warrant or court order under Section 62A-4a-202.1 .

             272          (3) That notice shall be personally served as soon as possible, but no later than one

             275          (b) both parents and any guardian of the minor, unless they cannot be located.

             276          (4) The following persons shall be present at the shelter hearing:
             277          (a) the child, unless it would be detrimental for the child;
             278          (b) the child's parents or guardian, unless they cannot be located, or fail to appear in

             282          (e) the caseworker from the Division of Child and Family Services who has been

             284          (f) the attorney from the attorney general's office who is representing the division.
             285          (5) (a) At the shelter hearing, the court shall provide an opportunity for the minor's
             286      parent or guardian, if present, and any other person having relevant knowledge, to provide
             287      relevant testimony. The court may also provide an opportunity for the minor to testify.
             288          (b) The court may consider all relevant evidence, in accordance with the Utah Rules of
             289      Juvenile Procedure. The court shall hear relevant evidence presented by the minor, his parent
             290      or guardian, the requesting party, or their counsel, but may in its discretion limit testimony and
             291      evidence to only that which goes to the issues of removal and the child's need for continued
             292      protection.
             293          (6) If the child is in the protective custody of the division, the division shall report to
             294      the court:
             295          (a) the reasons why the minor was removed from the parent's or guardian's custody;
             296          (b) any services provided to the child and his family in an effort to prevent removal;
             297          (c) the need, if any, for continued shelter;
             298          (d) the available services that could facilitate the return of the minor to the custody of
             299      his parent or guardian; and
             300          (e) whether the child has any relatives who may be able and willing to take temporary
             301      custody.
             302          (7) The court shall consider all relevant evidence provided by persons or entities
             303      authorized to present relevant evidence pursuant to this section.
             304          (8) If necessary to protect the child, preserve the rights of a party, or for other good
             305      cause shown, the court may grant no more than one time-limited continuance, not to exceed
             306      five judicial days.

             307          (9) If the child is in the protective custody of the division, the court shall order that the
             308      minor be released from the protective custody of the division unless it finds, by a
             309      preponderance of the evidence, that any one of the following exist:
             310          (a) there is a substantial danger to the physical health or safety of the minor and the
             311      minor's physical health or safety may not be protected without removing him from his parent's
             312      custody. If a minor has previously been adjudicated as abused, neglected, or dependent and a
             313      subsequent incident of abuse, neglect, or dependency occurs, that fact constitutes prima facie
             314      evidence that the child cannot safely remain in the custody of his parent;
             315          (b) (i) the minor is suffering emotional damage, as may be indicated by, but is not
             316      limited to, extreme anxiety, depression, withdrawal, or negative aggressive behavior toward

             375           78-3a-311. Dispositional hearing -- Reunification services -- Exceptions.

             422          (B) The court is not limited to the terms of the concurrent permanency goal in the event
