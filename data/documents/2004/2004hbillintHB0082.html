H.B. 82

             1     

VIRTUAL VISITATION AMENDMENTS

             2     
2004 GENERAL SESSION

             3     
STATE OF UTAH

             4     
Sponsor: Douglas C. Aagard

             5     
             6      LONG TITLE
             7      General Description:
             8          This bill provides that, if available, liberal virtual access be permitted and encouraged
             9      between children and a noncustodial parent.
             10      Highlighted Provisions:
             11          This bill:
             12          .    defines virtual parent-time; and
             13          .    adds virtual parent-time to the parent-time guidelines and schedules.
             14      Monies Appropriated in this Bill:
             15          None
             16      Other Special Clauses:
             17          None
             18      Utah Code Sections Affected:
             19      AMENDS:
             20          30-3-32, as last amended by Chapter 269, Laws of Utah 2003
             21          30-3-33, as last amended by Chapter 255, Laws of Utah 2001
             22          30-3-35, as last amended by Chapter 269, Laws of Utah 2003
             23          30-3-35.5, as last amended by Chapter 176, Laws of Utah 2003
             24     
             25      Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah:
             26          Section 1. Section 30-3-32 is amended to read:
             27           30-3-32. Parent-time -- Intent -- Policy -- Definitions.

             28          (1) It is the intent of the Legislature to promote parent-time at a level consistent with
             29      all parties' interests.
             30          (2) Absent a showing by a preponderance of evidence of real harm or substantiated
             31      potential harm to the child:
             32          (a) it is in the best interests of the child of divorcing, divorced, or adjudicated parents
             33      to have frequent, meaningful, and continuing access to each parent following separation or
             34      divorce;
             35          (b) each divorcing, separating, or adjudicated parent is entitled to and responsible for
             36      frequent, meaningful, and continuing access with his child consistent with the child's best
             37      interests; and
             38          (c) it is in the best interests of the child to have both parents actively involved in
             39      parenting the child.
             40          (3) For purposes of Sections 30-3-32 through 30-3-37 :
             41          (a) "Child" means the child or children of divorcing, separating, or adjudicated parents.
             42          (b) "Christmas school vacation" means the time period beginning on the evening the
             43      child gets out of school for the Christmas or winter school break until the evening before the
             44      child returns to school, except for Christmas Eve and Christmas Day.
             45          (c) "Extended parent-time" means a period of parent-time other than a weekend,
             46      holiday as provided in Subsections 30-3-35 (2)(f) and (2)(g), religious holidays as provided in
             47      Subsections 30-3-33 (3) and (15), and "Christmas school vacation."
             48          (d) "Virtual parent-time" means parent-time facilitated by tools such as video
             49      conferencing, web cams, email, instant messaging, and other wired or wireless technologies
             50      over the internet or other media, including a television with DSL cable, to supplement in
             51      person visits and telephone contact between a noncustodial parent and a child. Virtual
             52      parent-time is designed to supplement, not replace, regular parent-time.
             53          Section 2. Section 30-3-33 is amended to read:
             54           30-3-33. Advisory guidelines.
             55          In addition to the parent-time schedules provided in Sections 30-3-35 and 30-3-35.5 ,
             56      advisory guidelines are suggested to govern all parent-time arrangements between parents.
             57      These advisory guidelines include:
             58          (1) parent-time schedules mutually agreed upon by both parents are preferable to a

             59      court-imposed solution;
             60          (2) the parent-time schedule shall be utilized to maximize the continuity and stability
             61      of the child's life;
             62          (3) special consideration shall be given by each parent to make the child available to
             63      attend family functions including funerals, weddings, family reunions, religious holidays,
             64      important ceremonies, and other significant events in the life of the child or in the life of either
             65      parent which may inadvertently conflict with the parent-time schedule;
             66          (4) the noncustodial parent shall pick up the child at the times specified and return the
             67      child at the times specified, and the child's regular school hours shall not be interrupted;
             68          (5) the custodial parent shall have the child ready for parent-time at the time he is to be
             69      picked up and shall be present at the custodial home or shall make reasonable alternate
             70      arrangements to receive the child at the time he is returned;
             71          (6) the court may make alterations in the parent-time schedule to reasonably
             72      accommodate the work schedule of both parents and may increase the parent-time allowed to
             73      the noncustodial parent but shall not diminish the standardized parent-time provided in
             74      Sections 30-3-35 and 30-3-35.5 ;
             75          (7) the court may make alterations in the parent-time schedule to reasonably
             76      accommodate the distance between the parties and the expense of exercising parent-time;
             77          (8) neither parent-time nor child support is to be withheld due to either parent's failure
             78      to comply with a court-ordered parent-time schedule;
             79          (9) the custodial parent shall notify the noncustodial parent within 24 hours of
             80      receiving notice of all significant school, social, sports, and community functions in which the
             81      child is participating or being honored, and the noncustodial parent shall be entitled to attend
             82      and participate fully;
             83          (10) the noncustodial parent shall have access directly to all school reports including
             84      preschool and daycare reports and medical records and shall be notified immediately by the
             85      custodial parent in the event of a medical emergency;
             86          (11) each parent shall provide the other with his current address and telephone number,
             87      email address, and other virtual parent-time access information within 24 hours of any change;
             88          (12) each parent shall permit and encourage liberal telephone contact and virtual
             89      parent-time when the equipment can reasonably be made available, during reasonable hours,

             90      and uncensored mail privileges with the child;
             91          (13) parental care shall be presumed to be better care for the child than surrogate care
             92      and the court shall encourage the parties to cooperate in allowing the noncustodial parent, if
             93      willing and able, to provide child care;
             94          (14) each parent shall provide all surrogate care providers with the name, current
             95      address, and telephone number of the other parent and shall provide the noncustodial parent
             96      with the name, current address, and telephone number of all surrogate care providers unless the
             97      court for good cause orders otherwise; and
             98          (15) each parent shall be entitled to an equal division of major religious holidays
             99      celebrated by the parents, and the parent who celebrates a religious holiday that the other parent
             100      does not celebrate shall have the right to be together with the child on the religious holiday.
             101          Section 3. Section 30-3-35 is amended to read:
             102           30-3-35. Minimum schedule for parent-time for children 5 to 18 years of age.
             103          (1) The parent-time schedule in this section applies to children 5 to 18 years of age.
             104          (2) If the parties do not agree to a parent-time schedule, the following schedule shall be
             105      considered the minimum parent-time to which the noncustodial parent and the child shall be
             106      entitled:
             107          (a) (i) one weekday evening to be specified by the noncustodial parent or the court
             108      from 5:30 p.m. until 8:30 p.m.; or
             109          (ii) at the election of the noncustodial parent, one weekday from the time the child's
             110      school is regularly dismissed until 8:30 p.m., unless the court directs the application of
             111      Subsection (2)(a)(i);
             112          (b) (i) alternating weekends beginning on the first weekend after the entry of the decree
             113      from 6 p.m. on Friday until 7 p.m. on Sunday continuing each year; or
             114          (ii) at the election of the noncustodial parent, from the time the child's school is
             115      regularly dismissed on Friday until 7 p.m. on Sunday, unless the court directs the application of
             116      Subsection (2)(b)(i);
             117          (c) holidays take precedence over the weekend parent-time, and changes shall not be
             118      made to the regular rotation of the alternating weekend parent-time schedule;
             119          (d) if a holiday falls on a regularly scheduled school day, the noncustodial parent shall
             120      be responsible for the child's attendance at school for that school day;

             121          (e) (i) if a holiday falls on a weekend or on a Friday or Monday and the total holiday
             122      period extends beyond that time so that the child is free from school and the parent is free from
             123      work, the noncustodial parent shall be entitled to this lengthier holiday period; or
             124          (ii) at the election of the noncustodial parent, parent-time over a scheduled holiday
             125      weekend may begin from the time the child's school is regularly dismissed at the beginning of
             126      the holiday weekend until 7 p.m. on the last day of the holiday weekend;
             127          (f) in years ending in an odd number, the noncustodial parent is entitled to the
             128      following holidays:
             129          (i) child's birthday on the day before or after the actual birthdate beginning at 3 p.m.
             130      until 9 p.m.; at the discretion of the noncustodial parent, he may take other siblings along for
             131      the birthday;
             132          (ii) Martin Luther King, Jr. beginning 6 p.m. on Friday until Monday at 7 p.m. unless
             133      the holiday extends for a lengthier period of time to which the noncustodial parent is
             134      completely entitled;
             135          (iii) spring break or Easter holiday beginning at 6 p.m. on the day school lets out for
             136      the holiday until 7 p.m. on the Sunday before school resumes;
             137          (iv) Memorial Day beginning 6 p.m. on Friday until Monday at 7 p.m., unless the
             138      holiday extends for a lengthier period of time to which the noncustodial parent is completely
             139      entitled;
             140          (v) July 24th beginning 6 p.m. on the day before the holiday until 11 p.m. on the
             141      holiday;
             142          (vi) Veteran's Day holiday beginning 6 p.m. the day before the holiday until 7 p.m. on
             143      the holiday; and
             144          (vii) the first portion of the Christmas school vacation as defined in Subsection
             145      30-3-32 (3)(b) plus Christmas Eve and Christmas Day until 1 p.m., so long as the entire holiday
             146      is equally divided;
             147          (g) in years ending in an even number, the noncustodial parent is entitled to the
             148      following holidays:
             149          (i) child's birthday on actual birthdate beginning at 3 p.m. until 9 p.m.; at the discretion
             150      of the noncustodial parent, he may take other siblings along for the birthday;
             151          (ii) Washington and Lincoln Day beginning at 6 p.m. on Friday until 7 p.m. on Monday

             152      unless the holiday extends for a lengthier period of time to which the noncustodial parent is
             153      completely entitled;
             154          (iii) July 4th beginning at 6 p.m. the day before the holiday until 11 p.m. on the
             155      holiday;
             156          (iv) Labor Day beginning at 6 p.m. on Friday until Monday at 7 p.m. unless the holiday
             157      extends for a lengthier period of time to which the noncustodial parent is completely entitled;
             158          (v) the fall school break, if applicable, commonly known as U.E.A. weekend beginning
             159      at 6 p.m. on Wednesday until Sunday at 7 p.m. unless the holiday extends for a lengthier period
             160      of time to which the noncustodial parent is completely entitled;
             161          (vi) Columbus Day beginning at 6 p.m. the day before the holiday until 7 p.m. on the
             162      holiday;
             163          (vii) Thanksgiving holiday beginning Wednesday at 7 p.m. until Sunday at 7 p.m; and
             164          (viii) the second portion of the Christmas school vacation, including New Year's Day,
             165      as defined in Subsection 30-3-32 (3)(b) plus Christmas day beginning at 1 p.m. until 9 p.m., so
             166      long as the entire Christmas holiday is equally divided;
             167          (h) the custodial parent is entitled to the odd year holidays in even years and the even
             168      year holidays in odd years;
             169          (i) Father's Day shall be spent with the natural or adoptive father every year beginning
             170      at 9 a.m. until 7 p.m. on the holiday;
             171          (j) Mother's Day shall be spent with the natural or adoptive mother every year
             172      beginning at 9 a.m. until 7 p.m. on the holiday;
             173          (k) extended parent-time with the noncustodial parent may be:
             174          (i) up to four weeks consecutive at the option of the noncustodial parent;
             175          (ii) two weeks shall be uninterrupted time for the noncustodial parent; and
             176          (iii) the remaining two weeks shall be subject to parent-time for the custodial parent
             177      consistent with these guidelines;
             178          (l) the custodial parent shall have an identical two-week period of uninterrupted time
             179      during the children's summer vacation from school for purposes of vacation;
             180          (m) if the child is enrolled in year-round school, the noncustodial parent's extended
             181      parent-time shall be 1/2 of the vacation time for year-round school breaks, provided the
             182      custodial parent has holiday and phone visits;

             183          (n) notification of extended parent-time or vacation weeks with the child shall be
             184      provided at least 30 days in advance to the other parent; and
             185          (o) telephone contact and virtual parent-time shall be at reasonable hours and for
             186      reasonable duration.
             187          (3) Any elections required to be made in accordance with this section by either parent
             188      concerning parent-time shall be made a part of the decree and made a part of the parent-time
             189      order.
             190          Section 4. Section 30-3-35.5 is amended to read:
             191           30-3-35.5. Minimum schedule for parent-time for children under five years of
             192      age.
             193          (1) The parent-time schedule in this section applies to children under five years old.
             194          (2) If the parties do not agree to a parent-time schedule, the following schedule shall be
             195      considered the minimum parent-time to which the noncustodial parent and the child shall be
             196      entitled:
             197          (a) for children under five months of age:
             198          (i) six hours of parent-time per week to be specified by the court or the noncustodial
             199      parent preferably:
             200          (A) divided into three parent-time periods; and
             201          (B) in the custodial home, established child care setting, or other environment familiar
             202      to the child; and
             203          (ii) two hours on holidays and in the years specified in Subsections 30-3-35 (2)(f)
             204      through (i) preferably in the custodial home, the established child care setting, or other
             205      environment familiar to the child;
             206          (b) for children five months of age or older, but younger than ten months of age:
             207          (i) nine hours of parent-time per week to be specified by the court or the noncustodial
             208      parent preferably:
             209          (A) divided into three parent-time periods; and
             210          (B) in the custodial home, established child care setting, or other environment familiar
             211      to the child; and
             212          (ii) two hours on the holidays and in the years specified in Subsections 30-3-35 (2)(f)
             213      through (i) preferably in the custodial home, the established child care setting, or other

             214      environment familiar to the child;
             215          (c) for children ten months of age or older, but younger than 18 months of age:
             216          (i) one eight hour visit per week to be specified by the noncustodial parent or court;
             217          (ii) one three hour visit per week to be specified by the noncustodial parent or court;
             218          (iii) eight hours on the holidays and in the years specified in Subsections 30-3-35 (2)(f)
             219      through (i); and
             220          (iv) brief phone contact, using virtual parent-time with audio and visual capability
             221      when the equipment reasonably can be made available, with the noncustodial parent at least
             222      two times per week;
             223          (d) for children 18 months of age or older, but younger than three years of age:
             224          (i) one weekday evening between 5:30 p.m. and 8:30 p.m. to be specified by the
             225      noncustodial parent or court; however, if the child is being cared for during the day outside his
             226      regular place of residence, the noncustodial parent may, with advance notice to the custodial
             227      parent, pick up the child from the caregiver at an earlier time and return him to the custodial
             228      parent by 8:30 p.m.;
             229          (ii) alternative weekends beginning on the first weekend after the entry of the decree
             230      from 6 p.m. on Friday until 7 p.m. on Sunday continuing each year;
             231          (iii) parent-time on holidays as specified in Subsections 30-3-35 (2)(c) through (i);
             232          (iv) extended parent-time may be:
             233          (A) two one-week periods, separated by at least four weeks, at the option of the
             234      noncustodial parent;
             235          (B) one week shall be uninterrupted time for the noncustodial parent;
             236          (C) the remaining week shall be subject to parent-time for the custodial parent
             237      consistent with these guidelines; and
             238          (D) the custodial parent shall have an identical one-week period of uninterrupted time
             239      for vacation; and
             240          (v) brief phone contact, using virtual parent-time with audio and visual capability when
             241      the equipment reasonably can be made available, with the noncustodial parent at least two
             242      times per week;
             243          (e) for children three years of age or older, but younger than five years of age:
             244          (i) one weekday evening between 5:30 p.m. and 8:30 p.m. to be specified by the

             245      noncustodial parent or court; however, if the child is being cared for during the day outside his
             246      regular place of residence, the noncustodial parent may, with advance notice to the custodial
             247      parent, pick up the child from the caregiver at an earlier time and return him to the custodial
             248      parent by 8:30 p.m.;
             249          (ii) alternative weekends beginning on the first weekend after the entry of the decree
             250      from 6 p.m. on Friday until 7 p.m. on Sunday continuing each year;
             251          (iii) parent-time on holidays as specified in Subsections 30-3-35 (2)(c) through (i);
             252          (iv) extended parent-time with the noncustodial parent may be:
             253          (A) two two-week periods, separated by at least four weeks, at the option of the
             254      noncustodial parent;
             255          (B) one two-week period shall be uninterrupted time for the noncustodial parent;
             256          (C) the remaining two-week period shall be subject to parent-time for the custodial
             257      parent consistent with these guidelines; and
             258          (D) the custodial parent shall have an identical two-week period of uninterrupted time
             259      for vacation; and
             260          (v) brief phone contact, using virtual parent-time with audio and visual capability when
             261      the equipment reasonably can be made available, with the noncustodial parent at least two
             262      times per week.
             263          (3) A parent shall notify the other parent at least 30 days in advance of extended
             264      parent-time or vacation weeks.
             265          (4) Telephone contact or virtual parent-time shall be at reasonable hours and for
             266      reasonable duration.
