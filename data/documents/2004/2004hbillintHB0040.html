H.B. 40

             1     

FILLING MIDTERM VACANCIES IN THE

             2     
LEGISLATURE

             3     
2004 GENERAL SESSION

             4     
STATE OF UTAH

             5     
Sponsor: Craig A. Frank

             6     
             7      LONG TITLE
             8      General Description:
             9          This bill modifies provisions of the Election Code that govern the process for filling
             10      midterm vacancies in the Legislature.
             11      Highlighted Provisions:
             12          This bill:
             13          .    makes technical corrections to the process for filling vacancies by replacing the
             14      term "central committee" with the term "party liaison";
             15          .    requires each registered political party to establish procedures in its bylaws to
             16      submit names to the governor to fill a legislative vacancy; and
             17          .    makes other technical corrections.
             18      Monies Appropriated in this Bill:
             19          None
             20      Other Special Clauses:
             21          None
             22      Utah Code Sections Affected:
             23      AMENDS:
             24          20A-1-503, as last amended by Chapter 377, Laws of Utah 1998
             25          20A-8-401, as last amended by Chapter 78, Laws of Utah 2001
             26     
             27      Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah:

             28          Section 1. Section 20A-1-503 is amended to read:
             29           20A-1-503. Midterm vacancies in the Legislature.
             30          (1) As used in this section, ["central committee" means: (a) the state central
             31      committee, when the legislative district encompasses more than one county; and (b) the county
             32      central committee, when the legislative district is entirely within one county] "party liaison"
             33      means the political party officer designated to serve as a liaison with the lieutenant governor on
             34      all matters relating to the political party's relationship with the state as required by Section
             35      20A-8-401 .
             36          (2) When a vacancy occurs for any reason in the office of representative in the
             37      Legislature, the governor shall fill the vacancy by:
             38          (a) appointing the person who meets the qualifications for the office whose name was
             39      [submitted by the central committee of the same political party of the prior officeholder]
             40      selected by the same political party as the prior office holder according to the procedure
             41      established in its bylaws and submitted by the party liaison of that political party, if the process
             42      used to nominate the replacement was the standard process used by that political party to select
             43      a candidate without a primary election;
             44          (b) appointing a person who meets the qualifications for the office from two persons
             45      whose names were [submitted by the central committee of the same political party of the prior
             46      officeholder] selected by the same political party as the prior office holder according to the
             47      procedure established in its bylaws and submitted by the party liaison of that political party, if
             48      the process used to nominate those persons was the standard process used by that political party
             49      to select candidates for the primary election ballot; or
             50          (c) appointing a person who meets the qualifications for the office from three persons
             51      [nominated by the central committee of the same political party as the prior officeholder]
             52      whose names were selected by the same political party as the prior office holder according to
             53      the procedure established in its bylaws and submitted by the party liaison of that political party.
             54          (3) (a) When a vacancy occurs for any reason in the office of senator in the Legislature,
             55      it shall be filled for the unexpired term at the next regular general election.
             56          (b) The governor shall fill the vacancy until the next regular general election by:
             57          (i) appointing the person who meets the qualifications for the office whose name was
             58      [submitted by the central committee of the same political party of the prior officeholder]

             59      selected by the same political party as the prior office holder according to the procedure
             60      established in its bylaws and submitted by the party liaison of that political party, if the process
             61      used to select that person was the standard process used by that political party to nominate the
             62      replacement without a primary election;
             63          (ii) appointing a person who meets the qualifications for the office from two persons
             64      whose names were [submitted by the central committee of the same political party of the prior
             65      officeholder] selected by the same political party as the prior office holder according to the
             66      procedure established in its bylaws and submitted by the party liaison of that political party, if
             67      the process used to nominate those persons was the standard process used by that political party
             68      to select candidates for the primary election ballot; or
             69          (iii) appointing a person who meets the qualifications for the office from three persons
             70      [nominated by the central committee of the same political party as the prior officeholder]
             71      whose names were selected by the same political party as the prior office holder according to
             72      the procedure established in its bylaws and submitted by the party liaison of that political party.
             73          Section 2. Section 20A-8-401 is amended to read:
             74           20A-8-401. Registered political parties -- Bylaws.
             75          (1) (a) Each registered state political party shall file a copy of its constitution and
             76      bylaws with the lieutenant governor by January 1, 1995.
             77          (b) Each new or unregistered state political party that seeks to become a registered
             78      political party under the authority of this chapter shall file a copy of its proposed constitution
             79      and bylaws at the time it files its registration information.
             80          (c) Each registered state political party shall file revised copies of its constitution or
             81      bylaws with the lieutenant governor within 15 days after the constitution or bylaws are adopted
             82      or amended.
             83          (2) Each state political party, each new political party seeking registration, and each
             84      unregistered political party seeking registration shall ensure that its constitution or bylaws
             85      contain:
             86          (a) provisions establishing party organization, structure, membership, and governance
             87      that include:
             88          (i) a description of the position, selection process, qualifications, duties, and terms of
             89      each party officer and committees defined by constitution and bylaws;

             90          (ii) a provision requiring a designated party officer to serve as liaison with the
             91      lieutenant governor on all matters relating to the political party's relationship with the state;
             92          (iii) a description of the requirements for participation in party processes;
             93          (iv) the dates, times, and quorum of any regularly scheduled party meetings,
             94      conventions, or other conclaves; and
             95          (v) a mechanism for making the names of delegates, candidates, and elected party
             96      officers available to the public shortly after they are selected;
             97          (b) a procedure for selecting party officers that allows active participation by party
             98      members;
             99          (c) a procedure for selecting party candidates at the federal, state, and county levels that
             100      allows active participation by party members;
             101          (d) (i) a procedure for selecting electors who are pledged to cast their votes in the
             102      electoral college for the party's candidates for president and vice president of the United States;
             103      and
             104          (ii) a procedure for filling vacancies in the office of presidential elector because of
             105      death, refusal to act, failure to attend, ineligibility, or any other cause;
             106          (e) a procedure for submitting names to the governor to:
             107          (i) fill midterm vacancies in the office of representative in the Legislature consistent
             108      with Subsection 20A-1-503 (2)(c); and
             109          (ii) fill midterm vacancies in the office of senator in the Legislature consistent with
             110      Subsection 20A-1-503 (3)(b)(iii);
             111          [(e)] (f) a provision requiring the governor and lieutenant governor to run as a joint
             112      ticket;
             113          [(f)] (g) a procedure for replacing party candidates who die, become disabled, or are
             114      disqualified before a primary or regular general election;
             115          [(g)] (h) provisions governing the deposit and expenditure of party funds, and
             116      governing the accounting for, reporting, and audit of party financial transactions;
             117          [(h)] (i) provisions governing access to party records;
             118          [(i)] (j) a procedure for amending the constitution or bylaws that allows active
             119      participation by party members or their representatives; and
             120          [(j)] (k) a process for resolving grievances against the political party.
