7     LONG TITLE
8     General Description:
9          This bill prohibits sentencing an individual under 18 years of age to life in prison
10     without parole.
11     Highlighted Provisions:
12          This bill:
13          ▸     prohibits sentencing an individual under 18 years of age convicted of a capital crime
14     to life in prison without parole;
15          ▸     allows sentencing convicted capital offenders under 18 years of age only to an
16     indeterminate prison term of not less than 25 years and that may be for life;
17          ▸     provides that the court, rather than a jury, determine the length of prison sentence
18     for an individual younger than 18 years of age;
19          ▸     prohibits sentencing an individual under 18 years of age to life in prison without
20     parole if the individual commits certain additional crimes while serving a sentence;
21     and
22          ▸     makes technical changes.
23     Money Appropriated in this Bill:
24          None
25     Other Special Clauses:
26          None

35     Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah:
36          Section 1. Section 76-3-203.6 is amended to read:
37          76-3-203.6. Enhanced penalty for certain offenses committed by prisoner.
38          (1) As used in this section, "serving a sentence" means a prisoner is sentenced and
39     committed to the custody of the Department of Corrections, the sentence has not been
40     terminated or voided, and the prisoner:
41          (a) has not been paroled; or
42          (b) is in custody after arrest for a parole violation.
43          (2) If the trier of fact finds beyond a reasonable doubt that a prisoner serving a sentence
44     for a capital felony or a first degree felony commits any offense listed in Subsection [(3)] (5),
45     the court shall sentence the defendant to life in prison without parole. [However,]
46          (3) Notwithstanding Subsection (2), the court may sentence the defendant to an
47     indeterminate prison term of not less than 20 years and [which] that may be for life if the court
48     finds that the interests of justice would best be served and states the specific circumstances
49     justifying the disposition on the record.
50          (4) Subsection (2) does not apply if the prisoner is younger than 18 years of age at the
51     time the offense listed in Subsection (5) is committed.
52          [(3)] (5) Offenses referred to in Subsection (2) are:
53          (a) aggravated assault, [Subsection] Section 76-5-103[(2)];
54          (b) mayhem, Section 76-5-105;
55          (c) attempted murder, Section 76-5-203;
56          (d) kidnapping, Section 76-5-301;
57          (e) child kidnapping, Section 76-5-301.1;
58          (f) aggravated kidnapping, Section 76-5-302;
59          (g) rape, Section 76-5-402;
60          (h) rape of a child, Section 76-5-402.1;
61          (i) object rape, Section 76-5-402.2;
62          (j) object rape of a child, Section 76-5-402.3;
63          (k) forcible sodomy, Section 76-5-403;
64          (l) sodomy on a child, Section 76-5-403.1;
65          (m) aggravated sexual abuse of a child, Section 76-5-404.1;
66          (n) aggravated sexual assault, Section 76-5-405;
67          (o) aggravated arson, Section 76-6-103;
68          (p) aggravated burglary, Section 76-6-203; and
69          (q) aggravated robbery, Section 76-6-302.
70          [(4)] (6) The sentencing enhancement described in this section does not apply if:
71          (a) the offense for which the person is being sentenced is:
72          (i) a grievous sexual offense;
73          (ii) child kidnapping, Section 76-5-301.1; or
74          (iii) aggravated kidnapping, Section 76-5-302; and
75          (b) applying the sentencing enhancement provided for in this section would result in a
76     lower maximum penalty than the penalty provided for under the section that describes the
77     offense for which the person is being sentenced.
78          Section 2. Section 76-3-206 is amended to read:
79          76-3-206. Capital felony -- Penalties.
80          (1) A person who has pled guilty to or been convicted of a capital felony shall be
81     sentenced in accordance with this section and Section 76-3-207. [That sentence shall be death,
82     an indeterminate prison term of not less than 25 years and which may be for life, or, on or after
83     April 27, 1992, life in prison without parole.]
84          (2) (a) If the person described in Subsection (1) was 18 years of age or older at the time
85     the offense was committed, the sentence shall be:
86          (i) death;
87          (ii) an indeterminate prison term of not less than 25 years and that may be for life; or
88          (iii) on or after April 27, 1992, life in prison without parole.
89          (b) Subsections (2)(a)(i) and (2)(a)(iii) do not apply if the person was younger than 18
90     years of age at the time the offense was committed.
91          [(2)] (3) (a) The judgment of conviction and sentence of death is subject to automatic
92     review by the Utah State Supreme Court within 60 days after certification by the sentencing
93     court of the entire record unless time is extended an additional period not to exceed 30 days by
94     the Utah State Supreme Court for good cause shown.
95          (b) The review by the Utah State Supreme Court has priority over all other cases and
96     shall be heard in accordance with rules promulgated by the Utah State Supreme Court.
97          Section 3. Section 76-3-207 is amended to read:
98          76-3-207. Capital felony -- Sentencing proceeding.
99          (1) (a) When a defendant has pled guilty to or been found guilty of a capital felony,
100     there shall be further proceedings before the court or jury on the issue of sentence.
101          (b) In the case of a plea of guilty to a capital felony, the sentencing proceedings shall
102     be conducted before a jury or, upon request of the defendant and with the approval of the court
103     and the consent of the prosecution, by the court which accepted the plea.
104          (c) (i) When a defendant has been found guilty of a capital felony, the proceedings
105     shall be conducted before the court or jury which found the defendant guilty, provided the
106     defendant may waive hearing before the jury with the approval of the court and the consent of
107     the prosecution, in which event the hearing shall be before the court.
108          (ii) If circumstances make it impossible or impractical to reconvene the same jury for
109     the sentencing proceedings, the court may dismiss that jury and convene a new jury for the
110     proceedings.
111          (d) If a retrial of the sentencing proceedings is necessary as a consequence of a remand
112     from an appellate court, the sentencing authority shall be determined as provided in Subsection
113     (6).
114          (2) (a) In capital sentencing proceedings, evidence may be presented on:
115          (i) the nature and circumstances of the crime;
116          (ii) the defendant's character, background, history, and mental and physical condition;
117          (iii) the victim and the impact of the crime on the victim's family and community
118     without comparison to other persons or victims; and
119          (iv) any other facts in aggravation or mitigation of the penalty that the court considers
120     relevant to the sentence.
121          (b) Any evidence the court considers to have probative force may be received
122     regardless of its admissibility under the exclusionary rules of evidence. The state's attorney and
123     the defendant shall be permitted to present argument for or against the sentence of death.
124          (3) Aggravating circumstances include those outlined in Section 76-5-202.
125          (4) Mitigating circumstances include:
126          (a) the defendant has no significant history of prior criminal activity;
127          (b) the homicide was committed while the defendant was under the influence of mental
128     or emotional disturbance;
129          (c) the defendant acted under duress or under the domination of another person;
130          (d) at the time of the homicide, the capacity of the defendant to appreciate the
131     wrongfulness of his conduct or to conform his conduct to the requirement of law was impaired
132     as a result of a mental condition, intoxication, or influence of drugs, except that "mental
133     condition" under this Subsection (4)(d) does not mean an abnormality manifested primarily by
134     repeated criminal conduct;
135          (e) the youth of the defendant at the time of the crime;
136          (f) the defendant was an accomplice in the homicide committed by another person and
137     the defendant's participation was relatively minor; and
138          (g) any other fact in mitigation of the penalty.
139          (5) (a) The court or jury, as the case may be, shall retire to consider the penalty. Except
140     as provided in [Subsection] Subsections 76-3-207.5(2) and 76-3-206(2)(b), in all proceedings
141     before a jury, under this section, it shall be instructed as to the punishment to be imposed upon
142     a unanimous decision for death and that the penalty of either an indeterminate prison term of
143     not less than 25 years and which may be for life or life in prison without parole, shall be
144     imposed if a unanimous decision for death is not found.
145          (b) The death penalty shall only be imposed if, after considering the totality of the
146     aggravating and mitigating circumstances, the jury is persuaded beyond a reasonable doubt that
147     total aggravation outweighs total mitigation, and is further persuaded, beyond a reasonable
148     doubt, that the imposition of the death penalty is justified and appropriate in the circumstances.
149     If the jury reports unanimous agreement to impose the sentence of death, the court shall
150     discharge the jury and shall impose the sentence of death.
151          (c) If the jury is unable to reach a unanimous decision imposing the sentence of death,
152     the jury shall then determine whether the penalty of life in prison without parole shall be
153     imposed, except as provided in Subsection 76-3-207.5(2). The penalty of life in prison without
154     parole shall only be imposed if the jury determines that the sentence of life in prison without
155     parole is appropriate. If the jury reports agreement by 10 jurors or more to impose the sentence
156     of life in prison without parole, the court shall discharge the jury and shall impose the sentence
157     of life in prison without parole. If 10 jurors or more do not agree upon a sentence of life in
158     prison without parole, the court shall discharge the jury and impose an indeterminate prison
159     term of not less than 25 years and which may be for life.
160          (d) If the defendant waives hearing before the jury as to sentencing, with the approval
161     of the court and the consent of the prosecution, the court shall determine the appropriate
162     penalty according to the standards of Subsections (5)(b) and (c).
163          (e) If the defendant is sentenced to more than one term of life in prison with or without
164     the possibility of parole, or in addition to a sentence of life in prison with or without the
165     possibility of parole the defendant is sentenced for other offenses which result in terms of
166     imprisonment, the judge shall determine whether the terms of imprisonment shall be imposed
167     as concurrent or consecutive sentences in accordance with Section 76-3-401.
168          (6) Upon any appeal by the defendant where the sentence is of death, the appellate
169     court, if it finds prejudicial error in the sentencing proceeding only, may set aside the sentence
170     of death and remand the case to the trial court for new sentencing proceedings to the extent
171     necessary to correct the error or errors. An error in the sentencing proceedings may not result
172     in the reversal of the conviction of a capital felony. In cases of remand for new sentencing
173     proceedings, all exhibits and a transcript of all testimony and other evidence properly admitted
174     in the prior trial and sentencing proceedings are admissible in the new sentencing proceedings,
175     and if the sentencing proceeding was before a:
176          (a) jury, a new jury shall be impaneled for the new sentencing proceeding unless the
177     defendant waives the hearing before the jury with the approval of the court and the consent of
178     the prosecution, in which case the proceeding shall be held according to Subsection (6)(b) or
179     (c), as applicable;
180          (b) judge, the original trial judge shall conduct the new sentencing proceeding; or
181          (c) judge, and the original trial judge is unable or unavailable to conduct a new
182     sentencing proceeding, then another judge shall be designated to conduct the new sentencing
183     proceeding, and the new proceeding will be before a jury unless the defendant waives the
184     hearing before the jury with the approval of the court and the consent of the prosecution.
185          (7) If the penalty of death is held to be unconstitutional by the Utah Supreme Court or
186     the United States Supreme Court, the court having jurisdiction over a person previously
187     sentenced to death for a capital felony shall cause the person to be brought before the court, and
188     the court shall sentence the person to life in prison without parole.
189          (8) (a) If the appellate court's final decision regarding any appeal of a sentence of death
190     precludes the imposition of the death penalty due to mental retardation or subaverage general
191     intellectual functioning under Section 77-15a-101, the court having jurisdiction over a
192     defendant previously sentenced to death for a capital felony shall cause the defendant to be
193     brought before the sentencing court, and the court shall sentence the defendant to life in prison
194     without parole.
195          (b) If the appellate court precludes the imposition of the death penalty under
196     Subsection (8)(a), but the appellate court finds that sentencing the defendant to life in prison
197     without parole is likely to result in a manifest injustice, it may remand the case to the
198     sentencing court for further sentencing proceedings to determine if the defendant should serve
199     a sentence of life in prison without parole or an indeterminate prison term of not less than 25
200     years and which may be for life.
201          Section 4. Section 76-3-207.5 is amended to read:
202          76-3-207.5. Applicability -- Effect on sentencing -- Options of offenders.
203          (1) (a) The sentencing option of life without parole provided in Sections 76-3-201 and
204     76-3-207 applies only to those capital felonies for which the offender was 18 years of age or
205     older at the time the offense was committed and is sentenced on or after April 27, 1992.
206          (b) The sentencing option of life without parole provided in Sections 76-3-201 and
207     76-3-207 has no effect on sentences imposed in capital cases prior to April 27, 1992.
208          (2) An offender, who commits a capital felony prior to April 27, 1992, but is sentenced
209     on or after April 27, 1992, shall be given the option, prior to a sentencing hearing pursuant to
210     Section 76-3-207, to proceed either under the law which was in effect at the time the offense
211     was committed or under the additional sentencing option of life in prison without parole
212     provided in Sections 76-3-201 and 76-3-207.
213          (3) Notwithstanding any provision of this title, an offender may not be sentenced to life
214     without parole if the offender is younger than 18 years of age at the time the offense was
215     committed.
216          Section 5. Section 76-3-207.7 is amended to read:
217          76-3-207.7. First degree felony aggravated murder -- Noncapital felony --
218     Penalties -- Sentenced by court.
219          (1) A person who has pled guilty to or been convicted of first degree felony aggravated
220     murder under Section 76-5-202 shall be sentenced by the court.
221          (2) (a) The sentence under this section shall be:
222          (i) life in prison without parole; or
223          (ii) an indeterminate prison term of not less than 25 years and [which] that may be for
224     life.
225          (b) Subsection (2)(a)(i) does not apply if the person was younger than 18 years of age
226     at the time the offense was committed.
