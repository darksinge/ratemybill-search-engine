7     LONG TITLE
8     Committee Note:
9          The Education Interim Committee recommended this bill.
10     General Description:
11          This bill modifies and enacts provisions regarding early grade academic improvement.
12     Highlighted Provisions:
13          This bill:
14          ▸     requires the State Board of Education to:
15               •     develop kindergarten entry and exit assessments for use by certain kindergarten
16     student academic improvement programs; and
17               •     administer a grant program to support certain kindergarten student academic
18     improvement programs; and
19          ▸     recodifies and makes technical changes to provisions related to early interactive
20     reading software.
21     Money Appropriated in this Bill:
22          This bill appropriates in fiscal year 2017:
23          ▸     to State Board of Education -- Minimum School Program -- Related to Basic School
24     Program -- Early Intervention Program:
25               •     from the Education Fund, ($7,500,000).
26          ▸     to State Board of Education -- Minimum School Program -- Related to Basic School
27     Program -- Optional Enhanced Kindergarten Program, as an ongoing appropriation:
28               •     from the Education Fund, $17,500,000.

37     Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah:
38          Section 1. Section 53A-17a-167 is repealed and reenacted to read:
39          53A-17a-167. Grant funding for optional enhanced kindergarten programs.
40          (1) As used in this section:
41          (a) "Board" means the State Board of Education.
42          (b) "Local Education Agency" or "LEA" means a school district or charter school.
43          (c) "Optional enhanced kindergarten program" or "OEK program" means a program to
44     improve the academic competency of kindergarten students that:
45          (i) meets the criteria described in Subsection (4)(a);
46          (ii) receives funding from a grant program described in Subsection (3); and
47          (iii) is delivered by an LEA.
48          (2) (a) In accordance with this section, the board shall distribute funds appropriated
49     under this section to support optional enhanced kindergarten programs.
50          (b) The board may use up to 1% of the funds described in Subsection (2)(a) for the
51     administrative cost of running the grant programs described in Subsection (3).
52          (c) The board shall develop kindergarten entry and exit assessments for use by an OEK
53     program.
54          (3) (a) The board shall administer a qualifying base amount grant program and a
55     competitive grant program as described in this Subsection (3) to distribute funds described in
56     Subsection (2)(a) to LEAs that:
57          (i) apply for a grant; and
58          (ii) offer a kindergarten student academic improvement program that meets the
59     requirements described in Subsection (4).
60          (b) For each LEA that meets the criteria described in Subsection (3)(a), the board shall
61     administer a base amount grant to fund a minimum OEK program, taking into consideration:
62          (i) the number of the LEA's students who were eligible to receive free lunch in the
63     prior school year; and
64          (ii) the LEA's overall need for an OEK program, based on the results of the LEA's
65     kindergarten entry and exit assessments described in Subsection (4)(b)(ii).
66          (c) After distributing the base amount grants as described in Subsection (3)(b), the
67     board shall, on a competitive basis, distribute remaining grant money to one or more LEAs that
68     received a base amount grant under Subsection (3)(b), taking into consideration:
69          (i) the success of an LEA's OEK program, based on the results of the LEA's
70     kindergarten entry and exit assessments described in Subsection (4)(b)(ii); and
71          (ii) the extent to which an LEA's base amount grant does not meet the LEA's overall
72     need described in Subsection (3)(b)(ii).
73          (4) An LEA that receives a grant as described in Subsection (3) shall:
74          (a) use the grant money to offer an OEK program that:
75          (i) targets kindergarten students at risk for not meeting grade 3 core standards for Utah
76     public schools, established by the board under Section 53A-1-402.6, by the end of each
77     student's grade 3 year;
78          (ii) uses an evidence-based early intervention model;
79          (iii) focuses on academically improving age-appropriate literacy and numeracy skills;
80          (iv) emphasizes the use of live instruction;
81          (v) administers the kindergarten entry and exit assessments described in Subsection
82     (2)(c); and
83          (vi) is delivered through additional hours or other means; and
84          (b) report to the board annually regarding:
85          (i) how the LEA used grant money received under Subsection (3); and
86          (ii) the results of the LEA's kindergarten entry and exit assessments for the prior year.
87          (5) (a) Except as provided in Subsection (5)(b), an LEA may not charge a fee for a
88     student to participate in an OEK program.
89          (b) If an LEA determines that the LEA's OEK program has additional space at a
90     particular school after admitting the kindergarten students described in Subsection (4)(a)(i), the
91     LEA may charge a fee and admit additional kindergarten students not described in Subsection
92     (4)(a)(i) to fill the additional space at the school.
93          (6) A parent or legal guardian may decline participation of the parent or legal
94     guardian's kindergarten student in an OEK program.
95          Section 2. Section 53a-17a-167.5 is enacted to read:
96          53a-17a-167.5. Early intervention educational technology program.
97          (1) As used in this section:
98          (a) "Dosage" means amount of instructional time.
99          (b) "License" means a license for the early interactive reading software described in
100     Subsection (3)(a) that is distributed by the State Board of Education under Subsection (3)(b).
101          (2) In accordance with this section, the State Board of Education shall administer an
102     early intervention technology program to improve reading skills of students in kindergarten
103     through grade 3 through the use of early interactive reading software.
104          (3) (a) Subject to legislative appropriations, the State Board of Education shall select
105     and contract with one or more technology providers, through a request for proposals process, to
106     provide early interactive reading software for literacy instruction and assessments for students
107     in kindergarten through grade 3.
108          (b) By August 1 of each year, the State Board of Education shall distribute licenses to
109     school districts and charter schools that apply for the licenses.
110          (c) Except as provided in Subsection (4)(b), if a school district or charter school
111     received a license during the prior year, the State Board of Education shall give first priority to
112     the school district or charter school to receive an equivalent license during the current year.
113          (d) The State Board of Education shall distribute a license not described in Subsection
114     (3)(c) through a competitive process.
115          (4) (a) A public school that receives a license shall use the license:
116          (i) for a student in kindergarten or grade 1:
117          (A) for intervention for the student if the student is reading below grade level; or
118          (B) for advancement beyond grade level for the student if the student is reading at or
119     above grade level;
120          (ii) for a student in grade 2 or 3, for intervention for the student if the student is reading
121     below grade level; and
122          (iii) in accordance with the technology provider's dosage recommendations.
123          (b) The State Board of Education may not distribute a license to a public school that
124     fails to use the early interaction reading software in accordance with the technology provider's
125     dosage recommendations for two consecutive years.
126          (5) (a) On or before August 1 of each year, the State Board of Education shall select
127     and contract with an independent evaluator, through a request for proposals process, to act as
128     an independent contractor to evaluate early interactive reading software provided under this
129     section.
130          (b) The State Board of Education shall ensure that a contract with an independent
131     evaluator requires the independent evaluator to:
132          (i) evaluate a student's learning gains as a result of using early interactive reading
133     software provided under Subsection (3);
134          (ii) for an evaluation described in Subsection (5)(b)(i), use an assessment that is not
135     developed by a provider of early interactive reading software; and
136          (iii) determine the extent to which a public school uses the early interactive reading
137     software in accordance with a technology provider's dosage recommendations under
138     Subsection (4).
139          (c) The State Board of Education and the independent evaluator selected under
140     Subsection (5)(a) shall report annually on the results of the evaluation to the Education Interim
141     Committee and the governor.
142          (d) The State Board of Education may use up to 4% of an appropriation provided to
143     fund this section to contract with an independent evaluator selected under Subsection (5)(a).
