7     LONG TITLE
8     General Description:
9          This bill requires justice court judges to be admitted to the practice of law.
10     Highlighted Provisions:
11          This bill:
12          ▸     requires justice court judges to be admitted to the practice of law within this state;
13          ▸     is companion legislation to a proposed constitutional amendment; and
14          ▸     contains language making it effective only upon passage of the constitutional
15     amendment.
16     Money Appropriated in this Bill:
17          None
18     Other Special Clauses:
19          This bill provides a special effective date.
20     Utah Code Sections Affected:
21     AMENDS:
22          78A-7-201, as last amended by Laws of Utah 2012, Chapter 205
23     

24     Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah:
25          Section 1. Section 78A-7-201 is amended to read:
26          78A-7-201. Justice court judge eligibility -- Mandatory retirement.
27          (1) A justice court judge shall be:
28          (a) a citizen of the United States;
29          (b) 25 years of age or older;
30          (c) admitted to practice law in this state;
31          [(c)] (d) a resident of Utah for at least three years immediately preceding [his]
32     appointment;
33          [(d)] (e) a resident of the county in which the court is located or an adjacent county for
34     at least six months immediately preceding appointment; and
35          [(e)] (f) a qualified voter of the county in which the judge resides.
36          [(2) Justice court judges are not required to be admitted to practice law in the state as a
37     qualification to hold office but shall have at the minimum a diploma of graduation from high
38     school or its equivalent.]
39          [(3)] (2) A justice court judge shall be a person who has demonstrated maturity of
40     judgment, integrity, and the ability to understand and apply appropriate law with impartiality.
41          [(4)] (3) A Justice court [judges] judge shall retire upon attaining the age of 75 years.
42          Section 2. Contingent effective date.
43           The amendments proposed by this bill shall have the same effective date as H.J.R. 1,
44     Proposal to Amend Utah Constitution - Judges of Courts Not of Record, if H.J.R. 1 passes
45     during the 2016 General Session, and is approved by a majority of those voting on it at the next
46     regular general election.
