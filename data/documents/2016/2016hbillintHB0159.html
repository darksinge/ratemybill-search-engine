7     LONG TITLE
8     General Description:
9          This bill modifies the Public Safety Code by amending line-of-duty death benefits
10     provisions.
11     Highlighted Provisions:
12          This bill:
13          ▸     repeals a provision that discontinues employer paid health coverage for a surviving
14     spouse of a peace officer or firefighter who dies in the line of duty when the
15     surviving spouse remarries; and
16          ▸     makes technical changes.
17     Money Appropriated in this Bill:
18          None
19     Other Special Clauses:
20          This bill provides for retrospective operation.
21     Utah Code Sections Affected:
22     AMENDS:
23          53-17-201, as enacted by Laws of Utah 2015, Chapter 166
24     

25     Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah:
26          Section 1. Section 53-17-201 is amended to read:
27          53-17-201. Surviving spouse and children health coverage for line-of-duty death.
28          (1) (a) Subject to Subsection (1)(b), and in accordance with this section, an employer
29     shall allow the surviving spouse and children of a member whose death is classified by the
30     Utah State Retirement Office as a line-of-duty death under the provisions of Title 49, Utah
31     State Retirement and Insurance Benefit Act, to remain eligible for health coverage under the
32     employer's group health plan as if the surviving spouse was an employee of the employer.
33          (b) (i) The employer shall pay 100% of the premium costs and, if the health coverage is
34     a high-deductible plan, the employer share of any contribution into a health savings account for
35     the surviving spouse and dependent children as described under Subsections (1)(a) and (2), and
36     may not require payment from the surviving spouse for premium costs or health savings
37     account contributions as a condition of qualifying to continue to receive the health coverage.
38          (ii) For the first 24 months after the line-of-duty death, the employer shall pay the
39     amount specified under Subsection (1)(b)(i).
40          (iii) Beginning 25 months after the line-of-duty death, an employer may pay the
41     amount specified under Subsection (1)(b)(i) through a cost-sharing agreement under Section
42     53-17-301 associated with the trust fund created under Section [53-17-301] 53-17-401.
43          (2) An employer shall allow a surviving spouse and children to remain eligible to
44     receive health coverage from the employer under this section at the option of the surviving
45     spouse [until]:
46          (a) for health coverage for the surviving spouse, until the surviving spouse [remarries
47     or] becomes eligible for Medicare [whichever come first]; and
48          (b) [a] for health coverage of a child, until the child reaches the age of 26.
49          (3) This section does not apply to a member who:
50          (a) does not qualify for a line-of-duty death benefit under [the provisions of] Title 49,
51     Utah State Retirement and Insurance Benefit Act;
52          (b) at the time of death, did not receive or qualify to receive employer group health
53     coverage; or
54          (c) is covered under [the provisions of] Section 49-20-406.
55          Section 2. Retrospective operation.
56          This bill has retrospective operation to July 1, 2015.
