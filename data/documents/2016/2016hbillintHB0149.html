8     LONG TITLE
9     General Description:
10          This bill provides for the notification of a practitioner when the medical examiner
11     determines that a death resulted from poisoning or overdose involving a controlled
12     substance that the practitioner may have prescribed to the decedent.
13     Highlighted Provisions:
14          This bill:
15          ▸     requires the medical examiner to provide a report to the Division of Occupational
16     and Professional Licensing (DOPL) when the medical examiner determines that a
17     death resulted from poisoning or overdose involving a prescribed controlled
18     substance;
19          ▸     requires that, when DOPL receives a report described in the preceding paragraph,
20     DOPL shall notify each practitioner who may have written a prescription for the
21     controlled substance involved in the poisoning or overdose; and
22          ▸     makes technical changes.

34     Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah:
35          Section 1. Section 26-4-10.5 is enacted to read:
36          26-4-10.5. Medical examiner to report death caused by prescribed controlled
37     substance poisoning or overdose.
38          (1) If a medical examiner determines that the death of a person who is 12 years of age
39     or older at the time of death resulted from poisoning or overdose involving a prescribed
40     controlled substance, the medical examiner shall, within three business days after the day on
41     which the medical examiner determines the cause of death, send a written report to the
42     Division of Occupational and Professional Licensing, created in Section 58-1-103, that
43     includes:
44          (a) the decedent's name;
45          (b) each drug or other substance found in the decedent's system that may have
46     contributed to the poisoning or overdose, if known; and
47          (c) the name of each person the medical examiner has reason to believe may have
48     prescribed a controlled substance described in Subsection (1)(b) to the decedent.
49          (2) This section does not create a new cause of action.
50          Section 2. Section 58-37f-702 is amended to read:
51          58-37f-702. Reporting prescribed controlled substance poisoning or overdose to a
52     practitioner.
53          (1) [Beginning on July 1, 2012,] The division shall take the actions described in
54     Subsection (2) if the division receives a report from:
55          (a) a medical examiner under Section 26-4-10.5 regarding a death caused by poisoning
56     or overdose involving a prescribed controlled substance; or
57          (b) a general acute hospital under Section 26-21-26[,] regarding admission to a general
58     acute hospital for poisoning or overdose involving a prescribed controlled substance[, the].
59          (2) The division shall, within three business days after the day on which [the] a report
60     in Subsection (1) is received:
61          (a) attempt to identify, through the database, each practitioner who may have
62     prescribed the controlled substance to the patient; and
63          (b) provide each practitioner identified under Subsection [(1)] (2)(a) with:
64          (i) a copy of the report provided by the medical examiner under Section 26-4-10.5 or
65     the general acute hospital under Section 26-21-26; and
66          (ii) the information obtained from the database that led the division to determine that
67     the practitioner receiving the information may have prescribed the controlled substance to the
68     person named in the report.
69          [(2)] (3) It is the intent of the Legislature that the information provided under
70     Subsection [(1)] (2)(b) is provided for the purpose of assisting the practitioner in:
71          (a) discussing with the patient or others issues relating to the poisoning or overdose;
72          (b) advising the patient or others of measures that may be taken to avoid a future
73     poisoning or overdose; and
74          (c) making decisions regarding future prescriptions written for the patient or others.
75          [(3)] (4) Beginning on July 1, 2010, the division shall, in accordance with Section
76     63J-1-504, increase the licensing fee described in Subsection 58-37-6(1)(b) to pay the startup
77     and ongoing costs of the division for complying with the requirements of this section.
