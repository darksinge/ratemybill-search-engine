7     LONG TITLE
8     Committee Note:
9          The Health and Human Services Interim Committee recommended this bill.
10     General Description:
11          This bill amends the definition of "hemp extract" and modifies Title 63I, Chapter 1,
12     Legislative Oversight and Sunset Act, by extending the repeal date of the Hemp Extract
13     Registration Act and the exemption for use or possession of hemp extract.
14     Highlighted Provisions:
15          This bill:
16          ▸     amends the definition of "hemp extract";
17          ▸     modifies Title 63I, Chapter 1, Legislative Oversight and Sunset Act, by extending
18     the repeal date of the Hemp Extract Registration Act to July 1, 2021; and
19          ▸     modifies Title 63I, Chapter 1, Legislative Oversight and Sunset Act, by extending
20     the repeal date of Section 58-37-4.3, Exemption for use or possession of hemp
21     extract, to July 1, 2021.

33     Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah:
34          Section 1. Section 58-37-4.3 is amended to read:
35          58-37-4.3. Exemption for use or possession of hemp extract.
36          (1) As used in this section, "hemp extract" means an extract from a cannabis plant, or a
37     mixture or preparation containing cannabis plant material, that:
38          (a) is composed of less than 0.3% tetrahydrocannabinol by weight;
39          (b) is composed of at least [15%] 5% cannabidiol by weight; and
40          (c) contains no other psychoactive substance.
41          (2) Notwithstanding any other provision of this chapter, an individual who possesses or
42     uses hemp extract is not subject to the penalties described in this chapter for possession or use
43     of the hemp extract if the individual:
44          (a) possesses or uses the hemp extract only to treat intractable epilepsy, as defined in
45     Section 26-56-102;
46          (b) originally obtained the hemp extract from a sealed container with a label indicating
47     the hemp extract's place of origin, and a number that corresponds with a certificate of analysis;
48          (c) possesses, in close proximity to the hemp extract, a certificate of analysis that:
49          (i) has a number that corresponds with the number on the label described in Subsection
50     (2)(b);
51          (ii) indicates the hemp extract's ingredients, including its percentages of
52     tetrahydrocannabinol and cannabidiol by weight;
53          (iii) is created by a laboratory that is:
54          (A) not affiliated with the producer of the hemp extract; and
55          (B) licensed in the state where the hemp extract was produced; and
56          (iv) is transmitted by the laboratory to the Department of Health; and
57          (d) has a current hemp extract registration card issued by the Department of Health
58     under Section 26-56-103.
59          (3) Notwithstanding any other provision of this chapter, an individual who possesses
60     hemp extract lawfully under Subsection (2) and administers hemp extract to a minor is not
61     subject to the penalties described in this chapter for administering the hemp extract to the
62     minor if:
63          (a) the individual is the minor's parent or legal guardian; and
64          (b) the individual is registered with the Department of Health as the minor's parent
65     under Section 26-56-103.
