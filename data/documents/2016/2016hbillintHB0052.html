11     General Description:
12          This bill modifies provisions related to the Utah Office of Outdoor Recreation.

14          This bill:
15          ▸     defines terms;
16          ▸     creates the Outdoor Recreational Infrastructure Grant Program;
17          ▸     describes the requirements and purposes of the program;
18          ▸     grants rulemaking authority to the Utah Office of Outdoor Recreation; and
19          ▸     makes technical changes.

39     Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah:
40          Section 1. Section 63N-9-102 is amended to read:
41          63N-9-102. Definitions.
42          As used in this chapter:
43          (1) "Accessible to the general public," in relation to the awarding of an infrastructure
44     grant, means:
45          (a) the public may use the infrastructure in accordance with federal and state
46     regulations; and
47          (b) no community or group retains exclusive rights to access the infrastructure.
48          [(1)] (2) "Director" means the director of the outdoor recreation office.
49          [(2)] (3) "Executive director" means the executive director of GOED.
50          (4) "Infrastructure grant" means an outdoor recreational infrastructure grant described
51     in Section 63N-9-202.
52          [(3)] (5) "Outdoor recreation office" means the Utah Office of Outdoor Recreation
53     created in Section 63N-9-104.
54          (6) (a) "Recreational infrastructure project" means an undertaking to build or improve
55     the approved facilities, services, and installations needed for the public to access and enjoy the
56     state's outdoors.
57          (b) "Recreational infrastructure project" may include the:
58          (i) establishment, construction, or renovation of a trail, trail infrastructure, or trail
59     facilities;
60          (ii) construction of a project for water-related outdoor recreational activities;
61          (iii) development of a project for wildlife watching opportunities, including bird
62     watching;
63          (iv) development of a project that provides winter recreation amenities;
64          (v) construction or improvement of a community park that has amenities for outdoor
65     recreation;
66          (vi) construction or improvement of a naturalistic and accessible playground; and
67          (vii) development, establishment, or expansion of a program for youth related to
68     outdoor recreation.
69          (7) "Underserved or underprivileged community" means a group of people, including a
70     municipality, county, or American Indian tribe that:
71          (a) has limited access or has demonstrated a low level of use of recreational
72     infrastructure; and
73          (b) is economically disadvantaged.
74          Section 2. Section 63N-9-104 is amended to read:
75          63N-9-104. Creation of outdoor recreation office and appointment of director --
76     Responsibilities of outdoor recreation office.
77          (1) There is created within the Governor's Office of Economic Development the Utah
78     Office of Outdoor Recreation.
79          (2) (a) The executive director shall appoint a director of the outdoor recreation office.
80          (b) The director shall report to the executive director and may appoint staff.
81          (3) The [purposes of the office are to] outdoor recreation office shall:
82          (a) coordinate outdoor recreation policy, management, and promotion:
83          (i) among state and federal agencies and local government entities in the state; and
84          (ii) with the Public Lands Policy Coordinating Office created in Section 63J-4-602, if
85     public land is involved;
86          (b) promote economic development in the state by:
87          (i) coordinating with outdoor recreation stakeholders;
88          (ii) improving recreational opportunities; and
89          (iii) recruiting outdoor recreation business;
90          (c) recommend to the governor and Legislature policies and initiatives to enhance
91     recreational amenities and experiences in the state and help implement those policies and
92     initiatives;
93          (d) develop data regarding the impacts of outdoor recreation in the state; and
94          (e) promote the health and social benefits of outdoor recreation, especially to young
95     people.
96          (4) By following the procedures and requirements of Title 63J, Chapter 5, Federal
97     Funds Procedures Act, the outdoor recreation office may:
98          (a) seek federal grants or loans;
99          (b) seek to participate in federal programs; and
100          (c) in accordance with applicable federal program guidelines, administer federally
101     funded outdoor recreation programs.
102          (5) For purposes of administering this part, the outdoor recreation office may make
103     rules in accordance with Title 63G, Chapter 3, Utah Administrative Rulemaking Act.

124          Section 5. Section 63N-9-201 is enacted to read:
125     
Part 2. Outdoor Recreational Infrastructure Grant Program

126          63N-9-201. Title.
127          This part is known as the "Outdoor Recreational Infrastructure Grant Program."
128          Section 6. Section 63N-9-202 is enacted to read:
129          63N-9-202. Creation and purpose of infrastructure grant program.
130          (1) There is created the Outdoor Recreational Infrastructure Grant Program
131     administered by the outdoor recreation office.
132          (2) The outdoor recreation office may seek to accomplish the following objectives in
133     administering the infrastructure grant program:
134          (a) build, maintain, and promote recreational infrastructure to provide greater access to
135     low-cost outdoor recreation for the state's citizens;
136          (b) encourage residents and nonresidents of the state to take advantage of the beauty of
137     Utah's outdoors;
138          (c) encourage individuals and businesses to relocate to the state;
139          (d) promote outdoor exercise; and
140          (e) provide outdoor recreational opportunities to an underserved or underprivileged
141     community in the state.
142          Section 7. Section 63N-9-203 is enacted to read:
143          63N-9-203. Rulemaking and requirements for awarding an infrastructure grant.
144          (1) In accordance with Title 63G, Chapter 3, Utah Administrative Rulemaking Act, the
145     outdoor recreation office shall make rules establishing the eligibility and reporting criteria for
146     an entity to receive an infrastructure grant, including:
147          (a) the form and process of submitting an application to the outdoor recreation office
148     for an infrastructure grant;
149          (b) which entities are eligible to apply for an infrastructure grant;
150          (c) specific categories of projects that are eligible for an infrastructure grant;
151          (d) the method and formula for determining grant amounts; and
152          (e) the reporting requirements of grant recipients.
153          (2) In determining the award of an infrastructure grant, the outdoor recreation office
154     may prioritize a project that will serve an underprivileged or underserved community.
155          (3) An infrastructure grant may only be awarded by the executive director after
156     consultation with the director and the board.
157          (4) The following entities may not receive an infrastructure grant under this part:
158          (a) a federal government entity;
159          (b) a state agency;
160          (c) a political subdivision of the state; and
161          (d) a for-profit entity.
162          (5) An infrastructure grant may only be awarded under this part for a project that is
163     accessible to the general public.
