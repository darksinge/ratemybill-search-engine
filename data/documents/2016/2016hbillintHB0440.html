7     LONG TITLE
8     General Description:
9          This bill modifies the duties of the state suicide prevention coordinator.
10     Highlighted Provisions:
11          This bill:
12          ▸     requires the state suicide prevention coordinator to conduct a study on violent
13     incidents that involve a gun;
14          ▸     authorizes the state suicide prevention coordinator to contract with a state agency,
15     private entity, or research institution to assist in the study;
16          ▸     requires reports to the Health and Human Services Interim Committee; and
17          ▸     makes technical changes.
18     Money Appropriated in this Bill:
19          None
20     Other Special Clauses:
21          None

28     Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah:
29          Section 1. Section 62A-15-1101 is amended to read:
30          62A-15-1101. Suicide prevention -- Reporting requirements.
31          (1) As used in the section:
32          (a) "Bureau" means the Bureau of Criminal Identification created in Section 53-10-201
33     within the Department of Public Safety.
34          (b) "Division" means the Division of Substance Abuse and Mental Health.
35          (c) "Intervention" means an effort to prevent a person from attempting suicide.
36          (d) "Postvention" means mental health intervention after a suicide attempt or death to
37     prevent or contain contagion.
38          (e) "State suicide prevention coordinator" means an individual designated by the
39     division as described in Subsections (2) and (3).
40          (2) The division shall appoint a state suicide prevention coordinator to administer a
41     state suicide prevention program composed of suicide prevention, intervention, and postvention
42     programs, services, and efforts.
43          (3) The state suicide prevention program may include the following components:
44          (a) delivery of resources, tools, and training to community-based coalitions;
45          (b) evidence-based suicide risk assessment tools and training;
46          (c) town hall meetings for building community-based suicide prevention strategies;
47          (d) suicide prevention gatekeeper training;
48          (e) training to identify warning signs and to manage an at-risk individual's crisis;
49          (f) evidence-based intervention training;
50          (g) intervention skills training; and
51          (h) postvention training.
52          (4) The state suicide prevention coordinator shall coordinate with at least the following
53     to gather statistics, among other duties:
54          (a) local mental health and substance abuse authorities;
55          (b) prevention coordinator described in Section 53A-15-1301;
56          (c) the Department of Health;
57          (d) health care providers, including emergency rooms; and
58          (e) other public health suicide prevention efforts.
59          (5) The state suicide prevention coordinator shall provide a written report, and shall
60     orally report to the Health and Human Services Interim Committee, by the October meeting
61     every year, on:
62          (a) implementation of the state suicide prevention program, as described in Subsections
63     (2) and (3);
64          (b) data measuring the effectiveness of each component of the state suicide prevention
65     program;
66          (c) funds appropriated for each component of the state suicide prevention program; and
67          (d) five-year trends of suicides in Utah, including subgroups of youths and adults and
68     other subgroups identified by the state suicide prevention coordinator.
69          (6) The state suicide prevention coordinator shall report to the Legislature's:
70          (a) Education Interim Committee, by the October 2015 meeting, jointly with the State
71     Board of Education, on the coordination of suicide prevention programs and efforts with the
72     State Board of Education and the State Office of Education suicide prevention coordinator as
73     described in Section 53A-15-1301[.]; and
74          (b) Health and Human Services Interim Committee, by the October 2017 meeting,
75     statistics on the number of annual suicides in Utah, including how many suicides were
76     committed with a gun, and if so:
77          (i) where the victim procured the gun;
78          (ii) if the victim purchased the gun legally and whether a background check was
79     performed before the victim purchased the gun;
80          (iii) whether the victim had a history of mental illness;
81          (iv) whether drugs or alcohol were also involved in the suicide; and
82          (v) if the suicide incident also involved the injury or death of another individual,
83     whether the shooter had a history of domestic violence.
84          (7) The state suicide prevention coordinator shall consult with the bureau to implement
85     and manage the operation of a firearm safety program, as described in Subsection
86     53-10-202(18) and Section 53-10-202.1.
87          (8) In accordance with Title 63G, Chapter 3, Utah Administrative Rulemaking Act, the
88     division shall make rules governing the implementation of the state suicide prevention
89     program, consistent with this section.
90          Section 2. Section 62A-15-1102 is enacted to read:
91          62A-15-1102. Study on gun use -- Report.
92          (1) As used in this section:
93          (a) "Coordinator" means the state suicide prevention coordinator described in Section
94     62A-15-1101.
95          (b) "Legal intervention" means an incident in which an individual is shot by another
96     individual who has legal authority to use deadly force.
97          (c) "Shooter" means an individual who uses a gun in an act that results in the death of
98     the actor or another individual, whether the act was a suicide, homicide, legal intervention, act
99     of self-defense, or accident.
100          (2) The coordinator shall, by October 30, 2018, conduct a study on use of guns in the
101     state and on an ongoing basis report on the progress and findings of the study to the Health and
102     Human Services Interim Committee.
103          (3) By October 30, 2016, the coordinator shall:
104          (a) determine what information, and from which state and local agencies, will be
105     necessary to complete the study;
106          (b) determine how much the study will cost;
107          (c) make recommendations for legislation, if any, that will be necessary to facilitate
108     information-sharing between local, state, and private entities and the coordinator; and
109          (d) report the findings described in Subsections (3)(a) through (c) to the Health and
110     Human Services Interim Committee.
111          (4) The study described in Subsection (2) shall investigate:
112          (a) the number of deaths in the state that involved a gun, including deaths from suicide,
113     homicide including gang-related violence, legal intervention, self-defense, and accidents;
114          (b) where and how a gun that was involved in a death described in Subsection (4)(a)
115     was procured, and whether that procurement was legal;
116          (c) demographic information on the shooter and, where applicable, a victim of a death
117     described in Subsection (4)(a), including gender, race, and age;
118          (d) the total estimated number of gun owners in the state;
119          (e) information on the shooter, including whether the shooter has a history of:
120          (i) mental illness; or
121          (ii) domestic violence; and
122          (f) whether gun deaths are seasonal.
123          (5) The coordinator may contract with another state agency, private entity, or research
124     institution to assist the coordinator and office with the study required by Subsection (2).
125          (6) The coordinator shall submit a final report on the study described in Subsection (2),
126     including proposed legislation and recommendations, to the Health and Human Services
127     Interim Committee before November 30, 2018.
