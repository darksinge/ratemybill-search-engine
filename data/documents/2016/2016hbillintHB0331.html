7     LONG TITLE
8     General Description:
9          This bill enacts provisions related to school expenditure reporting and funding for
10     educators.
11     Highlighted Provisions:
12          This bill:
13          ▸     requires the State Board of Education to report certain school expenditures;
14          ▸     enacts language related to reimbursement for a teacher who receives a certificate;
15          ▸     amends the Teacher Salary Supplement Program to include a certificate teacher; and
16          ▸     makes technical and conforming corrections.
17     Money Appropriated in this Bill:
18          None
19     Other Special Clauses:
20          None
21     Utah Code Sections Affected:
22     AMENDS:

24     ENACTS:
25          53A-1-414, Utah Code Annotated 1953

28     Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah:
29          Section 1. Section 53A-1-414 is enacted to read:
30          53A-1-414. School expenditures -- Report.
31          (1) As used in this section:
32          (a) "Board" means the State Board of Education.
33          (b) "Local education agency" or "LEA" means:
34          (i) a school district; or
35          (ii) a charter school.
36          (c) "Teacher" means an individual employed by an LEA who:
37          (i) is licensed under Title 53A, Chapter 6, Educator Licensing and Professional
38     Practices Act; and
39          (ii) has an assignment to teach in a classroom.
40          (2) The board or the board's designee shall publish an LEA's total expenditures
41     annually on the board's website by providing the following information for each school within
42     the LEA:
43          (a) total teacher salary expenditures;
44          (b) capital expenditures;
45          (c) overhead and other expenditures not reported under Subsection (2)(a) or (b);
46          (d) the average per pupil funding; and
47          (e) the percent of teacher turnover from the prior school year.
48          (3) An LEA shall provide the information described in Subsections (2)(a) through (e)
49     as requested by the board or the board's designee.
50          Section 2. Section 53A-6-114 is enacted to read:
51          53A-6-114. National Board certification reimbursement.
52          (1) As used in this section:
53          (a) "Eligible educator" means an educator who:
54          (i) holds a current National Board certification; and
55          (ii) is employed as an educator by an LEA.
56          (b) "Local education agency" or "LEA" means:
57          (i) a school district;
58          (ii) a charter school; or
59          (iii) the Utah Schools for the Deaf and the Blind.
60          (2) Subject to legislative appropriations, the board shall reimburse an eligible educator
61     for the cost to attain or renew a National Board certification.
62          (3) The board shall reimburse an eligible educator under this section on a first come,
63     first served basis.
64          (4) The board shall make rules, in accordance with Title 63G, Chapter 3, Utah
65     Administrative Rulemaking Act, specifying procedures and timelines for reimbursing costs
66     under Subsection (2).
67          Section 3. Section 53A-17a-156 is amended to read:
68          53A-17a-156. Teacher Salary Supplement Program -- Appeal process.
69          (1) As used in this section:
70          (a) "Board" means the State Board of Education.
71          (b) "Certificate teacher" means a teacher who:
72          (i) holds a National Board certification; and
73          (ii) is assigned to teach at a Title I school.
74          [(b)] (c) "Eligible teacher" means a teacher who:
75          (i) has an assignment to teach:
76          (A) a secondary school level mathematics course;
77          (B) integrated science in grade seven or eight;
78          (C) chemistry;
79          (D) physics; or
80          (E) computer science;
81          (ii) holds the appropriate endorsement for the assigned course;
82          (iii) has qualifying educational background; and
83          (iv) (A) is a new employee; or
84          (B) received a satisfactory rating or above on the teacher's most recent evaluation.
85          (d) "National Board certification" means the same as that term is defined in Section
86     53A-6-103.
87          [(c)] (e) "Qualifying educational background" means:
88          (i) for a teacher who is assigned a secondary school level mathematics course:
89          (A) a bachelor's degree major, master's degree, or doctoral degree in mathematics; or
90          (B) a bachelor's degree major, master's degree, or doctoral degree that has course
91     requirements that are substantially equivalent to the course requirements for a bachelor's degree
92     major, master's degree, or doctoral degree in mathematics;
93          (ii) for a teacher who is assigned a grade seven or eight integrated science course,
94     chemistry course, or physics course, a bachelor's degree major, master's degree, or doctoral
95     degree in:
96          (A) integrated science;
97          (B) chemistry;
98          (C) physics;
99          (D) physical science;
100          (E) general science; or
101          (F) a bachelor's degree major, master's degree, or doctoral degree that has course
102     requirements that are substantially equivalent to the course requirements of those required for a
103     degree listed in Subsections (1)[(c)](e)(ii)(A) through (E);
104          (iii) for a teacher who is assigned a computer science course, a bachelor's degree major,
105     master's degree, or doctoral degree in:
106          (A) computer science;
107          (B) computer information technology; or
108          (C) a bachelor's degree major, master's degree, or doctoral degree that has course
109     requirements that are substantially equivalent to the course requirements of those required for a
110     degree listed in Subsections (1)[(c)](e)(iii)(A) and (B).
111          (f) "Title I school" means a school that receives funds under the Elementary and
112     Secondary Education Act of 1965, Title I, 20 U.S.C. Sec. 6301 et seq.
113          (2) (a) Subject to future budget constraints, the Legislature shall annually appropriate
114     money to the Teacher Salary Supplement Restricted Account established in Section
115     53A-17a-157 to fund the Teacher Salary Supplement Program.
116          (b) Money appropriated for the Teacher Salary Supplement Program shall include
117     money for the following employer-paid benefits:
118          (i) retirement;
119          (ii) workers' compensation;
120          (iii) Social Security; and
121          (iv) Medicare.
122          (3) (a) (i) The annual salary supplement for an eligible teacher who is assigned full
123     time to teach one or more courses listed in Subsections (1)[(b)](c)(i)(A) through (E) is $4,100.
124          [(b)] (ii) An eligible teacher who has a part-time assignment to teach one or more
125     courses listed in Subsections (1)[(b)](c)(i)(A) through (E) shall receive a partial salary
126     supplement based on the number of hours worked in a course assignment that meets the
127     requirements of Subsections (1)[(b)](c)(ii) and (iii).
128          (b) The annual salary supplement for a certificate teacher is $2,500.
129          (4) The board shall:
130          (a) create an online application system for a teacher to apply to receive a salary
131     supplement through the Teacher Salary Supplement Program;
132          (b) determine if a teacher:
133          (i) (A) is an eligible teacher; and
134          [(ii)] (B) has a course assignment as listed in Subsections (1)[(b)](c)(i)(A) through (E);
135     [and] or
136          (ii) is a certificate teacher;
137          (c) verify, as needed, the determinations made under Subsection (4)(b) with school
138     district and school administrators[.]; and
139          (d) certify a list of eligible teachers and certificate teachers.
140          (5) (a) An eligible teacher or a certificate teacher shall apply with the board before the
141     conclusion of a school year to receive the salary supplement authorized in this section.
142          (b) An eligible teacher or a certificate teacher may apply with the board, after
143     verification that the requirements under this section have been satisfied, to receive a salary
144     supplement after the completion of:
145          (i) the school year as an annual award; or
146          (ii) a semester or trimester as a partial award based on the portion of the school year
147     that has been completed.
148          (6) (a) The board shall establish and administer an appeal process for a teacher to
149     follow if the teacher applies for the salary supplement and is not certified under Subsection (4).
150          (b) (i) The appeal process established in Subsection (6)(a) shall allow a teacher to
151     appeal eligibility as an eligible teacher on the basis that the teacher has a degree or degree
152     major with course requirements that are substantially equivalent to the course requirements for
153     a degree listed in:
154          (A) Subsection (1)[(c)](e)(i)(A);
155          (B) Subsections (1)[(c)](e)(ii)(A) through (E); or
156          (C) Subsections (1)[(c)](e)(iii)(A) and (B).
157          (ii) A teacher shall provide transcripts and other documentation to the board in order
158     for the board to determine if the teacher has a degree or degree major with course requirements
159     that are substantially equivalent to the course requirements for a degree listed in:
160          (A) Subsection (1)[(c)](e)(i)(A);
161          (B) Subsections (1)[(c)](e)(ii)(A) through (E); or
162          (C) Subsections (1)[(c)](e)(iii)(A) and (B).
163          (c) The appeal process established under Subsection (6)(a) shall allow a teacher to
164     appeal eligibility as a certificate teacher on the basis that the teacher:
165          (i) holds a current certificate; or
166          (ii) is assigned to teach at a Title I school.
167          (d) A teacher shall provide to the board:
168          (i) a certificate or other related documentation in order for the board to determine if the
169     teacher holds a current certificate; or
170          (ii) verification that the teacher is assigned to teach at a Title I school.
171          (7) (a) The board shall distribute money from the Teacher Salary Supplement
172     Restricted Account to school districts and charter schools for the Teacher Salary Supplement
173     Program in accordance with the provisions of this section.
174          (b) The board shall include the employer-paid benefits described under Subsection
175     (2)(b) in the amount of each salary supplement.
176          (c) The employer-paid benefits described under Subsection (2)(b) are an addition to the
177     salary supplement limits described under Subsection (3).
178          (8) (a) Money received from the Teacher Salary Supplement Restricted Account shall
179     be used by a school district or charter school to provide a salary supplement equal to the
180     amount specified in Subsection (3) for each eligible teacher or certificate teacher.
181          (b) The salary supplement is part of the teacher's base pay, subject to the teacher's
182     qualification as an eligible teacher or certificate teacher every year, semester, or trimester.
183          (9) Notwithstanding the provisions of this section, if the appropriation for the program
184     is insufficient to cover the costs associated with salary supplements, the board may limit or
185     reduce the salary supplements.
