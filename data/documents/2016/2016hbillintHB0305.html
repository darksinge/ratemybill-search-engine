7     LONG TITLE
8     General Description:
9          This bill deals with the accuracy of water use data.
10     Highlighted Provisions:
11          This bill:
12          ▸     instructs the Drinking Water Board to require a certified water operator of a public
13     water supplier, or professional engineer performing the duties of an operator, to
14     verify the accuracy of water use and supply data submitted to the Division of
15     Drinking Water;
16          ▸     authorizes the Division of Water Rights, in conjunction with the Division of Water
17     Resources and the Division of Drinking Water, to collect and validate water use
18     data;
19          ▸     requires the director of the Division of Water Resources to oversee any validation of
20     water use data;
21          ▸     requires the Division of Water Resources to validate, as funding allows, the
22     accuracy of water use data reported by public water suppliers; and
23          ▸     makes technical changes.
24     Money Appropriated in this Bill:
25          None
26     Other Special Clauses:
27          None

37          Section 1. Section 19-4-104 is amended to read:
38          19-4-104. Powers of board.
39          (1) (a) The board may make rules in accordance with Title 63G, Chapter 3, Utah
40     Administrative Rulemaking Act:
41          (i) establishing standards that prescribe the maximum contaminant levels in any public
42     water system and provide for monitoring, record-keeping, and reporting of water quality related
43     matters;
44          (ii) governing design, construction, operation, and maintenance of public water
45     systems;
46          (iii) granting variances and exemptions to the requirements established under this
47     chapter that are not less stringent than those allowed under federal law;
48          (iv) protecting watersheds and water sources used for public water systems; [and]
49          (v) governing capacity development in compliance with Section 1420 of the federal
50     Safe Drinking Water Act, 42 U.S.C.A. Sec. 300f et seq.; and
51          (vi) specifying:
52          (A) what data a public water supplier shall provide to the division, pursuant to
53     Subsection (1)(c)(iv); and
54          (B) how the division shall validate the data described in Subsection (1)(a)(vi)(A).
55          (b) The board may:
56          (i) order the director to:
57          (A) issue orders necessary to enforce the provisions of this chapter;
58          (B) enforce the orders by appropriate administrative and judicial proceedings; or
59          (C) institute judicial proceedings to secure compliance with this chapter;
60          (ii) (A) hold a hearing that is not an adjudicative proceeding relating to the
61     administration of this chapter; or
62          (B) appoint hearing officers to conduct a hearing that is not an adjudicative proceeding;
63     or
64          (iii) request and accept financial assistance from other public agencies, private entities,
65     and the federal government to carry out the purposes of this chapter.
66          (c) The board shall:
67          (i) require the submission to the director of plans and specifications for construction of,
68     substantial addition to, or alteration of public water systems for review and approval by the
69     board before that action begins and require any modifications or impose any conditions that
70     may be necessary to carry out the purposes of this chapter;
71          (ii) advise, consult, cooperate with, provide technical assistance to, and enter into
72     agreements, contracts, or cooperative arrangements with state, federal, or interstate agencies,
73     municipalities, local health departments, educational institutions, and others necessary to carry
74     out the purposes of this chapter and to support the laws, ordinances, rules, and regulations of
75     local jurisdictions;
76          (iii) develop and implement an emergency plan to protect the public when declining
77     drinking water quality or quantity creates a serious health risk and issue emergency orders if a
78     health risk is imminent; [and]
79          (iv) require a certified operator of a public water supplier to verify by signature and
80     certification number, or a professional engineer performing the duties of a certified water
81     operator to verify by signature and stamp, the accuracy of any data on water use and water
82     supply submitted by the public water supplier to the division; and
83          [(iv)] (v) meet the requirements of federal law related or pertaining to drinking water.
84          (2) (a) The board may adopt and enforce standards and establish fees for certification
85     of operators of any public water system.
86          (b) The board may not require certification of operators for a water system serving a
87     population of 800 or less except:
88          (i) to the extent required for compliance with Section 1419 of the federal Safe Drinking
89     Water Act, 42 U.S.C.A. Sec. 300f et seq.; and
90          (ii) for a system that is required to treat its drinking water.
91          (c) The certification program shall be funded from certification and renewal fees.
92          (3) Routine extensions or repairs of existing public water systems that comply with the
93     rules and do not alter the system's ability to provide an adequate supply of water are exempt
94     from the provisions of Subsection (1)(c)(i).
95          (4) (a) The board may adopt and enforce standards and establish fees for certification
96     of persons engaged in administering cross connection control programs or backflow prevention
97     assembly training, repair, and maintenance testing.
98          (b) The certification program shall be funded from certification and renewal fees.
99          (5) A board member may not speak or act for the board unless the board member is
100     authorized by a majority of a quorum of the board in a vote taken at a meeting of the board.

103          (1) The Division of Water Rights shall, in accordance with Title 63G, Chapter 3, Utah
104     Administrative Rulemaking Act, make rules specifying:
105          (a) what water use data a person shall report, pursuant to this section; and
106          (b) how the Division of Water Rights shall validate the data described in Subsection
107     (1)(a).
108          (2) The Division of Water Rights, in conjunction with the Division of Water Resources
109     and the Division of Drinking Water, may collect and validate water use data:
110          (a) submitted by a public water supplier pursuant to a rule issued by the Drinking
111     Water Board, as described in Subsection 19-4-104(2)(c)(iv); and
112          (b) as described in this section.
113          (3) Every person using water from any river system or water source, when requested by
114     the state engineer, shall within 30 days after such request report to the state engineer in writing:
115          [(1)] (a) the nature of the use of any such water;
116          [(2)] (b) the area on which used;
117          [(3)] (c) the kind of crops to be grown; [and]
118          [(4)] (d) water elevations on wells or tunnels; and
119          (e) quantity of [underground] water used.
120          (4) A request made under provisions of this section has the same legal effect as a
121     written distribution order of the state engineer.
