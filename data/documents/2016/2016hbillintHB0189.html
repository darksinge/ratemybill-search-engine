7     LONG TITLE
8     General Description:
9          This bill amends provisions relating to towing and impoundment notifications.
10     Highlighted Provisions:
11          This bill:
12          ▸     amends the sentencing requirements for DUI convictions by requiring payment of
13     the administrative impound fee and towing and storage fees by the person
14     convicted;
15          ▸     requires the Motor Vehicle Division to provide notification to a dealer who has
16     issued a temporary permit for a car that is towed and impounded; and
17          ▸     makes technical changes.
18     Money Appropriated in this Bill:
19          None
20     Other Special Clauses:
21          None

81          (B) if the administrative impound fee was paid by a party described in Subsection

98     home confinement of not fewer than 1,500 hours through the use of electronic monitoring in
99     accordance with Section 41-6a-506.
100          (4) For Subsection (3)(a) or Subsection 41-6a-503(2)(b), the court shall impose an
101     order requiring the person to obtain a screening and assessment for alcohol and substance

105          (b) Probation or parole resulting from a conviction for a violation under this section

118          Section 2. Section 41-6a-1406 is amended to read:
119          41-6a-1406. Removal and impoundment of vehicles -- Reporting and notification
120     requirements -- Administrative impound fee -- Refunds -- Possessory lien -- Rulemaking.
121          (1) If a vehicle, vessel, or outboard motor is removed or impounded as provided under
122     Section 41-1a-1101, 41-6a-527, 41-6a-1405, 41-6a-1408, or 73-18-20.1 by an order of a peace
123     officer or by an order of a person acting on behalf of a law enforcement agency or highway
124     authority, the removal or impoundment of the vehicle, vessel, or outboard motor shall be at the
125     expense of the owner.
126          (2) The vehicle, vessel, or outboard motor under Subsection (1) shall be removed or
127     impounded to:
128          (a) a state impound yard; or
129          (b) if none, a garage, docking area, or other place of safety.
130          (3) The peace officer may move a vehicle, vessel, or outboard motor or cause it to be
131     removed by a tow truck motor carrier that meets standards established:
132          (a) under Title 72, Chapter 9, Motor Carrier Safety Act; and
133          (b) by the department under Subsection (10).
134          (4) (a) Immediately after the removal of the vehicle, vessel, or outboard motor, a report
135     of the removal shall be sent to the Motor Vehicle Division by:
136          (i) the peace officer or agency by whom the peace officer is employed; and
137          (ii) the tow truck operator or the tow truck motor carrier by whom the tow truck
138     operator is employed.
139          (b) The report shall be in a form specified by the Motor Vehicle Division and shall
140     include:
141          (i) the operator's name, if known;
142          (ii) a description of the vehicle, vessel, or outboard motor;
143          (iii) the vehicle identification number or vessel or outboard motor identification
144     number;
145          (iv) the license number or other identification number issued by a state agency;
146          (v) the date, time, and place of impoundment;
147          (vi) the reason for removal or impoundment;
148          (vii) the name of the tow truck motor carrier who removed the vehicle, vessel, or
149     outboard motor; and
150          (viii) the place where the vehicle, vessel, or outboard motor is stored.
151          (c) Until the tow truck operator or tow truck motor carrier reports the removal as
152     required under this Subsection (4), a tow truck motor carrier or impound yard may not:
153          (i) collect any fee associated with the removal; and
154          (ii) begin charging storage fees.
155          (5) (a) Except as provided in Subsection (5)(e) and upon receipt of the report, the
156     Motor Vehicle Division shall give notice [to], in the manner described in Section 41-1a-114, to
157     the following parties with an interest in the vehicle, vessel, or outboard motor, as applicable:
158          (i) the registered owner [of the vehicle, vessel, or outboard motor and];
159          (ii) any lien holder [in the manner prescribed by Section 41-1a-114.]; or
160          (iii) a dealer, as defined in Section 41-1a-102, if the vehicle, vessel, or outboard motor
161     is currently operating under a temporary permit issued by the dealer, as described in Section
162     41-3-302.
163          (b) The notice shall:
164          (i) state the date, time, and place of removal, the name, if applicable, of the person
165     operating the vehicle, vessel, or outboard motor at the time of removal, the reason for removal,
166     and the place where the vehicle, vessel, or outboard motor is stored;
167          (ii) state that the registered owner is responsible for payment of towing, impound, and
168     storage fees charged against the vehicle, vessel, or outboard motor;
169          (iii) [inform the registered owner of the vehicle, vessel, or outboard motor of] state the
170     conditions that must be satisfied before the vehicle, vessel, or outboard motor is released; and
171          (iv) inform the [registered owner and lienholder] parties described in Subsection (5)(a)
172     of the division's intent to sell the vehicle, vessel, or outboard motor, if, within 30 days [from
173     the date] after the day of the removal or impoundment under this section, [the owner, lien
174     holder, or the owner's agent] one of the parties fails to make a claim for release of the vehicle,
175     vessel, or outboard motor.
176          (c) Except as provided in Subsection (5)(e) and if the vehicle, vessel, or outboard
177     motor is not registered in this state, the Motor Vehicle Division shall make a reasonable effort
178     to notify the [registered owner and any lien holder] parties described in Subsection (5)(a) of the
179     removal and the place where the vehicle, vessel, or outboard motor is stored.
180          (d) The Motor Vehicle Division shall forward a copy of the notice to the place where
181     the vehicle, vessel, or outboard motor is stored.
182          (e) The Motor Vehicle Division is not required to give notice under this Subsection (5)
183     if a report was received by a tow truck operator or tow truck motor carrier reporting a tow truck
184     service in accordance with Subsection 72-9-603(1)(a)(i).
185          (6) (a) The vehicle, vessel, or outboard motor shall be released after [the registered
186     owner, lien holder, or the owner's agent] a party described in Subsection (5)(a):
187          (i) makes a claim for release of the vehicle, vessel, or outboard motor at any office of
188     the State Tax Commission;
189          (ii) presents identification sufficient to prove ownership of the impounded vehicle,
190     vessel, or outboard motor;
191          (iii) completes the registration, if needed, and pays the appropriate fees;
192          (iv) if the impoundment was made under Section 41-6a-527, pays an administrative
193     impound fee of $350; and
194          (v) pays all towing and storage fees to the place where the vehicle, vessel, or outboard
195     motor is stored.
196          (b) (i) Twenty-nine dollars of the administrative impound fee assessed under
197     Subsection (6)(a)(iv) shall be dedicated credits to the Motor Vehicle Division;
198          (ii) $97 of the administrative impound fee assessed under Subsection (6)(a)(iv) shall be
199     deposited in the Department of Public Safety Restricted Account created in Section 53-3-106;
200          (iii) $20 of the administrative impound fee assessed under Subsection (6)(a)(iv) shall
201     be deposited in the Traumatic Spinal Cord and Brain Injury Rehabilitation Fund; and
202          (iv) the remainder of the administrative impound fee assessed under Subsection
203     (6)(a)(iv) shall be deposited in the General Fund.
204          (c) The administrative impound fee assessed under Subsection (6)(a)(iv) shall be
205     waived or refunded by the State Tax Commission if the registered owner, lien holder, or
206     owner's agent presents written evidence to the State Tax Commission that:
207          (i) the Driver License Division determined that the arrested person's driver license
208     should not be suspended or revoked under Section 53-3-223 or 41-6a-521 as shown by a letter
209     or other report from the Driver License Division presented within 30 days of the final
210     notification from the Driver License Division; or
211          (ii) the vehicle was stolen at the time of the impoundment as shown by a copy of the
212     stolen vehicle report presented within 30 days of the impoundment.
213          (d) A tow truck operator, a tow truck motor carrier, and an impound yard shall accept
214     payment by cash and debit or credit card for a removal or impoundment under Subsection (1)
215     or any service rendered, performed, or supplied in connection with a removal or impoundment
216     under Subsection (1).
217          (e) The owner of an impounded vehicle may not be charged a fee for the storage of the
218     impounded vehicle, vessel, or outboard motor if:
219          (i) the vehicle, vessel, or outboard motor is being held as evidence; and
220          (ii) the vehicle, vessel, or outboard motor is not being released to [the registered owner,
221     lien holder, or the owner's agent] a party described in Subsection 5(a), even if the [registered
222     owner, lien holder, or the owner's agent] party satisfies the requirements to release the vehicle,
223     vessel, or outboard motor under this Subsection (6).
224          (7) (a) An impounded vehicle, vessel, or outboard motor not claimed by [the registered
225     owner or the owner's agent] a party described in Subsection (5)(a) within the time prescribed by
226     Section 41-1a-1103 shall be sold in accordance with that section and the proceeds, if any, shall
227     be disposed of as provided under Section 41-1a-1104.
228          (b) The date of impoundment is considered the date of seizure for computing the time
229     period provided under Section 41-1a-1103.
230          (8) [The registered owner who] A party described in Subsection (5)(a) that pays all fees
231     and charges incurred in the impoundment of the owner's vehicle, vessel, or outboard motor[,]
232     has a cause of action for all the fees and charges, together with damages, court costs, and
233     attorney fees, against the operator of the vehicle, vessel, or outboard motor whose actions
234     caused the removal or impoundment.
235          (9) Towing, impound fees, and storage fees are a possessory lien on the vehicle, vessel,
236     or outboard motor.
237          (10) In accordance with Title 63G, Chapter 3, Utah Administrative Rulemaking Act,
238     the department shall make rules setting the performance standards for towing companies to be
239     used by the department.
240          (11) (a) The Motor Vehicle Division may specify that a report required under
241     Subsection (4) be submitted in electronic form utilizing a database for submission, storage, and
242     retrieval of the information.
243          (b) (i) Unless otherwise provided by statute, the Motor Vehicle Division or the
244     administrator of the database may adopt a schedule of fees assessed for utilizing the database.
245          (ii) The fees under this Subsection (11)(b) shall:
246          (A) be reasonable and fair; and
247          (B) reflect the cost of administering the database.
248          Section 3. Section 72-9-603 is amended to read:
