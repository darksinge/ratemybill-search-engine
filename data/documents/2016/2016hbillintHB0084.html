7     LONG TITLE
8     General Description:
9           This bill clarifies the nature of a license, permit, tag, certificate of registration, point, or
10     credit issued to a person by the Division of Wildlife Resources.
11     Highlighted Provisions:
12          This bill:
13          ▸      states that a license, permit, tag, or certificate of registration issued to a person by
14     the Division of Wildlife Resources is a privilege, not a right;
15          ▸     states that a point or credit issued to a person to improve the person's chances of
16     receiving a hunting permit in a division-administered drawing:
17               •     may not be transferred, sold, or assigned to another person; and
18               •     is not a right; and
19          ▸     makes technical changes.
20     Money Appropriated in this Bill:
21          None
22     Other Special Clauses:
23          None
24     Utah Code Sections Affected:
25     AMENDS:
26          23-19-1, as last amended by Laws of Utah 2013, Chapter 418
27     

28     Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah:
29          Section 1. Section 23-19-1 is amended to read:
30          23-19-1. Possession of licenses, certificates of registration, permits, and tags
31     required -- Nonassignability -- Exceptions -- Free fishing day -- Nature of licenses,
32     permits, or tags issued by the division.
33          (1) Except as provided in Subsection (5), a person may not take, hunt, fish, or seine
34     protected wildlife or sell, trade, or barter protected wildlife or wildlife parts unless the person:
35          (a) procures the necessary licenses, certificates of registration, permits, or tags required
36     under this title, by rule made by the Wildlife Board under this title, or by an order or
37     proclamation issued in accordance with a rule made by the Wildlife Board under this title; and
38          (b) carries in the person's possession while engaging in the activities described in
39     Subsection (1) the license, certificate of registration, permit, or tag required under this title, by
40     rule made by the Wildlife Board under this title, or by an order or proclamation issued in
41     accordance with a rule made by the Wildlife Board under this title.
42          (2) Except as provided in Subsection (3) a person may not:
43          (a) lend, transfer, sell, give, or assign:
44          (i) a license, certificate of registration, permit, or tag belonging to the person; or
45          (ii) a right granted by a license, certificate of registration, permit, or tag; or
46          (b) use or attempt to use a license, certificate of registration, permit, or tag of another
47     person.
48          (3) In accordance with Title 63G, Chapter 3, Utah Administrative Rulemaking Act, the
49     Wildlife Board may, by rule, make exceptions to the prohibitions described in Subsection (2)
50     to:
51          (a) transport wildlife;
52          (b) allow a person to take protected wildlife for another person if:
53          (i) the person possessing the license, certificate of registration, permit, or tag has a
54     permanent physical impairment due to a congenital or acquired injury or disease; and
55          (ii) the injury or disease described in Subsection (3)(b)(i) results in the person having a
56     disability that renders the person physically unable to use a legal hunting weapon or fishing
57     device;
58          (c) allow a resident minor under 18 years of age to use the resident or nonresident
59     hunting permit of another person if:
60          (i) the resident minor is:
61          (A) the permit holder's child, stepchild, grandchild, or legal ward, if the permit holder's
62     guardianship of the legal ward is based solely on the minor's age; or
63          (B) suffering from a life threatening medical condition; and
64          (ii) the permit holder:
65          (A) receives no form of compensation or remuneration for allowing the minor to use
66     the permit;
67          (B) obtains the division's prior written approval to allow the minor to use the permit;
68     and
69          (C) accompanies the minor, for the purposes of advising and assisting during the hunt,
70     at a distance where the permit holder can communicate with the minor, in person, by voice or
71     visual signals; or
72          (d) subject to the requirements of Subsection (4), transfer to another person a certificate
73     of registration to harvest brine shrimp and brine shrimp eggs, if the certificate is transferred in
74     connection with the sale or transfer of the brine shrimp harvest operation or harvesting
75     equipment.
76          (4) A person may transfer a certificate of registration to harvest brine shrimp and brine
77     shrimp eggs if:
78          (a) the person submits to the division an application to transfer the certificate on a form
79     provided by the division;
80          (b) the proposed transferee meets all requirements necessary to obtain an original
81     certificate of registration; and
82          (c) the division approves the transfer of the certificate.
83          (5) A person is not required to obtain a license, certificate of registration, permit, or tag
84     to:
85          (a) fish on a free fishing day that the Wildlife Board may establish each year by rule
86     made by the Wildlife Board under this title or by an order or proclamation issued in accordance
87     with a rule made by the Wildlife Board under this title;
88          (b) fish at a private fish pond operated in accordance with Section 23-15-10; or
89          (c) hunt birds on a commercial hunting area that the owner or operator is authorized to
90     propagate, keep, and release for shooting in accordance with a certificate of registration issued
91     under Section 23-17-6.
92          (6) (a) A license, permit, tag, or certificate of registration issued under this title, or the
93     rules of the Wildlife Board issued pursuant to authority granted by this title, to take protected
94     wildlife is:
95          (i) a privilege; and
96          (ii) not a right or property for any purpose.
97          (b) A point or other form of credit issued to, or accumulated by, a person under
98     procedures established by the Wildlife Board in rule to improve the likelihood of obtaining a
99     hunting permit in a division-administered drawing:
100          (i) may not be transferred, sold, or assigned to another person; and
101          (ii) is not a right or property for any purpose.
